<p>The <b>Mazingira Green Party of Kenya</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenyan</a> <a href="/wiki/Green_party" title="Green party">green party</a>. It was formerly known as <b>Liberal Party of Kenya</b> (<b>LPK</b>).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
At the <a href="/wiki/Kenyan_general_election,_1997" title="Kenyan general election, 1997">1997 Kenyan General Elections</a> LPK fielded a presidential candidate, <a href="/wiki/Wangari_Maathai" title="Wangari Maathai">Wangari Maathai</a>, who later became a <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Peace_Prize" title="Nobel Peace Prize">Nobel Peace Prize</a> laureate. Maathai was only a minor candidate. She did not win a parliamentary seat.
In <a href="/wiki/Kenyan_general_election,_2002" title="Kenyan general election, 2002">2002, the next general elections</a> were held and the Maathai-led party was part of the victorious <a href="/wiki/National_Rainbow_Coalition" title="National Rainbow Coalition">NARC</a> coalition. Maathai herself won the <a href="/wiki/Tetu_Constituency" title="Tetu Constituency">Tetu Constituency</a> parliamentary seat.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
At the <a href="/wiki/Kenyan_general_election,_2007" title="Kenyan general election, 2007">Kenyan general election, 2007</a>, Mazingira was part of the newly created <a href="/wiki/Party_of_National_Unity_(Kenya)" title="Party of National Unity (Kenya)">Party of National Unity</a> led by President <a href="/wiki/Mwai_Kibaki" title="Mwai Kibaki">Mwai Kibaki</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> However, Mazingira also fielded own candidates. Mazingira won one parliamentary seat at the elections, after <a href="/wiki/Silas_Muriuki" title="Silas Muriuki">Silas Muriuki</a> beat PNU candidate <a href="/wiki/David_Mwiraria" title="David Mwiraria">David Mwiraria</a> to clinch the <a href="/wiki/North_Imenti_Constituency" title="North Imenti Constituency">North Imenti Constituency</a> parliamentary seat.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Maathai was first outvoted at the PNU primary elections and therefore vied on Mazingira ticket, but at the parliamentary elections lost again to the PNU candidate.
The word <i>Mazingira</i> is <a href="/wiki/Swahili_language" title="Swahili language">Swahili</a> for <i>environment</i>.
</p>
