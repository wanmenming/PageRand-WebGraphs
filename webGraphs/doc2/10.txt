<p>The <b>European <a href="/wiki/Green_Belt" class="mw-redirect" title="Green Belt">Green Belt</a></b> initiative is a grassroots movement for nature conservation and sustainable development along the corridor of the former <a href="/wiki/Iron_Curtain" title="Iron Curtain">Iron Curtain</a>. The term refers to both an environmental initiative as well as the area it concerns. The initiative is carried out under the patronage of the <a href="/wiki/International_Union_for_Conservation_of_Nature" title="International Union for Conservation of Nature">International Union for Conservation of Nature</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Gorbachev" title="Mikhail Gorbachev">Mikhail Gorbachev</a>. It is the aim of the initiative to create the backbone of an <a href="/wiki/Ecological_network" title="Ecological network">ecological network</a> that runs from the <a href="/wiki/Barents_Sea" title="Barents Sea">Barents</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adriatic_Sea" title="Adriatic Sea">Adriatic Seas</a>.
</p><p>The European Green Belt as an area follows the route of the former Iron Curtain and connects <a href="/wiki/National_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="National Park">National Parks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nature_park" title="Nature park">Nature Parks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biosphere_Reserve" class="mw-redirect" title="Biosphere Reserve">Biosphere Reserves</a> and transboundary protected areas as well as non-protected valuable habitats along or across the (former) <a href="/wiki/Border" title="Border">borders</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Riecken,_U._2007_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Riecken,_U._2007-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Green_Belt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1970, satellite pictures showed a dark green belt of old-growth forest on the Finnish-Russian border.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In the early 1980s, biologists discovered that the inner German border zone between <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a> in the west and <a href="/wiki/Thuringia" title="Thuringia">Thuringia</a> in the east was a refuge for several rare bird species that had disappeared from the intensely used areas covering most of <a href="/wiki/Central_Europe" title="Central Europe">Central Europe</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The reasoning behind this observation was that negative <a href="/wiki/Human_impact_on_the_environment" title="Human impact on the environment">human impact on the environment</a> is smaller in such border zones which are commonly closed to public access and thus <a href="/wiki/Involuntary_park" title="Involuntary park">wildlife is minimally impacted by human activities</a>.
</p><p>After the end of the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a> in the late 1980s, the <a href="/wiki/Border_barrier" title="Border barrier">strict border regimes</a> were abandoned and the border zones gradually opened, starting with the <a href="/wiki/German_reunification" title="German reunification">German reunification</a> in 1990 and continuing with the step-by-step integration of new member states into the <a href="/wiki/Schengen_Agreement" title="Schengen Agreement">Schengen Treaty</a> as part of the enlargement process of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>. At the same time, large military facilities such as training grounds and military research establishments in or close to the border zones were closed down. For most cases, it was unclear whom the property of these lands belonged to and thus what the fate of the valuable landscapes would be. Against this background, the conservation initiative Green Belt formed to conserve the natural assets along the former Iron Curtain.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Route">Route</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Green_Belt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Route">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The route of the Green Belt follows the course of the borders which during the second half of the 20th century divided the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Bloc" title="Eastern Bloc">eastern European communist countries</a> and the western capitalist countries. It is divided into three regional sections:
</p>
<ul><li>Fennoscandian Green Belt: <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a>.</li>
<li>Central European Green Belt: <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> (the <a href="/wiki/Inner_German_border" title="Inner German border">inner German border</a>), <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a></li>
<li>Balkan (or South Eastern European) Green Belt: <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Montenegro" title="Montenegro">Montenegro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kosovo" title="Kosovo">Kosovo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Macedonia" title="Republic of Macedonia">Republic of Macedonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Green_Belt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The historical starting point of the initiative was the Green Belt Resolution of Hof (Germany) in December 1989,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> one month after the fall of the <a href="/wiki/Berlin_Wall" title="Berlin Wall">Berlin Wall</a>. This document formulated and signed by more than 300 environmentalists from the <a href="/wiki/German_Democratic_Republic" class="mw-redirect" title="German Democratic Republic">German Democratic Republic</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Republic_of_Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="Federal Republic of Germany">Federal Republic of Germany</a> initiated the first conservation projects targeting the inner German border. After several achievements, the idea was taken to the European level. After a first conference on the European Green Belt in 2003, it was decided to establish a working group with the World Conservation Union (IUCN) as overall coordinator for its implementation; IUCN together with the Ferto-Hanság National Park in Hungary organized the first meeting of the working group, which took place 9–12 September 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In the following, the working group together with stakeholders of the Green Belt elaborated a Programme of Work and proposed representatives in each country along the Green Belt to be officially appointed as National Green Belt Focal Points by the respective Ministry of Environment. A <a href="/wiki/Memorandum_of_Understanding" class="mw-redirect" title="Memorandum of Understanding">Memorandum of Understanding</a> to jointly protect the Green Belt in <a href="/wiki/Fennoscandia" title="Fennoscandia">Fennoscandia</a> was signed by the Environmental Ministers of <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a> in 2010. In November 2010, the Binding Award for outstanding contributions to nature conservation was awarded to five individuals for their continuous engagement in protecting the Green Belt.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>For several years there have been considerations to nominate the European Green Belt as a <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" class="mw-redirect" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organisational_structure">Organisational structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Green_Belt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Organisational structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The initiative’s network consists of official representatives for the three regions named above (Regional Coordinators) and for each country (National Focal Points) appointed during the first European Green Belt meeting in 2003:<sup id="cite_ref-Riecken,_U._2007_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Riecken,_U._2007-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<ul><li>Fennoscandian Green Belt: Association of Reserves and National Parks of Russian North-West/ Baltic Fund for Nature</li>
<li>Central European Green Belt: <a href="/wiki/Bund_f%C3%BCr_Umwelt_und_Naturschutz_Deutschland" title="Bund für Umwelt und Naturschutz Deutschland">Bund Naturschutz Bayern</a>/ Friends of the Earth Germany</li>
<li>Balkan or South Eastern European Green Belt: Euronatur</li></ul>
<p>The implementation of the Green Belt vision in the regions is carried out by several hundred <a href="/wiki/Stakeholder_(corporate)" title="Stakeholder (corporate)">stakeholders</a> from nature conservation and sustainable development<sup id="cite_ref-Frobel,_K._2009_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Frobel,_K._2009-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> who contribute either on a project or voluntary basis.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecological_values">Ecological values</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Green_Belt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Ecological values">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Observations by biologists revealed that the military practice along the borderline led to wildlife conservation in numerous ways:<sup id="cite_ref-sky_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sky-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>A ban on pesticide spraying has preserved many rare insects.</li>
<li>Keeping the vegetation cut so border guards can see across easily, stopped the area from becoming continuous forest and thus preserved wildlife that needs open land.</li>
<li>One peculiar occurrence noticed was that in a forested part of this belt on the frontier between <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bohemia" title="Bohemia">Bohemia</a>, 18 years after the border barrier was removed, forest <a href="/wiki/Deer" title="Deer">deer</a> still refused to cross the frontier: compare <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/heft#English" class="extiw" title="wiktionary:heft">hefting</a> of livestock.</li>
<li>Old <a href="/wiki/Landmine" class="mw-redirect" title="Landmine">landmine</a> explosion craters have become wildlife ponds.</li>
<li>In the Bulgaria/Greece section there are many <a href="/wiki/Eastern_imperial_eagle" title="Eastern imperial eagle">eastern imperial eagle</a> nests.</li>
<li>Where the <a href="/wiki/River_Drava" class="mw-redirect" title="River Drava">River Drava</a> is the frontier between <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a>: mutual mistrust prevented river development works, so the river and its banks are still natural, including the river creating sand cliffs where <a href="/wiki/Sand_martin" title="Sand martin">sand martins</a> nest. The Drava has cut off <a href="/wiki/Meander" title="Meander">meanders</a>, leaving many bits of each nation's territory on the wrong side of the river; these areas are not farmed and have become wildlife areas.</li>
<li>Along the coast of the <a href="/wiki/Mecklenburg" title="Mecklenburg">Mecklenburg</a> area, restricted access to the coast, to stop people from crossing over by boat or swimming, helped to preserve coastal wildlife.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_values">Cultural values</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Green_Belt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Cultural values">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It has been proposed to develop not only the natural but also the cultural heritage of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_period" class="mw-redirect" title="Soviet period">Soviet period</a>: following the idea to link the numerous historical initiatives, installations, projects and relics in the Green Belt with the natural heritage,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> in order to turn the European Green Belt into living historical <a href="/wiki/Monument" title="Monument">monument</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a> during the 20th century.<sup id="cite_ref-Frobel,_K._2009_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Frobel,_K._2009-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In the context of the European Green Belt, cultural heritage has been assessed and/ or developed in several places already: 
</p>
<ul><li>On mount <a href="/wiki/Brocken" title="Brocken">Brocken</a>, Germany, the former border patrol path has been turned into a hiking route called “Harz border path”<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In the Slovenian Nature Park Goricko, border stones with information plates have been set up which inform visitors, about the history of the Iron Curtain and the natural values in place due to this history</li>
<li>Military heritage along the Latvian Green Belt has been assessed and compiled in a data base and map for visitors, including almost 100 stories of contemporary witnesses<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
