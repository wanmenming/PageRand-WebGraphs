<p>At right is displayed the <a href="/wiki/Color" title="Color">color</a> <b>forest green</b>.  
<i>Forest green</i> refers to a <a href="/wiki/Green" title="Green">green</a> color said to resemble the color of the <a href="/wiki/Tree" title="Tree">trees</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Plant" title="Plant">plants</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">forest</a>.
</p><p>This <a href="/wiki/Web_color" class="mw-redirect" title="Web color">web color</a>, when written as computer code in <a href="/wiki/HTML" title="HTML">HTML</a> for <a href="/wiki/Web_site" class="mw-redirect" title="Web site">web site</a> color display, is written in the form <b>forestgreen</b> (no space).<sup id="cite_ref-css3_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-css3-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The first recorded use of <i>forest green</i> as a color name in <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> was in 1810.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Forest_Green_(9674379239).jpg" class="image"><img alt="Ferns in a forest" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Forest_Green_%289674379239%29.jpg/220px-Forest_Green_%289674379239%29.jpg" width="220" height="139" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Forest_Green_%289674379239%29.jpg/330px-Forest_Green_%289674379239%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Forest_Green_%289674379239%29.jpg/440px-Forest_Green_%289674379239%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4608" data-file-height="2904" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Forest_Green_(9674379239).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Fern" title="Fern">Ferns</a> in a forest</div></div></div>
<p><i>Forest green</i> is a representation of the average color of the <a href="/wiki/Leaf" title="Leaf">leaves</a> of the trees of a <a href="/wiki/Temperate_zone" class="mw-redirect" title="Temperate zone">temperate zone</a> <a href="/wiki/Deciduous_forest" class="mw-redirect" title="Deciduous forest">deciduous forest</a>.
</p>
<div style="clear:both;"></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_culture">In culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Forest_green&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: In culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl><dt>Cartography</dt></dl>
<ul><li>Forest green is used to represent deciduous forest on <a href="/wiki/Map" title="Map">maps</a> depicting <i>natural vegetation</i>.</li></ul>
<dl><dt>Environmentalism</dt></dl>
<ul><li>Forest green may be used to represent the <a href="/wiki/Green_politics" title="Green politics">Green movement</a>, especially in <a href="/wiki/Graphic_design" title="Graphic design">graphic design</a> for environmental literature regarding issues having to do with <a href="/wiki/Forestry" title="Forestry">forest conservation</a>.</li>
<li>A <i>forest green environmentalist</i> (also called a <i><a href="/wiki/Dark_green_environmentalist" class="mw-redirect" title="Dark green environmentalist">dark green environmentalist</a></i>) is an environmentalist who is seriously committed to <a href="/wiki/Environmentalism" title="Environmentalism">environmentalism</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<dl><dt>School Colors</dt></dl>
<ul><li>Forest green is one of the school colors of <a href="/wiki/The_Evergreen_State_College" class="mw-redirect" title="The Evergreen State College">The Evergreen State College</a>, <a href="/wiki/Agincourt_Collegiate_Institute" title="Agincourt Collegiate Institute">Agincourt Collegiate Institute</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wagner_College" title="Wagner College">Wagner College</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cass_Technical_High_School" title="Cass Technical High School">Cass Technical High School</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Westminster_Schools" title="The Westminster Schools">The Westminster Schools</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newark_Arts_High_School" title="Newark Arts High School">Newark Arts High School (Newark, New Jersey)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canyon_Lake_High_School_(Texas)" class="mw-redirect" title="Canyon Lake High School (Texas)">Canyon Lake High School</a>, <a href="/wiki/St._Robert_Catholic_High_School" title="St. Robert Catholic High School">St Robert Catholic High School</a>, <a href="/wiki/Westlake_High_School_(Ohio)" title="Westlake High School (Ohio)">Westlake High School</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mesa_Verde_High_School" title="Mesa Verde High School">Mesa Verde High School (Citrus Heights, California)</a>.</li></ul>
<dl><dt>Scouting</dt></dl>
<ul><li>Forest green is used in the uniforms of the <a href="/wiki/Boy_Scouts_of_America" title="Boy Scouts of America">Boy Scouts of America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Venture_Scouts" class="mw-redirect" title="Venture Scouts">Venture Scouts</a>, and other Scouting organizations.</li></ul>
<dl><dt>Military</dt></dl>
<ul><li>Forest green is a frequent color used in woodland <a href="/wiki/Military_camouflage" title="Military camouflage">camouflage</a>. Beyond camo uniforms, soldiers will spray their weapons using a multitude of colors including forest green to what would otherwise be black or wooden material.</li></ul>
<dl><dt>Sports</dt></dl>
<ul><li>Forest green is one of the team colors of the <a href="/wiki/Nottingham_Hoods" title="Nottingham Hoods">Nottingham Hoods</a>, an English <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> team. It is also found on the team colors of the <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan_Roughriders" title="Saskatchewan Roughriders">Saskatchewan Roughriders</a>, as related to the flag of <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan" title="Saskatchewan">Saskatchewan</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Wild" title="Minnesota Wild">Minnesota Wild</a> of the <a href="/wiki/NHL" class="mw-redirect" title="NHL">NHL</a> has forest green as one of their team colors.</li></ul>
