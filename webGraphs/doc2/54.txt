<p>The Brazilian <b>Green Party</b> (<a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <i lang="pt">Partido Verde</i>, PV) was constituted after the <a href="/wiki/Military" title="Military">military</a> <a href="/wiki/Dictatorship" title="Dictatorship">dictatorship</a> period and, like other Green Parties around the world, is committed to establishing a set of policies on ensuring <a href="/w/index.php?title=Social-equity&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Social-equity (page does not exist)">social-equity</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> One of the party's founding members was the journalist and former anti-dictatorship revolutionary <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Gabeira" title="Fernando Gabeira">Fernando Gabeira</a> (a federal <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies" title="Chamber of Deputies">deputy</a> between 1995 and 2011), Alfredo Sirkis and Carlos Minc. 
The founding of the <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a> section of the Brazilian Green Party was led by a delegation from the southern Brazilian state of <a href="/wiki/Santa_Catarina_(state)" title="Santa Catarina (state)">Santa Catarina</a>, composed among others by Olga Maria Carvalho Luz, Luiz Henrique Gevaerd Odebrecht, Marcos Bayer, and Consuelo Luz Lins.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Platform">Platform</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_(Brazil)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Platform">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Among the main items on PV's <a href="/wiki/Political_agenda" title="Political agenda">agenda</a> are <a href="/wiki/Federalism" title="Federalism">federalism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Environmentalism" title="Environmentalism">environmentalism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Human_rights" title="Human rights">human rights</a>, a form of <a href="/wiki/Direct_democracy" title="Direct democracy">direct democracy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Parliamentarism" class="mw-redirect" title="Parliamentarism">parliamentarism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Social_welfare" class="mw-redirect" title="Social welfare">welfare</a>, <a href="/wiki/Civil_liberties" title="Civil liberties">civil liberties</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pacifism" title="Pacifism">pacifism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marijuana_legalization" class="mw-redirect" title="Marijuana legalization">marijuana legalization</a> under specific conditions.
</p><p>The party, however, argues to be in a position on the political spectrum that supposedly goes beyond the issue "<a href="/wiki/Left%E2%80%93right_political_spectrum" title="Left–right political spectrum">left-right</a>", considered by its members to be anachronistic and unrealistic. Many critics also believe that the party broke the limit not to be a small party set in the context of the "legends of rent" (used by political parties only to be elected). This image is rejected by one of the theoreticians of the party, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tibor_Rab%C3%B3czkay&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tibor Rabóczkay (page does not exist)">Tibor Rabóczkay</a>, in the book <i>Rethinking the Brazilian Green Party</i>, with the argument that the going round and round between legends is so common in the big parties, as in the small ones. The author, however, acknowledges that in the effort to achieve the 5% barrier imposed by the barrier clause ("law of exclusion policy" in the words of Rabóczkay), the green party has opened its doors to politicians who are not concerned with ecological issues and consequently, tend to be amorphous benches from the green.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_(Brazil)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Green Party was established in January 1986. It was founded by environmentalists and other activists from social movements, taking as their most expressive leaders <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Minc" title="Carlos Minc">Carlos Minc</a>, who soon returned to the <a href="/wiki/Worker%27s_Party_(Brazil)" class="mw-redirect" title="Worker&#39;s Party (Brazil)">PT</a>, Fernando Gabeira, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Alfredo_Sirkis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Alfredo Sirkis (page does not exist)">Alfredo Sirkis</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Domingos_Fernandes&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Domingos Fernandes (page does not exist)">Domingos Fernandes</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jose_Luiz_de_France_Penna&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jose Luiz de France Penna (page does not exist)">Jose Luiz de France Penna</a> and Mr <a href="/wiki/Sarney_Filho" title="Sarney Filho">Sarney Filho</a>.
</p><p>At the last <a href="/wiki/Legislative" class="mw-redirect" title="Legislative">legislative</a> <a href="/wiki/Elections_in_Brazil" title="Elections in Brazil">elections</a>, 3 October 2010, the party won 15 out of 513 seats in the Chamber of Deputies and no out of 81 seats in the Senate.
</p><p>After Lula's election as president of <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> in 2002, the Green Party was assigned the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Ministry of Culture">Ministry of Culture</a>, to which pop singer <a href="/wiki/Gilberto_Gil" title="Gilberto Gil">Gilberto Gil</a> was appointed.
</p><p>In the 2010 presidential election, the Green Party candidate <a href="/wiki/Marina_Silva" title="Marina Silva">Marina Silva</a> gained 19.3% of the vote, thus contributing to <a href="/wiki/Dilma_Rousseff" title="Dilma Rousseff">Dilma Rousseff</a>'s failure to gain 50% of the votes, making a run-off election necessary.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the 2014 presidential election PV candidated Eduardo Jorge, who obtained 0,61% of votes, and elected 6 Deputies and 1 senator. The party voted in favour of the <a href="/wiki/Impeachment_of_Dilma_Rousseff" title="Impeachment of Dilma Rousseff">impeachment of Dilma Roussef</a>. The party later went to support president Michel Temer.
</p><p>In May 2016 PV withdrew its support to Temer and went into <a href="/wiki/Crossbencher" title="Crossbencher">crossbench</a>.
</p><p>For the <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_general_election,_2018" title="Brazilian general election, 2018">Brazilian general election of 2018</a> the party formed with <a href="/wiki/Sustainability_Network" title="Sustainability Network">Sustainability Network</a> the coalition <i>United to transform Brazil</i>, in support of the candidacy of <a href="/wiki/Marina_Silva" title="Marina Silva">Marina Silva</a>. 
</p>
<h3><span id="Green_Party_in_S.C3.A3o_Paulo"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Green_Party_in_São_Paulo">Green Party in São Paulo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_(Brazil)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Green Party in São Paulo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The party is divided in the state in 21 river basins, where the capital are 4 basins – The Watershed are the regional governments of PV in the <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_(state)" title="São Paulo (state)">state of São Paulo</a>. They are bringing together the municipal executive of the party. The state chairman of the party, provisionally, is Belizário Marcos and the chairman of the municipal capital is Galeão Carlos Camacho.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Representation_in_government">Representation in government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_(Brazil)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Representation in government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The party is not a major political force in the country, unlike some of its similar Europeans (as the Green Party of Germany). For a long time its sole representative in Congress was the National Deputy Federal Fernando Gabeira, elected by <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro_(state)" title="Rio de Janeiro (state)">Rio de Janeiro</a> (1995–1998, 1999–2002; after brief period in <a href="/wiki/Workers_Party_(Brazil)" class="mw-redirect" title="Workers Party (Brazil)">Workers Party</a>, Gabeira returned to PV in 2005). During twenty-eight months, beginning in 2003, the party formed the basis of support for Lula's government, breaking up in the second half of May 2005, after stating general dissatisfaction with the environmental policies of the government. <a href="/wiki/Gilberto_Gil" title="Gilberto Gil">Gilberto Gil</a>, the former Minister of Culture in Lula's government is nonetheless a member of the party, and President of National PV is the Potiguar Jose Luiz de France Penna, which succeeded the former councillor Rio Alfredo Sirkis, a former Municipal Secretary of Environment and Municipal Secretary of Urbanism of Rio de Janeiro (also former candidate for President of the Republic by PV in 1998), in different administrations of Cesar Maia. Another ticket highlight is deputy Zequinha Sarney, of Maranhao, former Minister of the Environment in the government Fernando Henrique Cardoso. The party also differs from similar Europeans in low turnover in partisan positions and accumulation of power in the hands of relatively few people. In the meantime, it is equal to the other Brazilian political associations.
</p><p>After the aggregation of votes, on 3 October 2006, the Green Party reached 3.6% of valid votes, with 3,368,560 valid votes.
</p><p>In 2007, the National Convention was held in Brasília – DF, marked by legal challenges and complaints about the misuse of the Fund Partidário. Justice has come to stop the vote, guaranteed by point. Some critics of the administration of Penna in national PV were threatened with expulsion or expelled, as Paulo Moraes and Francis of Assisi (ex-pres. The PV / RJ). Complaints pass and the TSE evaluates suspend the fund of the party in 2008. Mr. Gabeira also speaks about reviewing the program's subtitle, which has since been widely criticised in the media.
</p>
