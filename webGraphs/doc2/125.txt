<p><b>European United Left–Nordic Green Left</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i lang="fr">Gauche unitaire européenne</i>, <b>GUE-NGL</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Left-wing_politics" title="Left-wing politics">left-wing</a> <a href="/wiki/Political_groups_of_the_European_Parliament" title="Political groups of the European Parliament">political group</a> in the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament" title="European Parliament">European Parliament</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Staab2011_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Staab2011-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> established in 1995. The group comprises political parties of <a href="/wiki/Socialism" title="Socialism">socialist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Communism" title="Communism">communist</a> orientation.<sup id="cite_ref-Trechsel2013_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Trechsel2013-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CasierJongerden2010_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CasierJongerden2010-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Position">Position</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=European_United_Left%E2%80%93Nordic_Green_Left&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Position">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to its 1994 constituent declaration, the group is opposed to the present <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> political structure, but committed to integration.<sup id="cite_ref-EULNGLConDec_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EULNGLConDec-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> That declaration sets out three aims for the construction of another European Union: the total change of institutions to make them "fully democratic"; breaking with "neo-liberal monetarist policies"; and a policy of co-development and equitable cooperation. The group wants to disband the <a href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_Treaty_Organisation" class="mw-redirect" title="North Atlantic Treaty Organisation">North Atlantic Treaty Organisation</a> (NATO) and "strengthen the <a href="/wiki/Organization_for_Security_and_Co-operation_in_Europe" title="Organization for Security and Co-operation in Europe">Organization for Security and Co-operation in Europe</a>" (OSCE).
</p><p>The group is ambiguous between reformism and revolution, leaving it up to each party to decide on the manner they deem best suited to achieve these aims. As such, it has simultaneously positioned itself as "insiders" within the European institutions, enabling it to influence the decisions made by co-decision, and as "outsiders" by its willingness to seek "another Union" which would abolish the <a href="/wiki/Maastricht_Treaty" title="Maastricht Treaty">Maastricht Treaty</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organisation">Organisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=European_United_Left%E2%80%93Nordic_Green_Left&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Organisation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The GUE/NGL is a confederal group: It is composed of <a href="/wiki/Member_of_the_European_Parliament" title="Member of the European Parliament">MEPs</a> from national parties. Those national parties must share common political objectives with the group, as specified in the group's constituent declaration. Nevertheless, those national parties, not the group, retain control of their MEPs. Thus, the Group may be divided on certain issues.
</p><p>Members of the group meet regularly to prepare for meetings, debate on policies and vote on resolutions. The group also publishes reports on various topics.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Member_parties">Member parties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=European_United_Left%E2%80%93Nordic_Green_Left&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Member parties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:GUE_NGL_after_2014_elections.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dc/GUE_NGL_after_2014_elections.png/250px-GUE_NGL_after_2014_elections.png" width="250" height="267" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dc/GUE_NGL_after_2014_elections.png/375px-GUE_NGL_after_2014_elections.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dc/GUE_NGL_after_2014_elections.png/500px-GUE_NGL_after_2014_elections.png 2x" data-file-width="560" data-file-height="599" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:GUE_NGL_after_2014_elections.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Member states with one MEP in GUE/NGL in pink, with two or more members in red</div></div></div>
<p>MEPs may be full or associate members.
</p>
<ul><li>Full members must accept the constitutional declaration of the Group.</li>
<li>Associate members need not fully do so but may sit with the full members.</li></ul>
<p>National parties may be full or associate members.
</p>
<ul><li>Full member parties must accept the constitutional declaration of the Group.</li>
<li>Associate member parties may include parties that do not have MEPs (e. g., French Trotskyist parties which did not get elected in the 2004 European elections), are from states that are not part of the European Union, or do not wish to be full members.</li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=European_United_Left%E2%80%93Nordic_Green_Left&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1995, the enlargement of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> led to the creation of the <a href="/wiki/Nordic_Green_Left_Alliance" title="Nordic Green Left Alliance">Nordic Green Left</a> group of parties. The Nordic Green Left merged with the <a href="/wiki/European_United_Left_(1994%E2%80%9395)" title="European United Left (1994–95)">Confederal Group of the European United Left</a> (GUE) on 6 January 1995,<sup id="cite_ref-t1s4_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-t1s4-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> forming the Confederal Group of the European United Left/Nordic Green Left.<sup id="cite_ref-t1s5_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-t1s5-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-t1s6_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-t1s6-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-t1s40_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-t1s40-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The NGL suffix was added to the name of the expanded group on insistence of Swedish and Finnish MEPs.<sup id="cite_ref-RaunioTiilikainen2013_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RaunioTiilikainen2013-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The group initially consisted of MEPs from the Finnish <a href="/wiki/Left_Alliance_(Finland)" title="Left Alliance (Finland)">Left Alliance</a>, Swedish <a href="/wiki/Left_Party_(Sweden)" title="Left Party (Sweden)">Left Party</a>, the Danish <a href="/wiki/Socialist_People%27s_Party_(Denmark)" title="Socialist People&#39;s Party (Denmark)">Socialist People's Party</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Left_(Spain)" title="United Left (Spain)">United Left of Spain</a> (including the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Spain_(main)" class="mw-redirect" title="Communist Party of Spain (main)">Spanish Communist Party</a>), <a href="/wiki/Coalition_of_Left,_of_Movements_and_Ecology" class="mw-redirect" title="Coalition of Left, of Movements and Ecology">Synaspismós</a> of Greece, the <a href="/wiki/French_Communist_Party" title="French Communist Party">French Communist Party</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Communist_Party" title="Portuguese Communist Party">Portuguese Communist Party</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Greece" title="Communist Party of Greece">Communist Party of Greece</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Refoundation_Party" title="Communist Refoundation Party">Communist Refoundation Party</a> of Italy.
</p><p>In 1999, the German <a href="/wiki/Party_of_Democratic_Socialism_(Germany)" title="Party of Democratic Socialism (Germany)">Party of Democratic Socialism</a> (PDS) and the Greek <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Social_Movement" title="Democratic Social Movement">Democratic Social Movement</a> (DIKKI) joined as full members, while the five MEPs elected from the list of the French Trotskyist alliance <a href="/wiki/Workers%27_Struggle" class="mw-redirect" title="Workers&#39; Struggle">LO</a>-<a href="/wiki/Revolutionary_Communist_League_(France)" title="Revolutionary Communist League (France)">LCR</a> joined as associate members.
</p><p>In 2002, four MEPs from the French <a href="/wiki/Citizen_and_Republican_Movement" title="Citizen and Republican Movement">Citizen and Republican Movement</a> also joined the group.
</p><p>In 2004, no MEPs were elected from LO-LCR, and DIKKI—which was undergoing a dispute with its leader over the party constitution—did not put forward candidates. MEPs from the Portuguese <a href="/wiki/Left_Bloc" title="Left Bloc">Left Bloc</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sinn_F%C3%A9in" title="Sinn Féin">Sinn Féin</a> both from the Republic of Ireland and Northern Ireland, the <a href="/wiki/Progressive_Party_of_Working_People" title="Progressive Party of Working People">Progressive Party of Working People</a> (AKEL) of Cyprus, and the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Bohemia_and_Moravia" title="Communist Party of Bohemia and Moravia">Communist Party of Bohemia and Moravia</a> joined the group.
</p>
