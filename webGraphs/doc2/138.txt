<h2><span id="10th_Bundestag_.281983.E2.80.931987.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="10th_Bundestag_(1983–1987)">10th Bundestag (1983–1987)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_faction_(Bundestag)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: 10th Bundestag (1983–1987)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Scoring 5.6% in the federal parliamentary elections in 1983, the <a href="/wiki/German_Green_Party" class="mw-redirect" title="German Green Party">Greens</a> entered the <a href="/wiki/Bundestag" title="Bundestag">Bundestag</a> for the first time with a total of 28 seats. Faction members were rotated after two years in 1985 (with the exception of <a href="/wiki/Petra_Kelly" title="Petra Kelly">Petra Kelly</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gert_Bastian" title="Gert Bastian">Gert Bastian</a>), but the concept was abolished in May 1986 again. The executive board, elected by faction members on April 3, 1984, consisted of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Annemarie_Borgmann&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Annemarie Borgmann (page does not exist)">Annemarie Borgmann</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Waltraud_Schoppe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Waltraud Schoppe (page does not exist)">Waltraud Schoppe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antje_Vollmer" title="Antje Vollmer">Antje Vollmer</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Christa_Nickels&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Christa Nickels (page does not exist)">Christa Nickels</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Heidemarie_Dann&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Heidemarie Dann (page does not exist)">Heidemarie Dann</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Erika_Hickel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Erika Hickel (page does not exist)">Erika Hickel</a>.

<h2><span id="11th_Bundestag_.281987.E2.80.931990.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="11th_Bundestag_(1987–1990)">11th Bundestag (1987–1990)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_faction_(Bundestag)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 11th Bundestag (1987–1990)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the 1987 parliamentary elections, the <a href="/wiki/German_Green_Party" class="mw-redirect" title="German Green Party">Green Party</a> managed to increase its share of votes to 8.3%, gaining 44 parliament seats in the process. When the <a href="/wiki/East_Germany" title="East Germany">East German</a> parliament, the <i>Volkskammer</i>, which was freely elected on March 18, 1990 for the first time, was disbanded in the process of the <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German reunification</a>, another 7 seats were added as 7 members of the 21-member <i>Volkskammer</i> faction of the Green Party, elected by their peers, entered the <a href="/wiki/Bundestag" title="Bundestag">Bundestag</a>.

<h2><span id="12th_Bundestag_.281990.E2.80.931994.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="12th_Bundestag_(1990–1994)">12th Bundestag (1990–1994)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_faction_(Bundestag)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 12th Bundestag (1990–1994)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1990, elections were held separately in former <a href="/wiki/East_Germany" title="East Germany">East</a> and <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a>; in West Germany, the <a href="/wiki/German_Green_Party" class="mw-redirect" title="German Green Party">Green Party</a> did not manage to gain enough votes to enter parliament, only scoring 4.8% instead of the necessary 5%, but in East Germany, the Greens gained a 6.1% share of the votes and 8 seats in the <a href="/wiki/Bundestag" title="Bundestag">Bundestag</a>. While a green presence in the 12th Bundestag was thus secured, the Greens could not form a faction, instead remaining a "group" (with less rights and a smaller budget).

</p>
<ul><li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Klaus-Dieter_Feige&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Klaus-Dieter Feige (page does not exist)">Klaus-Dieter Feige</a></li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ingrid_K%C3%B6ppe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ingrid Köppe (page does not exist)">Ingrid Köppe</a></li>

<h2><span id="14th_Bundestag_.281998.E2.80.932002.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="14th_Bundestag_(1998–2002)">14th Bundestag (1998–2002)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_faction_(Bundestag)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 14th Bundestag (1998–2002)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h2><span id="16th_Bundestag_.282005.E2.80.932009.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="16th_Bundestag_(2005–2009)">16th Bundestag (2005–2009)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_faction_(Bundestag)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 16th Bundestag (2005–2009)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The ruling <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_Party_of_Germany" title="Social Democratic Party of Germany">SPD</a>/<a href="/wiki/German_Green_Party" class="mw-redirect" title="German Green Party">Green Party</a> suffered losses during the 2005 parliamentary elections leading to a Grand Coalition between the <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_Party_of_Germany" title="Social Democratic Party of Germany">SPD</a> and the CDU/CSU. The <a href="/wiki/German_Green_Party" class="mw-redirect" title="German Green Party">Green Party</a> lost four seats to go from 55 to 51 and went into opposition with the <a href="/wiki/Freie_Demokratische_Partei" class="mw-redirect" title="Freie Demokratische Partei">FDP</a> and <a href="/wiki/Left_Party_(Germany)" class="mw-redirect" title="Left Party (Germany)">the Left Party</a>.
