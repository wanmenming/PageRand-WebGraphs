<p><b>The Green Ecologist Party</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Partido Ecologista Verde</i>, GEP) is a <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chilean</a> political party and one of <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>'s members of the global green movement.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Ecologist_Party_(Chile)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is the first Chilean ecologist political party legalized after the disappearance of <a href="/wiki/The_Greens_(Chile)" title="The Greens (Chile)">The Greens</a> (1987-2001) and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmentalist_Movement&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Environmentalist Movement (page does not exist)">Environmentalist Movement</a> (1993-1994), the first officially affiliated with the <a href="/wiki/Global_Greens" title="Global Greens">Global Greens</a>.
</p><p>It participated in the <a href="/wiki/Chilean_municipal_election,_2008" title="Chilean municipal election, 2008">municipal elections of 2008</a> in an alliance («For a clean Chile») with the <a href="/wiki/Independent_Regionalist_Party" title="Independent Regionalist Party">Independent Regionalist Party</a> (PRI) and <a href="/wiki/ChileFirst" class="mw-redirect" title="ChileFirst">ChileFirst</a> (CH1) movement. It managed to choose 1 councilor as an independent in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/San_Pedro_de_La_Paz" class="mw-redirect" title="San Pedro de La Paz">San Pedro de La Paz</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/Chilean_general_election,_2009" class="mw-redirect" title="Chilean general election, 2009">parliamentary elections the following year</a> it formed the <a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Majority_for_Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="New Majority for Chile (page does not exist)">New Majority for Chile</a> pact with the <a href="/wiki/Humanist_Party_(Chile)" title="Humanist Party (Chile)">Humanist Party</a> and supported the presidential candidacy of <a href="/wiki/Marco_Enr%C3%ADquez-Ominami" title="Marco Enríquez-Ominami">Marco Enríquez-Ominami</a>. In the municipal elections of 2012 it joined Change for You in conjunction with the <a href="/wiki/Progressive_Party_(Chile)" title="Progressive Party (Chile)">Progressive Party</a>.
</p><p>For the presidential election of 2013, and after carrying out a process of internal primary among its members, proclaimed as presidential candidate to the economist <a href="/wiki/Alfredo_Sfeir" title="Alfredo Sfeir">Alfredo Sfeir</a>, who obtained 2.35% of the vote.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In parliamentary and regional councilors elections that same year participated in coalition with the Equality Party by New Constitution for Chile pact.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> No deputies or senators won, but won the election of Felix Gonzalez as regional councilor of the Province of Concepción.
</p><p>Before the elections of 2013, the community was legalized by the name of Green Ecologist Party of North in three regions of northern Chile.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Both the Green Ecologist Party North as the Green Party itself did not reach 5% of the votes, and for that reason they were dissolved by the Electoral Service.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Given this, both parties decided to merge, in a process that was completed on April 15, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
