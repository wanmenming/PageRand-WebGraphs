<p><b>Hariyali Nepal Party</b> (<b>Green Nepal Party</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political party</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The party is registered with the <a href="/wiki/Election_Commission_of_Nepal" class="mw-redirect" title="Election Commission of Nepal">Election Commission of Nepal</a> ahead of the <a href="/wiki/Nepalese_Constituent_Assembly_election,_2008" title="Nepalese Constituent Assembly election, 2008">2008 Constituent Assembly election</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It was established in 1997 and is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Global_Greens" title="Global Greens">Global Greens</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Asia-Pacific_Green_Network" class="mw-redirect" title="Asia-Pacific Green Network">Asia-Pacific Green Network</a>.
</p><p><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kuber_Sharma_(Nepal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kuber Sharma (Nepal) (page does not exist)">Kuber Sharma</a>, the party's founding president, briefly held the post of <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a>'s Minister for Culture and Civil Aviation in November 2004. Another notable founding member is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Maita_Lal_Gurung&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Maita Lal Gurung (page does not exist)">Maita Lal Gurung</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hariyali_Nepal_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The party was, according to its chairman Sharma, founded by persons previously associated with various political ideals.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The party got 6638 votes in the <a href="/wiki/Nepalese_legislative_election,_1999" title="Nepalese legislative election, 1999">1999 legislative election</a>, but no seat.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The chairman of the party, Kuber Sharma, was named Culture, Tourism and Civil Aviation minister by <a href="/wiki/King_Gyanendra" class="mw-redirect" title="King Gyanendra">King Gyanendra</a> in 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
