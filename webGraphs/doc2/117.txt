<p>The <b>Green Party of Albania</b> (<a href="/wiki/Albanian_language" title="Albanian language">Albanian</a>: <i lang="sq">Partia e Gjelbër or Të gjelbrit</i>, PGJ), is a political party in <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a> that follows the traditions of <a href="/wiki/Green_politics" title="Green politics">Green politics</a> and maintains a strong commitment to social progressivism. Albanian Green Party was created on December 15, 2007, and is a full member of <a href="/wiki/Global_Greens" title="Global Greens">Global Greens</a> and <a href="/wiki/European_Green_Party" title="European Green Party">European Green Party</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_of_Albania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Green Party of Albania was created to represent the environmental concerns of Albanians. The importance and urgency of solving numerous environmental problems in Albania prompted a group of young intellectuals from different fields to initiate the beginnings of The Green Party of Albania. Founded on 1st of September 2001, as "The Greens of Albania" the official name changed to "Green Party of Albania" on December 15, 2007.
</p><p>On October 13, 2008, The Green Party of Albania became a full member of the <a href="/wiki/European_Green_Party" title="European Green Party">European Green Party</a>. This gives it voting rights in the General Council of the European Green Party.
</p><p>Under the motto "To protect the environment, vote Greens", the party participated for the first time in <a href="/wiki/Albanian_local_elections,_2003" title="Albanian local elections, 2003">Albanian local elections, 2003</a> winning local councillors. This win began Green representation in local decision-making structures. Since that time, the Green Party has regularly participated in local and parliamentary elections. In the <a href="/wiki/Albanian_local_elections,_2011" title="Albanian local elections, 2011">Albanian local elections, 2011</a>, the Green Party won 8,448 votes - 20 times more than in previous elections.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Positions">Positions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_of_Albania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Positions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since its inception, the party has taken several concrete steps to sensitize the Albanian public towards human impact on climate change and the environment. "The Greens" developed a political platform that provides environmentally conscious alternatives to current ways of life. There are now green movements across all 12 districts of the country.
</p><p>Protests occupy an important place in the political activity of the Greens. Through these protests, the Greens provide a public reaction to environmental degradation and pollution as a means of sensitizing the public to the ongoing issues. In addition to protests, The Green Party has undertaken a series of concrete actions for public awareness, such as seminars, workshops, environmental conferences, distribution of posters, leaflets, territory cleaning activities, and waste reduction. The party's activities have found wide support throughout different segments of the population.
</p>
