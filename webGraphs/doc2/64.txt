<p>The <b>Green Party of Alberta</b> is a registered political party in <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, that is allied with the <a href="/wiki/Green_Party_of_Canada" title="Green Party of Canada">Green Party of Canada</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-May_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-May-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and the other provincial Green parties. The party was registered by <a href="/wiki/Elections_Alberta" title="Elections Alberta">Elections Alberta</a> on December 22, 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-CH_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CH-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-EA_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EA-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> to replace the deregistered <a href="/wiki/Alberta_Greens" title="Alberta Greens">Alberta Greens</a>, and ran its first candidates for office in the <a href="/wiki/Alberta_general_election,_2012" title="Alberta general election, 2012">2012 provincial election</a> under the name <b>Evergreen Party of Alberta</b>.  The party changed its name to "Green Party of Alberta" on November 1, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-ELECTIONSALBERTA_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ELECTIONSALBERTA-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_of_Alberta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Alberta_Greens" title="Alberta Greens">Alberta Greens</a></div>
<p>Following a dispute of the leadership of the <a href="/wiki/Alberta_Greens" title="Alberta Greens">Alberta Greens</a> in 2008, <a href="/wiki/George_Read_(Alberta_politician)" title="George Read (Alberta politician)">George Read</a> withdrew as leader and <a href="/wiki/Joe_Anglin" title="Joe Anglin">Joe Anglin</a> remained as interim leader. On April 1, 2009, the executive of the party failed to file an annual financial statement with <a href="/wiki/Elections_Alberta" title="Elections Alberta">Elections Alberta</a>, as required by law, and was deregistered on July 16, 2009. Some of its members joined the <a href="/wiki/Alberta_Party" title="Alberta Party">Alberta Party</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wildrose_Party" title="Wildrose Party">Wildrose Party</a>, while others formed the Vision 2012 Society.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The independent group, dedicated to green principles, formed the legal entity required by Elections Alberta to register a political party. An annual general meeting was held on June 25, 2011 in <a href="/wiki/Red_Deer,_Alberta" title="Red Deer, Alberta">Red Deer</a> to elect an executive, and to raise a petition asking Elections Alberta to register a new party.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The petition was signed by 8,500 people, more than the required 7,000,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and on December 22, 2011, the "Evergreen Party of Alberta" was registered. According to Elections Alberta rules, a party cannot use a name used by another party until the name goes unused for a general election.<sup id="cite_ref-Western_Wheel_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Western_Wheel-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> After contesting the 2012 general election under the Evergreen banner, the party voted at its annual general meeting, on September 29, 2012, to change its name to "Green Party of Alberta".<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>  Elections Alberta approved the name change and it became effective 1 November 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-ELECTIONSALBERTA_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ELECTIONSALBERTA-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Platform">Platform</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_of_Alberta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Platform">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The party bases its principles on the Charter of the <a href="/wiki/Global_Greens" title="Global Greens">Global Greens</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The Green Party believes in humans' place in the world and sustainability, democracy, justice and diversity, and <a href="/wiki/Nonviolence" title="Nonviolence">nonviolence</a>.
</p>
