<p>The <b>Green Eto-Ecologist Party</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es"><b>Partido Verde Eto-Ecologista</b></i>) was a <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">Uruguayan</a> <a href="/wiki/Green_party" title="Green party">green party</a>.
</p><p>Established in 1987, it obtained 11,000 votes in the <a href="/wiki/1989_Uruguayan_general_election" class="mw-redirect" title="1989 Uruguayan general election">1989 Uruguayan general election</a>, with Dr. <a href="/wiki/Rodolfo_T%C3%A1lice" title="Rodolfo Tálice">Rodolfo Tálice</a> as its presidential candidate.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Uruguayan_general_election,_1994" title="Uruguayan general election, 1994">Five years later</a> it obtained 5,000 votes. Afterwards the party almost lost presence, associating itself with the Civic Union.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
