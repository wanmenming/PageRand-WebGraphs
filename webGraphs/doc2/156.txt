<p>The <b>Hungarian Social Green Party</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">Hungarian</a>: <i lang="hu">Magyar Szociális Zöld Párt</i>; <b>MSZZP</b>), also known its shortened form <b>Green Party</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Green_anarchism" title="Green anarchism">green anarchist</a> political party in <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>, founded in December 1995.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hungarian_Social_Green_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 22 December 1995, Ottó Stekler (husband of fellow politician <a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%A1ria_Seres&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mária Seres (page does not exist)">Mária Seres</a>) and his followers left the <a href="/wiki/Green_Party_of_Hungary" title="Green Party of Hungary">Green Party of Hungary</a> (MZP) to form the MSZZP in <a href="/wiki/Ny%C3%ADregyh%C3%A1za" title="Nyíregyháza">Nyíregyháza</a>. During the foundation, the party described itself as an "anti-party", with several green anarchist and anti-establishment elements in its programme. In the <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_parliamentary_election,_1998" title="Hungarian parliamentary election, 1998">1998 parliamentary election</a>, the MSZZP was able to set up a regional list in <a href="/wiki/Szabolcs-Szatm%C3%A1r-Bereg_County" title="Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg County">Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg County</a> and obtained the 0.05 percent of votes, thus won no seats.<sup id="cite_ref-NS_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NS-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The party skipped the <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_parliamentary_election,_2002" title="Hungarian parliamentary election, 2002">2002 parliamentary election</a>.
</p><p>By the <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_parliamentary_election,_2006" title="Hungarian parliamentary election, 2006">2006 parliamentary election</a>, the MSZZP became known of its guerrilla campaign methods and media PR campaigns and performances, having entered into cooperation with infamous far-right blogger <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tam%C3%A1s_Polg%C3%A1r&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tamás Polgár (page does not exist)">Tamás Polgár</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;(<a href="https://hu.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polg%C3%A1r_Tam%C3%A1s_(aktivista)" class="extiw" title="hu:Polgár Tamás (aktivista)">hu</a>)</span> (<i>Tomcat</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEVida2011418_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEVida2011418-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> For instance, young activists of the party tried to attack <a href="/wiki/Fidesz" title="Fidesz">Fidesz</a> politician <a href="/wiki/Zolt%C3%A1n_Pokorni" title="Zoltán Pokorni">Zoltán Pokorni</a> with pillows during a campaign event in <a href="/wiki/P%C3%BCsp%C3%B6klad%C3%A1ny" title="Püspökladány">Püspökladány</a>, but the event organizers have prevented this. Previously the party activists also tried to disturb the campaign speech of <a href="/wiki/G%C3%A1bor_Demszky" title="Gábor Demszky">Gábor Demszky</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Mayor_of_Budapest" title="Mayor of Budapest">Mayor of Budapest</a>, by throwing a cake at his face. The MSZZP also released a video record which claimed  system-wide counterfeiting of proposal coupons took place at the <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Socialist_Party" title="Hungarian Socialist Party">Hungarian Socialist Party</a> (MSZP) headquarters. Protesting against the electoral system, MSZZP activists burned their proposal coupons in front of the <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Parliament_Building" title="Hungarian Parliament Building">Hungarian Parliament Building</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEVida2011418_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEVida2011418-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The MSZZP intended to run in the February 2009 Ferencváros parliamentary by-election and nominated Tamás Polgár as their candidate. However the Metropolitan Court rejected Polgár's entrance because 1152 proposal coupons out of 1165 pieces proved to be invalid.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
