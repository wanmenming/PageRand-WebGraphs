<p><b>Green Left Party</b> (<a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>: <i lang="tr">Yeşil Sol Parti</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Left-libertarian" class="mw-redirect" title="Left-libertarian">left-libertarian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Green_politics" title="Green politics">green</a> party in <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>. It was founded on 25 November 2012 with the name <b>Greens and the Left Party of the Future</b> (<a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>: <i lang="tr">Yeşiller ve Sol Gelecek Partisi</i>) as a merger of the <a href="/wiki/Greens_Party_(Turkey)" title="Greens Party (Turkey)">Greens Party</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Equality_and_Democracy_Party" title="Equality and Democracy Party">Equality and Democracy Party</a>. The party changed its name in April 2016.
</p><p>Prominent members include <a href="/wiki/Murat_Belge" title="Murat Belge">Murat Belge</a>, left-liberal political author and columnist for <i><a href="/wiki/Taraf" title="Taraf">Taraf</a></i>; <a href="/wiki/Kutlu%C4%9F_Ataman" title="Kutluğ Ataman">Kutluğ Ataman</a>, filmmaker and contemporary artist; and <a href="/wiki/Ufuk_Uras" title="Ufuk Uras">Ufuk Uras</a>, former <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a> deputy and president of the <a href="/wiki/Freedom_and_Solidarity_Party" title="Freedom and Solidarity Party">Freedom and Solidarity Party</a>.
</p><p>The party is one of the participants in the <a href="/wiki/Peoples%27_Democratic_Congress" title="Peoples&#39; Democratic Congress">Peoples' Democratic Congress</a>, a political initiative instrumental in founding the <a href="/wiki/Peoples%27_Democratic_Party_(Turkey)" title="Peoples&#39; Democratic Party (Turkey)">Peoples' Democratic Party</a> in 2012.
</p><p>The party has formally acknowledged the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Genocide" title="Armenian Genocide">Armenian Genocide</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
