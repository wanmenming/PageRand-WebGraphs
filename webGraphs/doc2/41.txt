<p>The <b>Mongolian Green Party</b> (Mongolyn Nogoon Nam) was founded in 1990 when <a href="/wiki/Mongolia" title="Mongolia">Mongolia</a> became a <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democracy</a>, becoming the first Green party founded in Asia. From 1996 until 2000 the party was in the governing coalition with the <a href="/wiki/Mongolian_Social_Democratic_Party" title="Mongolian Social Democratic Party">Mongolian Social Democratic Party</a>, <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(Mongolia)" title="Democratic Party (Mongolia)">Democratic Party</a>, and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mongolian_Democratic_Religion_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mongolian Democratic Religion Party (page does not exist)">Mongolian Democratic Religion Party</a>.
</p><p>The party has around 6,000 members and has worked towards improving <a href="/wiki/Human_rights" title="Human rights">human rights</a> and democracy in Mongolia and protecting the environment.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Since 2012 Mongolian Green Party has a new leader: O.Boum-Yalagch.
</p>
