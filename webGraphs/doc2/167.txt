<p>The <b>Green Party of Vancouver</b>, founded in 1984,<sup id="cite_ref-About_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-About-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> is a municipal <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political party</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>. It is affiliated with both the provincial <a href="/wiki/Green_Party_of_British_Columbia" title="Green Party of British Columbia">Green Party of British Columbia</a>, and the national <a href="/wiki/Green_Party_of_Canada" title="Green Party of Canada">Green Party of Canada</a>.
</p><p>Roslyn Cassels was the first elected Green in Canada and was elected to the Vancouver Park Board in the <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_municipal_election,_1999" title="Vancouver municipal election, 1999">1999 Vancouver municipal election</a>. In 2005, <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Reimer" title="Andrea Reimer">Andrea Reimer</a> was elected to the <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_School_Board" title="Vancouver School Board">Vancouver School Board</a> as a Trustee, and in 2008 Stuart Mackinnon was elected a Park Board Commissioner.
</p><p>The party nominated <a href="/wiki/Green_Party_of_Canada" title="Green Party of Canada">Green Party of Canada</a> deputy leader <a href="/wiki/Adriane_Carr" title="Adriane Carr">Adriane Carr</a> as their sole nominee for <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_City_Council" title="Vancouver City Council">Vancouver City Council</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_municipal_election,_2011" title="Vancouver municipal election, 2011">2011 Vancouver municipal election</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-VSun_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VSun-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Carr subsequently won the seat.<sup id="cite_ref-VO1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VO1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GS1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GS1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Carr retained her council seat during the <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_municipal_election,_2014" title="Vancouver municipal election, 2014">2014 Vancouver municipal election</a>, winning with the highest number of votes of any council candidate.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> School board candidate Janet Fraser and Park Board candidates Michael Wiebe and Stuart Mackinnon were also elected.
</p><p>The Green Party of Vancouver further increased its seat count following the <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_municipal_by-election,_2017" title="Vancouver municipal by-election, 2017">2017 Vancouver municipal by-election</a> where all three of the party's school board candidates were elected. The party will run 10 candidates in the <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_municipal_election,_2018" title="Vancouver municipal election, 2018">2018 Vancouver municipal election</a>.
</p>

</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Council_Platform">Council Platform</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_of_Vancouver&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Council Platform">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Public_interest" title="Public interest">Public interest</a> first</li>
<li>Real affordable housing</li>
<li>A compassionate, safe, and inclusive city</li>
<li>People-centred planning</li>
<li>Practical transportation solutions</li>
<li>A robust and resilient local economy</li>
<li>A genuine <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_city" title="Sustainable city">green city</a><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Park_Board_Platform">Park Board Platform</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_of_Vancouver&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Park Board Platform">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>Community-driven planning</li>
<li>Accessible, safe <a href="/wiki/Public_space" title="Public space">public spaces</a> for everyone</li>
<li>Strengthening stakeholder communication</li>
<li>Maximizing facility usage with a stronger digital strategy</li>
<li>Green initiatives: <a href="/wiki/Zero_waste" title="Zero waste">zero waste</a>, local food systems, and access to nature</li>
<li>Parks that focus on natural features</li>
<li>Revitalized facility and <a href="/wiki/Park" title="Park">park</a> infrastructure</li>
<li>Outdoor pools and <a href="/wiki/National_Historic_Sites_of_Canada" title="National Historic Sites of Canada">heritage buildings</a><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<li>Safe schools for everyone</li>
<li>Financial sustainability and transparency</li>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Prior_to_2014">Prior to 2014</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_of_Vancouver&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Prior to 2014">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Roslyn Cassels was the first elected Green in Canada and was elected to the Vancouver Park Board in the <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_municipal_election,_1999" title="Vancouver municipal election, 1999">1999 Vancouver municipal election</a>. In 2005, <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Reimer" title="Andrea Reimer">Andrea Reimer</a> was elected to the <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_School_Board" title="Vancouver School Board">Vancouver School Board</a> as a Trustee, and in 2008 Stuart Mackinnon was elected a Park Board Commissioner.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_municipal_election">2014 municipal election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_of_Vancouver&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2014 municipal election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Green Party of Vancouver nominated seven candidates for the <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_municipal_election,_2014" title="Vancouver municipal election, 2014">2014 Vancouver municipal election</a>, held on November 15th 2014. <a href="/wiki/Adriane_Carr" title="Adriane Carr">Adriane Carr</a> received the most votes of any council candidate.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>School board candidate Janet Fraser and Park Board candidates Michael Wiebe and Stuart Mackinnon were also elected.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2017_municipal_by-election">2017 municipal by-election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_of_Vancouver&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2017 municipal by-election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_municipal_by-election,_2017" title="Vancouver municipal by-election, 2017">2017 Vancouver municipal by-election</a> was called to replace a single vacant council seat, due to <a href="/wiki/Geoff_Meggs" title="Geoff Meggs">Geoff Meggs</a>' departure to take on the role of Premier <a href="/wiki/John_Horgan" title="John Horgan">John Horgan</a>'s chief of staff. The by-election was also meant to elect a new board of school trustees, who had been dismissed by provincial education minister <a href="/wiki/Mike_Bernier" title="Mike Bernier">Mike Bernier</a> after failing to pass a balanced budget and allegations of workplace harassment arose.
</p><p>The Green Party of Vancouver ran Pete Fry for council and Janet Fraser, Estrellita Gonzalez and Judy Zaichkowsky for school board. All three school trustee candidates were elected, finishing in the top three spots.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Non-Partisan_Association" title="Non-Partisan Association">Non-Partisan Association</a> candidate <a href="/wiki/Hector_Bremner" title="Hector Bremner">Hector Bremner</a> was elected to council, and Fry finished a close third behind anti-poverty activist <a href="/wiki/Jean_Swanson" title="Jean Swanson">Jean Swanson</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2018_municipal_election">2018 municipal election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_of_Vancouver&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2018 municipal election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Green Party of Vancouver nominated 11 candidates to run in the <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_municipal_election,_2018" title="Vancouver municipal election, 2018">2018 Vancouver municipal election</a> on June 28th, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> School board candidate Nicholas Chernen resigned on July 9th, 2018 after it was discovered that he had failed to disclose his involvement in a pending lawsuit to the party,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> resulting in the party running four council candidates, three school board candidates, and three park board candidates. Prior to the election, both the Vancouver School Board and Park Board were chaired by Green Party <a href="/wiki/Incumbents" class="mw-redirect" title="Incumbents">incumbents</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
