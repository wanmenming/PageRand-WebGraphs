<p>The <b>Ecological Green Party of <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Partido Verde Ecologista de México</i>, <b>PVEM</b> or <b>PVE</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Green_conservatism" title="Green conservatism">green-conservative</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Mexico" title="List of political parties in Mexico">political party in Mexico</a>. In the 2012 Legislative elections, the party took 34 seats in the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_of_Mexico" class="mw-redirect" title="Chamber of Deputies of Mexico">Chamber of Deputies</a> (out of 500) and nine seats in the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Mexico" class="mw-redirect" title="Senate of Mexico">Senate</a> (out of 128).<sup id="cite_ref-CRSSeelke_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CRSSeelke-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/Mexican_general_election,_2012" title="Mexican general election, 2012">2012 Presidential election</a>, PVEM supported <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Pe%C3%B1a_Nieto" title="Enrique Peña Nieto">Enrique Peña Nieto (EPN)</a>, the candidate from the <a href="/wiki/Institutional_Revolutionary_Party" title="Institutional Revolutionary Party">Institutional Revolutionary Party (PRI)</a>, who was elected.
</p>

<p>In 2008, the PVEM initiated an advertising campaign in favor of reintroducing the <a href="/wiki/Capital_punishment_in_Mexico" title="Capital punishment in Mexico">death penalty in Mexico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> This led to the <a href="/wiki/European_Green_Party" title="European Green Party">European Green Party</a>'s withdrawal of recognition of the PVEM as a legitimate green party.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anti-LGBT_rights_factions">Anti-LGBT rights factions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecologist_Green_Party_of_Mexico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Anti-LGBT rights factions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During an interview, PVE candidate <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gamaliel_Ramirez&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gamaliel Ramirez (page does not exist)">Gamaliel Ramirez</a> verbally attacked an openly gay candidate for Guadalajara mayor and called for criminal laws against homosexuality to be established. In the following days, Ramirez issued a written apology after the party expressed disappointment at his remarks.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While the party has pledged to support LGBT rights issues, 3 representatives abstained from a vote on <a href="/wiki/Same-sex_marriage_in_Mexico_City" title="Same-sex marriage in Mexico City">Mexico City granting legal recognition to same-sex couples</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Accusations_of_corruption_and_nepotism">Accusations of corruption and nepotism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecologist_Green_Party_of_Mexico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Accusations of corruption and nepotism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The PVEM is also widely criticized because its current leader, <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Emilio_Gonz%C3%A1lez_Mart%C3%ADnez" title="Jorge Emilio González Martínez">Jorge Emilio González Martínez</a>, was appointed for being the son of former leader <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Gonz%C3%A1lez_Torres" title="Jorge González Torres">Jorge González Torres</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and for supporting the political and business agenda of Mexican businessman <a href="/wiki/V%C3%ADctor_Gonz%C3%A1lez_Torres" title="Víctor González Torres">Víctor González Torres</a>, owner of the <i>Farmacias Similares</i> drugstore franchise and González Martínez's uncle.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Unlawful_political_advertising_in_movie_theaters">Unlawful political advertising in movie theaters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecologist_Green_Party_of_Mexico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Unlawful political advertising in movie theaters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 2015, the <a href="/wiki/National_Electoral_Institute" class="mw-redirect" title="National Electoral Institute">National Electoral Institute</a> (INE) ordered the PVEM and theater chains <a href="/wiki/Cinemex" title="Cinemex">Cinemex</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cin%C3%A9polis" title="Cinépolis">Cinépolis</a> to cease airing PVEM advertisements, on the grounds of fairness in electoral contests. When the PVEM and the theaters did not comply, the INE imposed a fine of $35 million on the PVEM and $7 million on both theater chains.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
