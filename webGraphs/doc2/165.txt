<p>The <b>Green Left</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Anti-capitalist" class="mw-redirect" title="Anti-capitalist">anti-capitalist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eco-socialist" class="mw-redirect" title="Eco-socialist">eco-socialist</a> grouping within the <a href="/wiki/Green_Party_of_England_and_Wales" title="Green Party of England and Wales">Green Party of England and Wales</a>. It seeks to constitute a network for "socialists and other radicals" in the Green Party, as well as "act[ing] as an outreach body that will communicate the party's radical policies to other socialists and anti-capitalists outside the party." It includes some prominent members of the Green Party of England and Wales, and held its first meeting on 4 June 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-GLSite_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GLSite-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Green Left members were early supporters of an "ecosocialist international", such as the Ecosocialist International Network (EIN).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Left_(UK)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Green Left was launched on 4 June 2006 by members of the Green Party of England and Wales. Those who supported the group included various members of the Green Party of England and Wales Executive, including Richard Mallender (Chair), Peter Cranie (elections co-ordinator)<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and <a href="/wiki/Si%C3%A2n_Berry" title="Siân Berry">Siân Berry</a> (at that time one of the two <a href="/wiki/Principal_speaker" class="mw-redirect" title="Principal speaker">principal speakers</a>, and campaigns coordinator), as well as Penny Kemp (former party chair), Joseph Healy (Secretary, London Green Party), <a href="/wiki/Derek_Wall" title="Derek Wall">Derek Wall</a> (writer, and at that time the other principal speaker) and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Tatchell" title="Peter Tatchell">Peter Tatchell</a> (human rights activist). Cllr Sarah Farrow and Cllr Matt Sellwood were elected as the co-conveners of the new group.<sup id="cite_ref-GLSite_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GLSite-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aims_and_Beliefs">Aims and Beliefs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Left_(UK)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Aims and Beliefs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Green Left formulated its beliefs, agreed on at the meeting, in the <a href="/wiki/Headcorn" title="Headcorn">Headcorn</a> Declaration (<a href="#The_Headcorn_Declaration">below</a>), which stated that Green Left hopes "to raise Green Party politics to meet the demands of its radical policies". The statement criticised the "<a href="/wiki/New_Labour" title="New Labour">New Labour</a> government's abandonment of the policies of the left" and claimed  "that the Green Party's progressive agenda makes it the natural home for the <a href="/wiki/Left-wing_politics" title="Left-wing politics">left</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-GLSite_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GLSite-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Headcorn_Declaration">The Headcorn Declaration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Left_(UK)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Headcorn Declaration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In June 2006, 36 members of the Green Party agreed to the core beliefs and policies of the Headcorn declaration which became a launch statement of the Green left. The launch statement included the following points:<sup id="cite_ref-GLSite_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GLSite-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Green Left is <a href="/wiki/Criticism_of_capitalism" title="Criticism of capitalism">critical of capitalism</a>, and sees capitalism as incompatible with ideals like <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peace" title="Peace">peace</a> and <a href="/wiki/Social_justice" title="Social justice">social justice</a>. As such it places itself in the tradition of <a href="/wiki/William_Morris" title="William Morris">William Morris</a>, the British ecosocialist who operated within the <a href="/wiki/Marxist" class="mw-redirect" title="Marxist">Marxist</a> <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_Federation" title="Social Democratic Federation">Social Democratic Federation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Socialist_League_(UK,_1885)" title="Socialist League (UK, 1885)">Socialist League</a>.</li>
<li>It seeks to unite all <a href="/wiki/Socialist" class="mw-redirect" title="Socialist">socialists</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anti-capitalist" class="mw-redirect" title="Anti-capitalist">anti-capitalists</a> and <a href="/wiki/Political_radicalism" title="Political radicalism">radicals</a>, in and outside of the Green Party.</li>
<li>It welcomes and wishes to continue the <a href="/wiki/Grassroots_democracy" title="Grassroots democracy">grassroots democracy</a> within the Green Party of England and Wales, which should remain a "bottom-up" organisation.</li>
<li>Green Left wants to apply the slogan <i><a href="/wiki/Think_global,_act_local" class="mw-redirect" title="Think global, act local">think global, act local</a></i> to its own party, by increasing international contacts, while also seeking to create local coalitions, made up of various groups such as <a href="/wiki/Trade_unions" class="mw-redirect" title="Trade unions">trade unions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Faith-based_communities" class="mw-redirect" title="Faith-based communities">faith-based communities</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Minority_group" title="Minority group">minority groups</a>.</li></ul>
