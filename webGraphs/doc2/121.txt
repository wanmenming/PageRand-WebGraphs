<p>The <b>Green Party</b> (<a href="/wiki/Slovak_language" title="Slovak language">Slovak</a>: <i lang="sk">Strana zelených</i>, SZ), until January 2006 called Green Party in Slovakia (Slovak: <i lang="sk">Strana zelených na Slovensku</i>), is an environmentalist <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political party</a> in <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a> without parliamentary representation.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_(Slovakia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Green Party was founded on 9 December 1989  as a <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovakia" title="Czechoslovakia">Czechoslovakia</a>-wide party, and was the second green party in post-communist countries. It was registered on 3 February 1990 and three branches were set up – <a href="/wiki/Bohemia" title="Bohemia">Bohemian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moravia" title="Moravia">Moravian</a>-<a href="/wiki/Silesia" title="Silesia">Silesian</a> and Slovak. The first congress of the Slovak branch was held in February 1990 in <a href="/wiki/Banska_Bystrica" class="mw-redirect" title="Banska Bystrica">Banska Bystrica</a>, with Juraj Mesík elected as chairman.
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/Slovak_parliamentary_election,_1990" title="Slovak parliamentary election, 1990">1990 Slovak elections</a> the party received 3.1% of the vote, winning six of the 150 seats in the <a href="/wiki/National_Council_(Slovakia)" title="National Council (Slovakia)">National Council</a>. The combined party failed to win any seats in the <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovak_parliamentary_election,_1990" title="Czechoslovak parliamentary election, 1990">nationwide elections</a>. On 21 October 1991 the Slovak branch of the party was officially registered as a separate party with the Ministry of the Interior as the <b>Green Party of Slovakia</b>.
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/Slovak_parliamentary_election,_1992" title="Slovak parliamentary election, 1992">1992 Slovak elections</a> the party's vote share fell to 1.1% and it lost all six seats. Prior to the <a href="/wiki/Slovak_parliamentary_election,_1994" title="Slovak parliamentary election, 1994">1994 elections</a> the party joined the <a href="/wiki/Common_Choice" title="Common Choice">Common Choice</a> alliance. The alliance finished in second place, winning 18 seats, of which two were taken by the Greens.
</p><p>In June 1995 the party became the 28th full member of the <a href="/wiki/European_Green_Party" title="European Green Party">European Green Party</a>. It joined the <a href="/wiki/Slovak_Democratic_Coalition" title="Slovak Democratic Coalition">Slovak Democratic Coalition</a> (SDK) in the build up to the <a href="/wiki/Slovak_parliamentary_election,_1998" title="Slovak parliamentary election, 1998">1998 elections</a>. With the SDK winning 42 seats, the Greens took four seats. The party contested the <a href="/wiki/Slovak_parliamentary_election,_2002" title="Slovak parliamentary election, 2002">2002 elections</a> alone, but received only 1% of the vote and failing to win a seat.
</p><p>In 2006 it changed its name from the Green Party of Slovakia to the Green Party. It had some candidates on the <a href="/wiki/Free_Forum" title="Free Forum">Free Forum</a> list in the <a href="/wiki/Slovak_parliamentary_election,_2006" title="Slovak parliamentary election, 2006">2006 elections</a>, but failed to win a seat. In the <a href="/wiki/Slovak_parliamentary_election,_2010" title="Slovak parliamentary election, 2010">2010 elections</a> it had candidates on the <a href="/wiki/Party_of_the_Democratic_Left_(Slovakia)" title="Party of the Democratic Left (Slovakia)">Party of the Democratic Left</a> list, again failing to win a seat. They contested the <a href="/wiki/Slovak_parliamentary_election,_2012" title="Slovak parliamentary election, 2012">2012 elections</a> alone, receiving 0.4% of the vote, remaining seatless.
</p>
