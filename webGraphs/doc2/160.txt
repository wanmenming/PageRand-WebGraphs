<p>The <b>leadership election</b> for the <b><a href="/wiki/Green_Party_of_England_and_Wales" title="Green Party of England and Wales">Green Party of England and Wales</a></b>  is held routinely every two years. The 2018 election, which ran from 1 June to 31 August, was the sixth since the party <a href="/wiki/Principal_Speaker" class="mw-redirect" title="Principal Speaker">decided to have a leader</a> and a deputy leader (or two co-leaders). The result was declared on 4 September; <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Bartley" title="Jonathan Bartley">Jonathan Bartley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Si%C3%A2n_Berry" title="Siân Berry">Siân Berry</a> were elected as Co-Leaders and <a href="/wiki/Amelia_Womack" title="Amelia Womack">Amelia Womack</a> was re-elected as Deputy Leader.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_4.9.18_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker_4.9.18-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_of_England_and_Wales_leadership_election,_2018&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Green Party elects its leader (or joint leaders), deputy leader (or two co-deputies) and seven other senior positions every two years.<sup id="cite_ref-guardiangreensanounce_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardiangreensanounce-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The rules are that co-leaders will have one deputy, but if there is a single leader elected, they will have two deputies. The election process is also subject to balancing for gender.
</p><p>Incumbent Co-Leader <a href="/wiki/Caroline_Lucas" title="Caroline Lucas">Caroline Lucas</a> announced on 30 May 2018 that she would not seek nomination and was therefore standing down.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The other incumbent Co-Leader, <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Bartley" title="Jonathan Bartley">Jonathan Bartley</a>, announced on 1 June that he would run for re-election, in a <a href="/wiki/Job_sharing" title="Job sharing">job share</a> with former Principal Speaker and current <a href="/wiki/London_Assembly" title="London Assembly">London Assembly</a> group leader <a href="/wiki/Si%C3%A2n_Berry" title="Siân Berry">Siân Berry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Incumbent Deputy Leader <a href="/wiki/Amelia_Womack" title="Amelia Womack">Amelia Womack</a> announced on 7 June that she would also run for re-election.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Other candidates contested each of the elections.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Timetable">Timetable</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_of_England_and_Wales_leadership_election,_2018&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Timetable">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nominations opened on 1 June and closed on 29 June. Voting was from 30 June to 31 August.<sup id="cite_ref-Elections_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Elections-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Seven hustings were arranged for leadership and deputy leadership candidates<sup id="cite_ref-Elections_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Elections-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>&#160;– on 14 July in <a href="/wiki/Birmingham" title="Birmingham">Birmingham</a>, on 21 July in <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a><sup id="cite_ref-Willers_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Willers-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>, on 28 July in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cambridge" title="Cambridge">Cambridge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bristol" title="Bristol">Bristol</a>, and on 4 August in <a href="/wiki/Chepstow" title="Chepstow">Chepstow</a>. The election result was declared on 4 September.<sup id="cite_ref-guardiangreensanounce_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardiangreensanounce-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Tim Young's application was rejected as he had insufficient nominations.<sup id="cite_ref-candidates_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-candidates-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Leadership_campaign">Leadership campaign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_of_England_and_Wales_leadership_election,_2018&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Leadership campaign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Berry said that, if elected, she would continue as a councillor in Highgate and a <a href="/wiki/London_Assembly" title="London Assembly">London Assembly</a> member. Oliver Lewis, a <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour Party</a> councillor for the same ward as Berry, said, "She [Siân] must have known this was coming. They had a leadership website ready to go."<sup id="cite_ref-Olsey_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Olsey-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Bartley and Berry released a detailed plan for their leadership on their website, promising a renewed focus on <a href="/wiki/Non-violent_direct_action" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-violent direct action">non-violent direct action</a>, expanding election training to train the next generation of Green leaders, and to prepare the party for greater electoral success.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Berry said that she would not be interested in standing for Parliament, if elected Co-Leader, but would use her increased profile to compete again for the London mayoralty.<sup id="cite_ref-Morris_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Morris-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 11 August 2018, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times">The Times</a></i> reported that on <a href="/wiki/Holocaust_Memorial_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="Holocaust Memorial Day">Holocaust Memorial Day</a>, in January 2009, Shahrar Ali had made a speech comparing Israel’s treatment of Palestinians to the <a href="/wiki/Holocaust" class="mw-redirect" title="Holocaust">Holocaust</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Webber_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Webber-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In an article published on the <i><a href="/wiki/Left_Foot_Forward" title="Left Foot Forward">Left Foot Forward</a></i> blog, the <a href="/wiki/Campaign_Against_Antisemitism" title="Campaign Against Antisemitism">Campaign Against Antisemitism</a> described his speech as <a href="/wiki/Antisemitism" title="Antisemitism">anti-semitic</a> and an “offensive rant”.<sup id="cite_ref-anti_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-anti-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Ali described the accusation as a "gross fabrication", telling the <i><a href="/wiki/Evening_Standard" class="mw-redirect" title="Evening Standard">Evening Standard</a></i> that it was "designed to stifle legitimate criticism of the Israeli government".<sup id="cite_ref-Londoner_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Londoner-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The Green Party said that its initial handling of reports about Ali's speech in 2009 were inadequate and that it is seeking to revise procedures.<sup id="cite_ref-Welch_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Welch-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
