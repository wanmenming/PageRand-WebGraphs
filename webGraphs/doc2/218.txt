<p><b>Four Green Steps</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">internet</a> <a href="/wiki/Environmentalism" title="Environmentalism">environmental</a> organization based in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Concordian_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Concordian-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The organization was founded in 2008 by Jaye and Bill Yarrow. The business has been active for three years, but the official launch was held in October 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-Westmount_Independent_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Westmount_Independent-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The organization consists of four sections: School Program, Community, Marketplace and InfoZone.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Concordian_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Concordian-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Four Green Steps contains information on various environmental topics such as green living, sustainable products, and corporate responsibility. The site also provides an environmental curriculum for various schools of different ages.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Alternative_Consumer_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Alternative_Consumer-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Environmental_Education_in_Kentucky_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Environmental_Education_in_Kentucky-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>...<sup id="cite_ref-Environmental_Education_in_Georgia_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Environmental_Education_in_Georgia-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Environmental_Education_in_Mississippi_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Environmental_Education_in_Mississippi-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The organization celebrated 'Unsolicited Acts of Kindness Week' in August 2012, celebrating by donating used office equipment and pledging to plant a tree for anyone who does a good deed during Kindness Week.<sup id="cite_ref-Four_Green_Steps_Presents_Unsolicited_Acts_of_Kindness_Week_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Four_Green_Steps_Presents_Unsolicited_Acts_of_Kindness_Week-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Four_Green_Steps&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="School_program">School program</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Four_Green_Steps&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: School program">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Four Green Steps school program was developed in an effort to encourage a younger generation to become interested in environmental sustainability.<sup id="cite_ref-HEAA_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HEAA-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LEEF_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LEEF-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Kids_Garden_News_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kids_Garden_News-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> 
<sup id="cite_ref-EE_in_Alabama_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EE_in_Alabama-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-EE_in_NC_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EE_in_NC-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-EE_in_Tennessee_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EE_in_Tennessee-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The school program is a comprehensive, multipurpose organization dedicated to bringing awareness and solutions to our shared environmental problems.<sup id="cite_ref-ESE_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ESE-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The School Program has a universal curriculum and activities for primary and secondary schools.<sup id="cite_ref-New_Zealand_Education_Gazette_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-New_Zealand_Education_Gazette-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MEEA_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MEEA-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BASEF_2012_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BASEF_2012-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Macomb_County_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Macomb_County-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-EEinWisconsin_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EEinWisconsin-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
The program features a curriculum of various assignments that will entice students to use their creativity and imagination, learn sustainable habits, take initiative, and develop a sense of environmental responsibility — all while fostering a sense of community and interaction among students the world over.<sup id="cite_ref-Four_Green_Steps_Wisconsin_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Four_Green_Steps_Wisconsin-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-EEON_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EEON-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The site also offers opportunities for students to share their projects and ideas online as well as compete for incredible prizes.<sup id="cite_ref-CREEC_Network_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CREEC_Network-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The School Program is offered for free and available in five different languages: English, French, Spanish, Arabic and Chinese.<sup id="cite_ref-Four_Green_Steps_School_Program_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Four_Green_Steps_School_Program-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The curriculum's environmental focus includes the following subjects Animals/Wildlife, Biodiversity, Climate Change/Weather, Conservation, Creative Arts, Ecology, Energy, Gardening/Composting, Habitats/Ecosystems, Litter, Nature Awareness, Plants, Pollution, Recycling/Waste Management, Sustainability, Team-building/Problem-solving, Water.<sup id="cite_ref-Four_Green_Steps:_Making_a_Difference_One_Step_at_a_Time_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Four_Green_Steps:_Making_a_Difference_One_Step_at_a_Time-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> It was first launched in October 2009 and was used by 40 schools in 15 different countries that year.<sup id="cite_ref-Teacher_Magazine_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Teacher_Magazine-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> They also host an environmental science fair and ‘one-day’ curriculum designed for substitutes or special events. The school program has engaged hundreds of schools in more than 55 countries worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-Montreal_Gazette_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Montreal_Gazette-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Canada.com_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Canada.com-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Community">Community</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Four_Green_Steps&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Community">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Community section is made up of <a href="/wiki/Blog" title="Blog">blogs</a>, videos, events and recipes related to the environment. The public can also contribute their own ideas.<sup id="cite_ref-Small_Business_Big_Impact_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Small_Business_Big_Impact-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-The_Team_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Team-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Four Green Steps was an official partner of the LA Green Festival <sup id="cite_ref-Green_Festivals_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Green_Festivals-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Actor Booboo Stewart and sister Fivel Stewart joined the Four Green Steps team in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Booboo_Girls_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Booboo_Girls-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketplace">Marketplace</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Four_Green_Steps&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Marketplace">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The organization sells products that are certified organic, fair-trade or are listed that they are produced in a more sustainable way. Products include clothing, food, furniture and household products.<sup id="cite_ref-Small_Business_Big_Impact_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Small_Business_Big_Impact-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Four Green Steps launched their marketplace at the Los Angeles Green Festival in October 2011 <sup id="cite_ref-Green_Towns_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Green_Towns-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="InfoZone">InfoZone</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Four_Green_Steps&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: InfoZone">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Infozone contains articles discussing environmental issues in various subject areas including science and technology, business and world news.<sup id="cite_ref-Green_Diets_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Green_Diets-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Four_Green_Steps&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ethical products and services are becoming increasingly popular, especially in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> where $12 billion ethical products were sold in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-Vancouver_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vancouver-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of August 10, 2010, there are schools in 72 different countries registered for Four Green Step’s School Programs.<sup id="cite_ref-Teacher_Magazine_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Teacher_Magazine-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The School Program is also recognized by the New Zealand Ministry of Education.,<sup id="cite_ref-New_Zealand_Education_Gazette_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-New_Zealand_Education_Gazette-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> as well as the state of <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> <sup id="cite_ref-EEddNews_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EEddNews-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Four Green Steps collaborates with students at universities in Montreal such as <a href="/wiki/McGill_University" title="McGill University">McGill University</a> <sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Concordia_University_(Quebec)" class="mw-redirect" title="Concordia University (Quebec)">Concordia University</a> <sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> on environmental initiatives and research.
</p><p>They are recognized by <a href="/wiki/ECO_Canada" title="ECO Canada">ECO Canada</a> as an environmental organization.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Booboo_Stewart" title="Booboo Stewart">Booboo Stewart</a> of Twilight <sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> and his younger sister Fivel are both involved with Four Green Steps <sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
