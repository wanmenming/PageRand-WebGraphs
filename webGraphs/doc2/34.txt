<p>The term <b>green left</b> is used primarily to refer to a combination of <a href="/wiki/Environmentalism" title="Environmentalism">environmentalism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Feminism" title="Feminism">feminism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Socialism" title="Socialism">socialism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pacifism" title="Pacifism">pacifism</a> in countries where the term is used. It is primarily a <a href="/wiki/Social_justice" title="Social justice">social justice</a> and <a href="/wiki/Human_rights" title="Human rights">human rights</a> oriented ideology, with an expansion in focus to the <a href="/wiki/Animal_rights" title="Animal rights">rights of other species</a>. 
</p><p>The name "<a href="/wiki/Green_Left_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Green Left (disambiguation)">Green Left</a>" is also used by a variety of organisations which espouse socialist or Marxist principles, but with a greater emphasis on environmental preservation than previous iterations of <a href="/wiki/Socialism" title="Socialism">socialism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Communism" title="Communism">communism</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_left&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_left&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, the green left arose partly out of the declining <a href="/wiki/Eurocommunism" title="Eurocommunism">Eurocommunist</a> tendency that has been mostly associated with various communist parties in the continent. As a result, many former communist parties and remnants of communist parties were either reestablished or fused into existing <a href="/wiki/Green_party" title="Green party">green parties</a>.
</p><p>Far-left political parties or <a href="/wiki/Electoral_alliance" title="Electoral alliance">joint electoral lists</a> have been formed over the years, most often between <a href="/wiki/Marxism" title="Marxism">Marxist</a> and radical greens. In the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, the <a href="/wiki/GroenLinks" title="GroenLinks">GroenLinks</a> party was formed in 1989 by a merger of a <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_the_Netherlands" title="Communist Party of the Netherlands">communist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pacifist_Socialist_Party" title="Pacifist Socialist Party">pacifist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Evangelical_People%27s_Party_(Netherlands)" title="Evangelical People&#39;s Party (Netherlands)">left-wing Christian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Political_Party_of_Radicals" title="Political Party of Radicals">green</a> parties. In December 2007, an <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <a href="/wiki/Electoral_alliance" title="Electoral alliance">electoral coalition</a> of the radical left was formed known as <a href="/wiki/The_Left_%E2%80%93_The_Rainbow" title="The Left – The Rainbow">The Left – The Rainbow</a>, comprising <a href="/wiki/Federation_of_the_Greens" title="Federation of the Greens">Federation of the Greens</a>, two communist parties and a small <a href="/wiki/Democratic_socialism" title="Democratic socialism">democratic socialist</a> party.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Elsewhere">Elsewhere</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_left&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Elsewhere">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The green left has also been prominent in the <a href="/wiki/Green_politics" title="Green politics">green politics</a> outside of Europe, especially in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, although these green parties may involve themselves in alliances with otherwise conservative political organisations in <a href="/wiki/Blue%E2%80%93green_alliance" title="Blue–green alliance">blue–green alliances</a>.
</p>
