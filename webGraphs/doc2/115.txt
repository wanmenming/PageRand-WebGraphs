<p><b>The Green Group in the European Parliament</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Green_politics" title="Green politics">Green</a> political group with seats in the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament" title="European Parliament">European Parliament</a> between 1989 and 1999.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Green_Group_in_the_European_Parliament&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1989<sup id="cite_ref-t1s11_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-t1s11-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-t1s3_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-t1s3-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Rainbow_Group_(1984%E2%80%9389)" title="Rainbow Group (1984–89)">Rainbow Group</a> split, with the Greens forming a group called "The Green Group in the European Parliament", and the Regionalists remaining within a continuing but diminished <a href="/wiki/Rainbow_Group_(1989%E2%80%9394)" title="Rainbow Group (1989–94)">Rainbow Group</a>. The Greens and Regionalists stayed within separate parliamentary groups until 1999,<sup id="cite_ref-t1s11_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-t1s11-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-t1s2_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-t1s2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> when the two Groups reunited under the <a href="/wiki/Greens%E2%80%93European_Free_Alliance" title="Greens–European Free Alliance">European Greens–European Free Alliance</a> banner.<sup id="cite_ref-t1s2_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-t1s2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nomenclature">Nomenclature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Green_Group_in_the_European_Parliament&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Nomenclature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The group's formal name was "<i>The</i> Green Group in the European Parliament",<sup id="cite_ref-t1s6_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-t1s6-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-t1s59_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-t1s59-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ZAESs1752cdb_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ZAESs1752cdb-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> not the "Green Group in the European Parliament". Unlike other groups, "The" was part of the formal name of the group.
</p>
