<p>The <b>Green Party</b> (<a href="/wiki/Serbian_language" title="Serbian language">Serbian</a>: <span lang="sr">Зелена Странка</span>, <i>Zelena Stranka</i>, ZES) is a <a href="/wiki/Slovaks_in_Serbia" title="Slovaks in Serbia">Slovak minority</a> political party in <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> based in <a href="/wiki/Novi_Sad" title="Novi Sad">Novi Sad</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_(Serbia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The party was established in 2014. In the <a href="/wiki/Serbian_parliamentary_election,_2016" title="Serbian parliamentary election, 2016">2016 parliamentary elections</a> it received 0.6% of the vote, well below the 5% threshold. However, as a registered minority party it was exempted from the threshold and won a single seat in the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_(Serbia)" title="National Assembly (Serbia)">National Assembly</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
