<p>The <b>Green Senatorial Campaign Committee</b> (<b>GSCC</b>) is the <a href="/wiki/Green_Party_of_the_United_States" title="Green Party of the United States">Green Party</a> <a href="/wiki/Committee" title="Committee">committee</a> for the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">United States Senate</a>, working to elect Greens to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">United States Senate</a>. The GSCC filed with the US <a href="/wiki/Federal_Election_Commission" title="Federal Election Commission">Federal Election Commission</a> for official recognition in September 2006, which was received in February 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> This is the first time a party other than the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Senatorial_Campaign_Committee" title="Democratic Senatorial Campaign Committee">Democrats</a> or <a href="/wiki/National_Republican_Senatorial_Committee" title="National Republican Senatorial Committee">Republicans</a> have had a Senatorial Campaign Committee recognized by the FEC.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Senatorial_Campaign_Committee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the 2006 elections the party created the GSCC in June 2006, based in Minneapolis. Without official recognition, the committee had to operate like a <a href="/wiki/Political_action_committee" title="Political action committee">political action committee</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Senatorial_Campaign_Committee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>GSCC is a seven-member committee elected by the National Committee of the Green Party of the United States (GPUS).  It presently has one vacancy. Its current members including its nonvoting Treasurer are:
</p>
<ul><li>Teresa Keane, Chair: (Portland, Oregon)</li>
<li>Anita Wessling, Vice-Chair: (Omaha, Arkansas)</li>
<li>Brent White, Secretary: (Seattle, Washington)</li>
<li>Dave Jette, Treasurer and Webmaster: (Seattle, Washington)</li>
<li>Eric Oines, Assistant Treasurer: (Minneapolis, Minnesota)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jim_Lendall" title="Jim Lendall">Jim Lendall</a>, (Mabelvale, Arkansas)</li>
<li>Marc Sanson, (Springfield, Illinois)</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Former_Members">Former Members</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Senatorial_Campaign_Committee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Former Members">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>Roger Snyder, (Founding Member) (New York)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dean_Myerson" title="Dean Myerson">Dean Myerson</a>, (Founding Member) nonvoting Treasurer: (Washington)</li></ul>
<p>During the 2006 elections the GSCC contributed $1,809 to 9 Green Senatorial Campaigns.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
