<p><b>Jungle green</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Color" title="Color">color</a> that is a rich tone of medium <a href="/wiki/Spring_green" title="Spring green">spring green</a>.
</p><p>The specific tone of the color <i>jungle green</i> called "jungle green" by Crayola, displayed at right, was formulated by Crayola in 1990.
</p><p>The first recorded use of <i>jungle green</i> as a color name in <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> was in 1926.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_culture">In culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jungle_green&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: In culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl><dt>Military</dt></dl>
<p><i>See also <a href="/wiki/Green_Berets_in_popular_culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Green Berets in popular culture">Green Berets in popular culture</a></i>
</p>
<ul><li>In the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">United States Army</a>, <i>jungle green</i> is the color used for the uniforms and berets of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Special_Forces" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Army Special Forces">United States Army Special Forces</a>.  (The shade of jungle green used in the uniforms and berets of the U.S. Army <i>Green Berets</i> is closely equivalent to the color shown above as <i>deep jungle green</i>.) In the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a> <i>jungle green</i> is the color of the combat or working uniform worn in the <a href="/wiki/Far_East" title="Far East">Far East</a> and in parts of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>.  The uniform was often called "jaygees" in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>. Green berets are also used by the elite forces of a number of other nations--see the article on <a href="/wiki/Green_berets" class="mw-redirect" title="Green berets">green berets</a>.  The berets used by these various elite military forces are usually a tone of <i>jungle green</i> or <a href="/wiki/Forest_green" title="Forest green">forest green</a>.</li></ul>
