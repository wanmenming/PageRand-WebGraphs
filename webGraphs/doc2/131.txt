<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Green Scare</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>
				<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Support_Shac_7_Banner.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Support_Shac_7_Banner.jpg/220px-Support_Shac_7_Banner.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Support_Shac_7_Banner.jpg/330px-Support_Shac_7_Banner.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/7c/Support_Shac_7_Banner.jpg 2x" data-file-width="400" data-file-height="300" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Support_Shac_7_Banner.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Banner supporting the <a href="/wiki/SHAC_7" class="mw-redirect" title="SHAC 7">SHAC 7</a> and the green scare website.</div></div></div>
<p>The <b>Green Scare</b> is legal action by the <a href="/wiki/US_government" class="mw-redirect" title="US government">US government</a> against the radical <a href="/wiki/Environmental_movement" title="Environmental movement">environmental movement</a>. It alludes to the <a href="/wiki/Red_Scare" title="Red Scare">Red Scares</a>, periods of fear over <a href="/wiki/Communist" class="mw-redirect" title="Communist">communist</a> infiltration of US society.
</p><p>The term was popularized by <a href="/wiki/Environmental_activist" class="mw-redirect" title="Environmental activist">environmental activists</a>. It is first known to have appeared in 2002 in the wake of the February 12 congressional hearings titled "The Threat of Eco-Terrorism", which discussed groups including the <a href="/wiki/Earth_Liberation_Front" title="Earth Liberation Front">Earth Liberation Front</a> (ELF) and the <a href="/wiki/Animal_Liberation_Front" title="Animal Liberation Front">Animal Liberation Front</a> (ALF).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The spring edition of the prisoner support <a href="/wiki/Zine" title="Zine">zine</a> <i>Spirit of Freedom</i> defined the term as "the tactics that the US government and all their tentacles (<a href="/wiki/FBI" class="mw-redirect" title="FBI">FBI</a>, <a href="/wiki/IRS" class="mw-redirect" title="IRS">IRS</a>, <a href="/wiki/BATF" class="mw-redirect" title="BATF">BATF</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joint_Terrorism_Task_Force" title="Joint Terrorism Task Force">Joint Terrorism Task Forces</a>, local police, the court system) are using to attack the ELF/ALF and specifically those who publicly support them."
</p><p>The term has been used by activists to describe a sweep of arrests, convictions and <a href="/wiki/Grand_jury" title="Grand jury">grand jury</a> indictments of ELF and ALF operatives on charges relating to acts of <a href="/wiki/Property_damage" title="Property damage">property damage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Conspiracy_(crime)" class="mw-redirect" title="Conspiracy (crime)">conspiracy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arson" title="Arson">arson</a> and use of <a href="/wiki/Destructive_device" title="Destructive device">destructive devices</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cases">Cases</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Scare&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Cases">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since 2000, the <a href="/wiki/US_government" class="mw-redirect" title="US government">US government</a> has prosecuted over 20 cases involving <a href="/wiki/Environmentalist" title="Environmentalist">environmentalists</a>, which <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_CityBeat" class="mw-redirect" title="Los Angeles CityBeat">Los Angeles CityBeat</a></i> claims "redefined not only free speech, but also redefined environmentally motivated property destruction - like torching <a href="/wiki/Hummer" title="Hummer">Hummers</a> or tree-felling equipment." <sup id="cite_ref-CityBeat_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CityBeat-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> These arrests, indictments and trials, which have collectively been termed the <i>Green Scare</i> by <a href="/wiki/Environmentalist" title="Environmentalist">environmentalists</a>, include:
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Individuals">Individuals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Scare&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Individuals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Rod_Coronado" title="Rod Coronado">Rod Coronado</a>, a prominent <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_the_Americas" title="Indigenous peoples of the Americas">Native American</a> <a href="/wiki/Eco-anarchist" class="mw-redirect" title="Eco-anarchist">eco-anarchist</a>, who was arrested on a felony charge of demonstrating the use of a destructive device.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> His indictment indirectly related to an August 1, 2003 fire in <a href="/wiki/San_Diego,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="San Diego, California">San Diego</a> that destroyed an apartment complex causing an estimated $50 million worth of damage.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Coronado, a self-described "unofficial ELF spokesman",<sup id="cite_ref-NZ_Herald_10370129_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NZ_Herald_10370129-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> stood trial in 2007. After two days of deliberations, the jury remained deadlocked so the judge declared a mistrial.<sup id="cite_ref-Hoffman_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hoffman-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He subsequently entered a guilty plea and accepted a deal for a one-year prison term.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Jeff_Luers" title="Jeff Luers">Jeff "Free" Luers</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Veganarchist" class="mw-redirect" title="Veganarchist">veganarchist</a> from <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles, California</a>, who served a prison sentence of almost ten years for <a href="/wiki/Arson" title="Arson">arson</a>.  His initial sentence was over 22 years, but the <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Court_of_Appeals" title="Oregon Court of Appeals">Oregon Court of Appeals</a> overturned it,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and the Lane County Circuit Court determined a new sentence of 10 years in February 2008,<sup id="cite_ref-RGresentence_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RGresentence-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> after what <i><a href="/wiki/The_Independent" title="The Independent">The Independent</a></i> described as an "international campaign for a more appropriate sentence for a crime in which no one was hurt." <sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In 2000 he set fire to three <a href="/wiki/SUV" class="mw-redirect" title="SUV">SUVs</a> at Romania Chevrolet dealership in <a href="/wiki/Eugene,_Oregon" title="Eugene, Oregon">Eugene, Oregon</a> as a protest against excessive consumption and <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-hug_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hug-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>  He has become a <a href="/wiki/Cause_c%C3%A9l%C3%A8bre" title="Cause célèbre">cause célèbre</a> among some radicals, <a href="/wiki/Prison_abolition_movement" title="Prison abolition movement">anti-prison activists</a>, and people associated with the Earth Liberation Front, although Luers has said that he does not consider himself an ELF member.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>  Upon his resentencing, Judge Billings said that he expected Luers would be treated as an "elder statesman" among some activists, and hoped that Luers would use that influence in a productive way. Luers declared that he was looking forward to "promoting activism through legal means."<sup id="cite_ref-RGresentence_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RGresentence-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Christopher McIntosh, who was sentenced to eight years in prison for setting fire to a Seattle <a href="/wiki/McDonald%27s" title="McDonald&#39;s">McDonald's</a> restaurant in January, 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> McIntosh admitted guilt as part of a <a href="/wiki/Plea_agreement" class="mw-redirect" title="Plea agreement">plea agreement</a>, in which the prosecution said they would not ask that he spend more than ten years in prison.<sup id="cite_ref-McIntosh_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McIntosh-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Supporters criticized the sentence as excessive "for someone who caused $5,000 damage". Prosecutors argued it was important to "protect the public" from some one who expressed no remorse, saying McIntosh was "proud of his crime, and, given the chance, he would do the same thing again."<sup id="cite_ref-McIntosh_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McIntosh-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
<a href="/wiki/Peter_Daniel_Young" title="Peter Daniel Young">Peter Daniel Young</a> and a friend, Justin Samuel, who were indicted in by a federal grand jury in September 1998 on four charges of "Extortion by Interfering with Interstate Commerce", and two charges of "Animal Enterprise Terrorism".<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> They released over 8,000 animals from various fur farms in <a href="/wiki/Iowa" title="Iowa">Iowa</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Dakota" title="South Dakota">South Dakota</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin" title="Wisconsin">Wisconsin</a> and disposed of the breeding records at each farm. Samuel was arrested on September 4, 1999 in Belgium and was extradited to the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> to face trial. He agreed to cooperate with the government in exchange for a reduced sentence of two years. Young was arrested in <a href="/wiki/San_Jose,_California" title="San Jose, California">San Jose</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> on March 21, 2005 on charges of <a href="/wiki/Shoplifting" title="Shoplifting">shoplifting</a> from a local <a href="/wiki/Starbucks" title="Starbucks">Starbucks</a> and was extradited to Wisconsin to face trial for the fur farm raids. He was sentenced to two years in federal prison; 360 hours of community service at a charity; $254,000 restitution; and one year probation.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Eric_McDavid" title="Eric McDavid">Eric McDavid</a>, Lauren Weiner and Zachary Jenson, who were arrested in <a href="/wiki/Auburn,_California" title="Auburn, California">Auburn, California</a>, and charged with conspiring to damage telephone towers, the <a href="/wiki/Nimbus_Dam" title="Nimbus Dam">Nimbus Dam</a>, and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Forest_Service" title="United States Forest Service">Forest Service</a> facilities "by explosive or fire".<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Prosecutors allege the FBI recorded one of the suspects suggesting anti-personnel <a href="/wiki/Shrapnel_shell" title="Shrapnel shell">shrapnel</a> be added to the planned bombs.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> According to the FBI affidavit, evidence against the three came from a paid "confidential source (CS) who is deeply embedded within the subjects' cell. The CS had worked for the FBI since early 2004 and agreed to testify in court." <sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Groups">Groups</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Scare&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Groups">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/SHAC_7" class="mw-redirect" title="SHAC 7">SHAC 7</a>, six <a href="/wiki/Animal_rights" title="Animal rights">animal rights</a> activists, who were found guilty of using their website to incite threats, <a href="/wiki/Harassment" title="Harassment">harassment</a>, vandalism and attacks against the company <a href="/wiki/Huntingdon_Life_Sciences" title="Huntingdon Life Sciences">Huntingdon Life Sciences</a> and their business partners.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Originally seven individuals were charged, leading sympathizers to describe the defendants as the <i>SHAC 7</i>, but the case against one of the defendants was dropped. <a href="/wiki/Pamelyn_Ferdin" title="Pamelyn Ferdin">Pamelyn Ferdin</a>, the current president of <a href="/wiki/Stop_Huntingdon_Animal_Cruelty" title="Stop Huntingdon Animal Cruelty">SHAC USA</a>, told the jury that "for the government to say you can't say this and you can't say that is going down a very scary path of going toward <a href="/wiki/Fascism" title="Fascism">fascism</a>." However, the US Attorney's Office described the convicts as "thugs who went far beyond protected speech and lawful protest to engage in and incite intimidation, harassment and violence."<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The judge sentenced the individuals to an aggregate of 24 years in prison, and ordered to pay a joint <a href="/wiki/Restitution" title="Restitution">restitution</a> of $1,000,001.00.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Operation_Backfire_(FBI)" title="Operation Backfire (FBI)">Operation Backfire</a>, a multi-agency criminal investigation into destructive acts in the name of animal rights and environmental causes in the United States, that resulted in the December 7, 2005 arrest of seven people.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> At least six people were subpoenaed to testify before grand juries. Three of the individuals subpoenaed ended up on a January 20, 2006, 65 count, 84-page-long indictment. The new indictment charged 11 people with conspiring to commit 18 acts of arson and vandalism over a 5-year period across multiple states. Some of the charges relate to a 1998 arson attack on the Vail Ski Resort in <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a> and the sabotage of a power line near <a href="/wiki/Bend,_Oregon" title="Bend, Oregon">Bend, Oregon</a>, in 1999. The <a href="/wiki/FBI" class="mw-redirect" title="FBI">FBI</a> considers these crimes to be acts of <a href="/wiki/Domestic_terrorism" title="Domestic terrorism">domestic terrorism</a> and the ELF to be the nation's top domestic terror threat.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Response">Response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Scare&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Christian_Science_Monitor" class="mw-redirect" title="Christian Science Monitor">Christian Science Monitor</a></i> reports that the Operation Backfire indictments have elicited concern, from activists, that authorities have "cracked the super-secrecy of ALF and ELF".<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>  <a href="/wiki/Alternative_media" title="Alternative media">Alternative media</a> organizations have condemned the arrests, some calling them a "<a href="/wiki/Witch-hunt" title="Witch-hunt">witch-hunt</a>", "aimed at disrupting and discrediting political movements". <a href="/wiki/Activist" class="mw-redirect" title="Activist">Activists</a>, maintaining the <i>Red Scare</i> <a href="/wiki/Allusion" title="Allusion">allusion</a>, claim the operations are "<a href="/wiki/Fishing_expedition" title="Fishing expedition">fishing expedition</a>[s]" carried out "in the midst of <a href="/wiki/9/11" class="mw-redirect" title="9/11">9/11</a> <a href="/wiki/McCarthyism" title="McCarthyism">McCarthyism</a>. The FBI disputes these claims, with former Director <a href="/wiki/Robert_Mueller" title="Robert Mueller">Robert Mueller</a> claiming the agency takes action "only when volatile talk crosses the line into violence and criminal activity."<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
