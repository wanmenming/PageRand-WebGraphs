<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">List of Green politicians who have held office in the United States</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>
				<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>As of  October&#160;2016<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=List_of_Green_politicians_who_have_held_office_in_the_United_States&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, 143 officeholders in the United States were affiliated with the <a href="/wiki/Green_Party_of_the_United_States" title="Green Party of the United States">Green Party</a>, the majority of them in <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, several in <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>, <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oregon" title="Oregon">Oregon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin" title="Wisconsin">Wisconsin</a>, with five or fewer in ten other states.<sup id="cite_ref-GPUS01_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPUS01-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> These included one mayor and one deputy mayor, and fourteen county or city commissioners (or equivalent). The remainder were members of school boards, clerks, and other local administrative bodies and positions.<sup id="cite_ref-GPUS01_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPUS01-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Several Green Party members have been elected to state-level office, though not always as affiliates of the party. <a href="/wiki/John_Eder" title="John Eder">John Eder</a> was elected to the <a href="/wiki/Maine_House_of_Representatives" title="Maine House of Representatives">Maine House of Representatives</a>, re-elected in 2004, but defeated in 2006. <a href="/wiki/Audie_Bock" title="Audie Bock">Audie Bock</a> was elected to the <a href="/wiki/California_State_Assembly" title="California State Assembly">California State Assembly</a> in 1999, but switched her registration to Independent seven months later<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> running as such in the 2000 election.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Richard_Carroll_(politician)" title="Richard Carroll (politician)">Richard Carroll</a> was elected to the <a href="/wiki/Arkansas_House_of_Representatives" title="Arkansas House of Representatives">Arkansas House of Representatives</a> in 2008, but switched parties to become a Democrat five months after his election.<sup id="cite_ref-San_Francisco_Chronicle_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-San_Francisco_Chronicle-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Fred_Smith_(Arkansas_politician)" title="Fred Smith (Arkansas politician)">Fred Smith</a> was elected to the <a href="/wiki/Arkansas_House_of_Representatives" title="Arkansas House of Representatives">Arkansas House of Representatives</a> in 2012,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> but re-registered as a Democrat in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-reelection_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reelection-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 2010, former Green Party leader <a href="/wiki/Ben_Chipman" title="Ben Chipman">Ben Chipman</a> was elected to the <a href="/wiki/Maine_House_of_Representatives" title="Maine House of Representatives">Maine House of Representatives</a> as an <a href="/wiki/Independent_politician" title="Independent politician">unenrolled</a> candidate, and was re-elected in 2012 and 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-GPUS04_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPUS04-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Gayle_McLaughlin" title="Gayle McLaughlin">Gayle McLaughlin</a> was twice elected mayor of <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_California" title="Richmond, California">Richmond, California</a>, defeating two Democrats in 2006,<sup id="cite_ref-RichElect_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RichElect-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Rich1_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rich1-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and reelected in 2010, and elected to City Council in 2014 after completing her second term as mayor.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> With a population of over 100,000 people, it was the largest US city with a Green mayor. <a href="/wiki/Fairfax,_California" title="Fairfax, California">Fairfax, California</a>; <a href="/wiki/Arcata,_California" title="Arcata, California">Arcata, California</a>; <a href="/wiki/Sebastopol,_California" title="Sebastopol, California">Sebastopol, California</a>; and <a href="/wiki/New_Paltz,_New_York" title="New Paltz, New York">New Paltz, New York</a> are the only towns in the United States to have had a Green Party majority in their <a href="/wiki/Town_councils" class="mw-redirect" title="Town councils">town councils</a>. Twin Ridges Elementary in <a href="/wiki/Nevada_County,_California" title="Nevada County, California">Nevada County, California</a> held the first Green Party majority school board in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-GPUS05_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPUS05-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>No nominee of the Green Party has been elected to office in the federal government.
</p>
