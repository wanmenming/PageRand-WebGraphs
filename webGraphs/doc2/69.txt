<p>The <b>Green Party of Prince Edward Island</b> is a registered provincial <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political party</a> in <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Island" title="Prince Edward Island">Prince Edward Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> founded by <a href="/wiki/Sharon_Labchuk" title="Sharon Labchuk">Sharon Labchuk</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The party was registered in 2005 and ran candidates in the <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Island_general_election,_2007" title="Prince Edward Island general election, 2007">2007</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Island_general_election,_2011" title="Prince Edward Island general election, 2011">2011 elections</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Island_general_election,_2015" title="Prince Edward Island general election, 2015">2015 election</a>, the party elected its first member of the legislature, leader <a href="/wiki/Peter_Bevan-Baker" title="Peter Bevan-Baker">Peter Bevan-Baker</a>, with 54% of the vote.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_of_Prince_Edward_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Green Party of PEI  ran 18 candidates during its <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Island_general_election,_2007" title="Prince Edward Island general election, 2007">first election</a>, in May 2007, winning 3.04% of the vote or 4.44% in ridings contested, and replacing the <a href="/wiki/Island_New_Democrats" class="mw-redirect" title="Island New Democrats">Island New Democrats</a> as the Island's third party.  The result had marked the first time a Green Party has bested a New Democratic Party at the federal or provincial level in Canada at that time.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A third of all candidates won more than 5.40%.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Jamie Larkin had the best result of 8.45% followed by dentist Peter Bevan-Baker 6.84%, Cindy Burton 6.68%, Party leader <a href="/wiki/Sharon_Labchuk" title="Sharon Labchuk">Sharon Labchuk</a> 6.03%, Denise Reiser 5.79%, and Jodie Bowmaster 5.40%.
</p><p>All other candidates captured less than 5.0%, however 14 out of the 18 candidates had placed ahead of the NDP, two of the four candidates who finished fourth did so by just one and two votes.
</p><p>The party ran a candidate in the 2007 by-election<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> to replace <a href="/wiki/Pat_Binns" title="Pat Binns">Pat Binns</a> in the riding of <a href="/wiki/Belfast-Murray_River" title="Belfast-Murray River">Belfast-Murray River</a>. Candidate Ahmon Katz finished 3.2% ahead of NDP candidate Jane McNeil.
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Island_general_election,_2007" title="Prince Edward Island general election, 2007">2011 general election</a> the party increased its number of candidates from 18 in the 2007 election to 22, and upped the vote share from 3% to 4.4%, or 5.3% in ridings contested, beating out the PEI New Democrats for third place again. The party increased its votes per riding from 138 to 147, even though turnout in the province fell from 84 to 77%.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Party leader Sharon Labchuk running in <a href="/wiki/Charlottetown-Victoria_Park" title="Charlottetown-Victoria Park">Charlottetown-Victoria Park</a> had the best result at 12.82%, followed by Elizabeth Schoales in <a href="/wiki/Charlottetown-Brighton" title="Charlottetown-Brighton">Charlottetown-Brighton</a> at 10.17%, and Peter Bevan-Baker in <a href="/wiki/Kellys_Cross-Cumberland" title="Kellys Cross-Cumberland">Kellys Cross-Cumberland</a> at 9.36%.
</p><p>At 59%, the party had the highest number of women candidates in the 2011 election.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  The Green Party of PEI is the first Canadian political party to run more than 50% women candidates.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Sharon_Labchuk" title="Sharon Labchuk">Sharon Labchuk</a> resigned the <a href="/wiki/Green_Party_of_Prince_Edward_Island_leadership_election,_2012" title="Green Party of Prince Edward Island leadership election, 2012">leadership</a> of the Green Party of PEI on 12 July 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-TGC_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TGC-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> She was succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Bevan-Baker" title="Peter Bevan-Baker">Peter Bevan-Baker</a> on November 3, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The party released its platform for the 2015 election on March 20, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In that election, leader Bevan-Baker was elected in the district of <a href="/wiki/Kellys_Cross-Cumberland" title="Kellys Cross-Cumberland">Kellys Cross-Cumberland</a> with 54% of the vote, and won the party's first seat.
</p><p>The party secured their second seat in a by-election to fill a vacancy left by retiring <a href="/wiki/Charlottetown-Parkdale" title="Charlottetown-Parkdale">Charlottetown-Parkdale</a> MLA, <a href="/wiki/Doug_Currie" title="Doug Currie">Doug Currie</a>, on November 27, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-CBCPEI17_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBCPEI17-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
