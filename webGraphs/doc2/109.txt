<p>The <b>Democratic Green Party of Rwanda</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Green_party" title="Green party">green political party</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rwanda" title="Rwanda">Rwanda</a>, established in 2009. The party was registered in August 2013, but too late to contest the <a href="/wiki/Rwandan_parliamentary_election,_2013" title="Rwandan parliamentary election, 2013">2013 parliamentary elections</a>. Its platform emphasizes unity, non-violence, social justice, participatory democracy, and calls for subsidized prices for agricultural produce. It believes that the unalienable rights of the people include "the right to life, liberty, peaceful assembly, expression, worship and the pursuit of happiness", and that these rights are granted by God.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Democratic_Green_Party_of_Rwanda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The party was established on 14 August 2009, and aimed to contest the <a href="/wiki/Rwandan_presidential_election,_2010" title="Rwandan presidential election, 2010">2010 presidential elections</a>. However, it was prevented from registering.<sup id="cite_ref-Time_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Time-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The party's vice-president, <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Kagwa_Rwisereka" title="André Kagwa Rwisereka">André Kagwa Rwisereka</a>, was found beheaded during the election campaign.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Green Party leaders in the US called on the Obama Administration to support an investigation into his murder and the allegations that it was politically motivated. President <a href="/wiki/Paul_Kagame" title="Paul Kagame">Paul Kagame</a> and his ruling <a href="/wiki/Rwanda_Patriotic_Front" class="mw-redirect" title="Rwanda Patriotic Front">Rwanda Patriotic Front</a> (RFP) has close ties to the US.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>The party was finally registered in August 2013, but too late to contest the <a href="/wiki/Rwandan_parliamentary_election,_2013" title="Rwandan parliamentary election, 2013">2013 parliamentary elections</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 17 December 2016, <a href="/wiki/Frank_Habineza" title="Frank Habineza">Frank Habineza</a> was nominated as the party leader and flag bearer for the <a href="/wiki/Rwandan_presidential_election,_2017" title="Rwandan presidential election, 2017">2017 presidential elections</a>. By doing this the party abandoned its earlier threat to boycott the election after its demands for electoral reforms were snubbed by the government.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Habineza  went on to finish third of the three candidates with just 0.5% of the vote. However, in the <a href="/wiki/Rwandan_parliamentary_election,_2018" title="Rwandan parliamentary election, 2018">parliamentary elections</a> the following year the party entered parliament after winning two seats.
</p>
