<p>A <b>green company</b> claims to act in a way which minimizes <a href="/wiki/Environmental_degradation" title="Environmental degradation">damage to the environment</a>.
</p><p>As <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a> continues apace and becomes an increasingly prevalent topic amongst world governments, many companies are doing their part to become environmentally more responsible or "green".
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Examples">Examples</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Examples">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Automotive">Automotive</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Automotive">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A number of major auto companies have developed <a href="/wiki/Alternative_fuel_vehicle" title="Alternative fuel vehicle">alternative fuel vehicles</a> and all-electric vehicles in a move to reduce  petroleum consumption. Technologies include <a href="/wiki/Compressed_natural_gas" title="Compressed natural gas">compressed natural gas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fuel_cell_vehicle" title="Fuel cell vehicle">Fuel cell vehicles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Battery_electric_vehicle" title="Battery electric vehicle">Battery electric vehicles</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_electric_vehicle" title="Hybrid electric vehicle">Hybrid electric vehicles</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Aviation">Aviation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Aviation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Environmental_impact_of_aviation" title="Environmental impact of aviation">environmental impact of aviation</a> is significant, though a number of companies are working on <a href="/wiki/Mitigation_of_aviation%27s_environmental_impact" title="Mitigation of aviation&#39;s environmental impact">mitigation of aviation's environmental impact</a> through new technologies including <a href="/wiki/Aviation_biofuel" title="Aviation biofuel">aviation biofuel</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Doors_and_Garage_Doors">Doors and Garage Doors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Doors and Garage Doors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Pella" title="Pella">Pella</a>, <a href="/wiki/Masonite_International" title="Masonite International">Masonite</a>, Marvin, <a href="/wiki/Jeld_Wen" class="mw-redirect" title="Jeld Wen">Jeld-Wen</a> and other top door manufacturers claim that their entry doors meet <a href="/wiki/Energy_Star" title="Energy Star">Energy Star</a> requirements. <a href="/wiki/Doors" class="mw-redirect" title="Doors">Entry doors</a> represent an important source of energy waste in buildings.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Ultimately, doors are an important cause of <a href="/wiki/Carbon_emissions" class="mw-redirect" title="Carbon emissions">carbon emissions</a> (buildings account for about 41% of our primary energy consumption and an almost equivalent amount of CO2 emissions).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Petroleum">Petroleum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Petroleum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Suncor" class="mw-redirect" title="Suncor">Suncor</a>, a Canadian-based oil company, was named a top performer among 23 global petroleum-producing firms with its environmentally responsible greenhouse gas management programs.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Household">Household</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Household">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/S.C._Johnson" class="mw-redirect" title="S.C. Johnson">S.C. Johnson</a> Company, maker of household items such as <a href="/wiki/Windex" title="Windex">Windex</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ziploc" title="Ziploc">Ziploc</a> plastic bags was environmentally aware long before it became popular to be so. Using its innovative "Greenlist" process which serves to evaluate what environmental impact certain raw materials used in producing its products can have, S.C. Johnson has been able to eliminate some 1.8 million pounds of <a href="/wiki/Volatile_organic_compounds" class="mw-redirect" title="Volatile organic compounds">volatile organic compounds</a> and 4 million pounds of <a href="/wiki/Polyvinylidene_chloride" title="Polyvinylidene chloride">polyvinylidene chloride</a> from commonly used household items.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Small-business_partnerships">Small-business partnerships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Small-business partnerships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Virginia-Highland" class="mw-redirect" title="Virginia-Highland">Virginia-Highland</a> became the first <a href="/wiki/Carbon-neutral" class="mw-redirect" title="Carbon-neutral">carbon-neutral</a> zone in the United States. This partnership, developed by Verus Carbon Neutral, links 17 merchants of the historic <a href="/wiki/Virginia-Highland_commercial_districts" class="mw-redirect" title="Virginia-Highland commercial districts">Corner Virginia-Highland</a> shopping and dining neighborhood retail district, through the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Climate_Exchange" title="Chicago Climate Exchange">Chicago Climate Exchange</a>, to directly fund the Valley Wood <a href="/wiki/Carbon_Sequestration" class="mw-redirect" title="Carbon Sequestration">Carbon Sequestration</a> Project (thousands of acres of forest in rural <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Supporters of green companies claim that it is far more economical to <a href="/wiki/Go_green" class="mw-redirect" title="Go green">go green</a> than it is to continue adding harmful chemicals to the atmosphere and the environment in general.
</p><p>Opponents believe that the environmental claims of "green companies" are often exaggerated and have variously raised accusations of <a href="/wiki/Greenwash" class="mw-redirect" title="Greenwash">greenwashing</a>.  In 2013, <a href="/wiki/The_Coca-Cola_Company" title="The Coca-Cola Company">The Coca-Cola Company</a> was accused of <a href="/wiki/Greenwashing" title="Greenwashing">greenwashing</a>, after marketing a new line of plastic bottles.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
