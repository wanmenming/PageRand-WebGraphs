<p><b>Independent Green Voice</b> is a minor <a href="/wiki/Environmentalist" title="Environmentalist">environmentalist</a> <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political party</a> in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>.  First registered with the <a href="/wiki/Electoral_Commission_(UK)" class="mw-redirect" title="Electoral Commission (UK)">Electoral Commission</a> in 2003, it is led by Alistair McConnachie.
</p><p>It opposes <a href="/wiki/Debt_slavery" class="mw-redirect" title="Debt slavery">debt slavery</a> and calls for the establishment of a not-for-profit "Public Investment Bank" to replace the "debt-based money system".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The party is also opposed to Public Private Partnerships, and would restore the link between pensions and average earnings.
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Parliament_election,_2003" title="Scottish Parliament election, 2003">2003 Scottish Parliament election</a>, McConnachie stood in <a href="/wiki/Glasgow_Kelvin_(Scottish_Parliament_constituency)" title="Glasgow Kelvin (Scottish Parliament constituency)">Glasgow Kelvin</a> and received 1,300 votes (5.9%).  In the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_general_election,_2005" title="United Kingdom general election, 2005">2005 UK general election</a>, he stood in <a href="/wiki/Glasgow_South_West_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Glasgow South West (UK Parliament constituency)">Glasgow South West</a>, and received 379 votes (1.2%).
</p><p>McConnachie is also the party's treasurer and nominating officer.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
Prior to establishing the party, he was the Scottish organiser for the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Independence_Party" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Independence Party">United Kingdom Independence Party</a> but his membership was not renewed in the summer of 2001 amid allegations that he was a <a href="/wiki/Holocaust_denial" title="Holocaust denial">holocaust denier</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> McConnachie responded to these allegations at the time of the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Parliament_election,_2007" title="Scottish Parliament election, 2007">2007 Scottish Parliamentary election</a><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> when the party stood in the <a href="/wiki/Glasgow_(Scottish_Parliament_electoral_region)" title="Glasgow (Scottish Parliament electoral region)">Glasgow region</a> and received 496 votes (0.2%).
</p>
