<p>The <b>Green Alliance</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Alianza Verde</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombian</a> <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political party</a> located in the centre of the political spectrum.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The party advocates social justice, electoral reform and economic sustainability.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The party supports the <a href="/wiki/Colombian_peace_process" title="Colombian peace process">Colombian peace process</a> and formed the electoral alliance Coalition Colombia with centrist and centre left parties such as Civic Compromise to present a single presidential candidate <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Fajardo" title="Sergio Fajardo">Sergio Fajardo</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Colombian_presidential_election,_2018" title="Colombian presidential election, 2018">2018 presidential election</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Alliance_(Colombia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The party was founded on November 25, 2005 in <a href="/wiki/Bogot%C3%A1" title="Bogotá">Bogotá</a> by a group of people headed by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carlos_Ram%C3%B3n_Gonz%C3%A1lez_Merchan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Carlos Ramón González Merchan (page does not exist)">Carlos Ramón González Merchan</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=El%C3%ADas_Pineda&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Elías Pineda (page does not exist)">Elías Pineda</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007_regional_elections">2007 regional elections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Alliance_(Colombia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2007 regional elections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Colombian_regional_election,_2007" class="mw-redirect" title="Colombian regional election, 2007">Colombian regional election, 2007</a></div>
<p>For the October 28, 2007 <a href="/wiki/Colombian_regional_elections,_2007" title="Colombian regional elections, 2007">Colombian regional elections</a> to elect <a href="/wiki/List_of_Colombian_Department_Governors" title="List of Colombian Department Governors">department governors</a>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_Colombian_Department_Assemblies" title="List of Colombian Department Assemblies">department assembly deputies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Colombia" title="Municipalities of Colombia">mayors and councils</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_Administrative_Juntas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Local Administrative Juntas (page does not exist)">Local Administrative Juntas</a> the party oddly won the governorships of <a href="/wiki/Cesar_Department" title="Cesar Department">Cesar</a> with candidate <a href="/wiki/Cristian_Moreno_Panezo" title="Cristian Moreno Panezo">Cristian Moreno Panezo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Boyac%C3%A1_Department" title="Boyacá Department">Boyacá</a> with candidate <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jos%C3%A9_Roso_Mill%C3%A1n&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="José Roso Millán (page does not exist)">José Roso Millán</a>. The party also obtained 23 Municipal mayors.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_congressional_elections">2010 congressional elections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Alliance_(Colombia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2010 congressional elections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Three independent former mayors of Bogota, <a href="/wiki/Luis_Eduardo_Garz%C3%B3n" title="Luis Eduardo Garzón">Luis Eduardo Garzón</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antanas_Mockus" title="Antanas Mockus">Antanas Mockus</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Pe%C3%B1alosa" title="Enrique Peñalosa">Enrique Peñalosa</a>, formed an alliance to choose an independent candidate for the presidency. However, they required a political structure. The ad-hoc coalition merged with the Centre Option Green Party, which changed its name to Green Party. Following this, the new party joined by many regional politicians.
</p><p>Mockus was elected candidate for the presidency in the Green Party's primary elections, held on March 14, 2010. On the same day, the party gained 5 seats in the Senate. Independent presidential candidate and former mayor of Medellín, <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Fajardo" title="Sergio Fajardo">Sergio Fajardo</a>, joined the Mockus campaign soon after and was chosen as the Green Party's Vice Presidential candidate.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_Presidential_Elections">2010 Presidential Elections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Alliance_(Colombia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010 Presidential Elections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Colombian_presidential_election,_2010" title="Colombian presidential election, 2010">Colombian presidential election, 2010</a></div>
<p>On May 30, the Colombian Green Party became the second political force as a result of the First Round of the <a href="/wiki/Colombian_presidential_election,_2010" title="Colombian presidential election, 2010">Presidential Election</a> with 21% of the electorate.
On June 21, they received 28% of the vote thereby losing the Presidential election to Juan Manuel Santos who achieved 69%.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
