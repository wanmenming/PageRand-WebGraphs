<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Green Party of Fiji</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<p>The <b>Green Party of Fiji</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political party</a> in <a href="/wiki/Fiji" title="Fiji">Fiji</a>.  The party was formed in November 2008 by former interim regime Cabinet Minister <a href="/wiki/Bernadette_Ganilau" title="Bernadette Ganilau">Bernadette Ganilau</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  Like other <a href="/wiki/Green_party" title="Green party">green parties</a>, the party followed principles of "<a href="/wiki/Ecological_wisdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Ecological wisdom">ecological wisdom</a>, social justice and non-violence, participatory democracy and sustainability".<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The party was registered with the Fiji Elections Office.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In January 2013 the military-backed regime promulgated new regulations governing the registration of political parties, requiring all parties to have at least 5,000 members.  All existing parties had to re-register under the new regulations.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  The party was not one of the two to re-register,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and as a result was wound up and its assets forfeited to the state.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
