<p>The <b>Green Movement</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hebrew_language" title="Hebrew language">Hebrew</a>: <span class="script-hebrew" style="font-size: 115%; font-family: &#39;Ezra SIL&#39;, &#39;Ezra SIL SR&#39;, &#39;Keter Aram Tsova&#39;, &#39;Taamey Ashkenaz&#39;, &#39;Taamey David CLM&#39;, &#39;Taamey Frank CLM&#39;, &#39;Frank Ruehl CLM&#39;, &#39;Keter YG&#39;, &#39;Shofar&#39;, &#39;David CLM&#39;, &#39;Hadasim CLM&#39;, &#39;Simple CLM&#39;, &#39;Nachlieli&#39;, &#39;SBL BibLit&#39;, &#39;SBL Hebrew&#39;, Cardo, Alef, &#39;Noto Serif Hebrew&#39;, &#39;Noto Sans Hebrew&#39;, &#39;David Libre&#39;, David, &#39;Times New Roman&#39;, Gisha, Arial, FreeSerif, FreeSans;" dir="rtl">הַתְּנוּעָה הַיְרוּקָה</span>&#x202c;, <i>HaTnuʿa HaYeruqa</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Social_movement" title="Social movement">social</a>-<a href="/wiki/Environmental_movement" title="Environmental movement">environmental</a> movement and <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Israel" title="List of political parties in Israel">political party</a> in <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-greener_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-greener-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Movement_(Israel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Green Movement was established in the summer 2008 by green activists and led by Eran Ben-Yemini and <a href="/wiki/Alon_Tal" title="Alon Tal">Alon Tal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-greener_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-greener-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> It formed as a political party and ran a joint list with <a href="/wiki/Meimad" title="Meimad">Meimad</a>, a dovish religious party, for the <a href="/wiki/Israeli_legislative_election,_2009" title="Israeli legislative election, 2009">2009 elections</a> after Meimad had ended its alliance with the <a href="/wiki/Israeli_Labor_Party" title="Israeli Labor Party">Labor Party</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Meimad leader <a href="/wiki/Michael_Melchior" title="Michael Melchior">Michael Melchior</a> headed its list and Ben-Yemini and Tal in second and third place; former <a href="/wiki/Shinui" title="Shinui">Shinui</a> MK <a href="/wiki/Meli_Polishook-Bloch" title="Meli Polishook-Bloch">Meli Polishook-Bloch</a> was also on its list in eleventh place.<sup id="cite_ref-K_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-K-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In addition to the environment, the electoral slate's platform addressed issues of education, social democracy, religious pluralism, and co-existence.<sup id="cite_ref-greener_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-greener-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The campaign downplayed religious issues and emphasized environmental questions.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The alliance failed to win any seats in the Knesset after failing to pass the <a href="/wiki/Election_threshold" title="Election threshold">election threshold</a>, receiving the largest number of votes of any party not to do so.
</p><p>It contested the <a href="/wiki/Israeli_legislative_election,_2013" title="Israeli legislative election, 2013">2013 elections</a> in an alliance with <a href="/wiki/Tzipi_Livni" title="Tzipi Livni">Tzipi Livni</a>'s party, <a href="/wiki/Hatnuah" title="Hatnuah">Hatnuah</a> or 'The Movement'. Party leader <a href="/wiki/Alon_Tal" title="Alon Tal">Alon Tal</a> was given the 13th spot on the party list. However, Hatnuah only won 6 mandates. Following the election, there was a new leadership elected, with <a href="/wiki/Yael_Cohen_Paran" title="Yael Cohen Paran">Yael Cohen Paran</a> replacing Tal as co-chair. 
</p><p>It ran in the <a href="/wiki/Israeli_legislative_election,_2015" title="Israeli legislative election, 2015">2015 elections</a> on a joint <a href="/wiki/Zionist_Union" title="Zionist Union">Zionist Union</a> list with <a href="/wiki/Hatnuah" title="Hatnuah">Hatnuah</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Israeli_Labor_Party" title="Israeli Labor Party">Israeli Labor Party</a>. <a href="/wiki/Tzipi_Livni" title="Tzipi Livni">Tzipi Livni</a> chose Green Movement co-chair <a href="/wiki/Yael_Cohen_Paran" title="Yael Cohen Paran">Yael Cohen Paran</a> for the 25th spot (reserved for Hatnuah members) on the list.<sup id="cite_ref-jpost_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jpost-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Paran missed out on election but got in following the resignation of another list member in November 2015.
</p><p>Within the Knesset, the Movement’s main focus is on toxic pollution in Haifa Bay and the affects on nearby neighbourhoods. Another focus is on air pollution. Paran is heading a parliamentary inquiry on renewable energy, pioneered a study on Israel becoming carbon free, and is introducing legislation to require solar systems in high-rise buildings.
</p>
