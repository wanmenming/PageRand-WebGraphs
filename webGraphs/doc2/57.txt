<p>The <b>Latvian Green Party</b> (<a href="/wiki/Latvian_language" title="Latvian language">Latvian</a>: <i lang="lv">Latvijas Zaļā partija</i>, LZP) is a <a href="/wiki/Green_politics" title="Green politics">green</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Latvia" title="List of political parties in Latvia">political party in Latvia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Nordsieck_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nordsieck-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It was founded on 13 January 1990 and is a member of the <a href="/wiki/European_Green_Party" title="European Green Party">European Green Party</a>. Unusually for a <a href="/wiki/Green_party" title="Green party">green party</a> in Europe, the LZP is generally considered a <a href="/wiki/Centre-right_politics" title="Centre-right politics">centre-right</a> party.<sup id="cite_ref-FIBS_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIBS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Nissinen_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nissinen-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Leading politicians of the party have often supported reactionary, nationalist, and anti-LGBT positions.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The party has only gained representation in the Saeima by forming <a href="/wiki/Electoral_alliance" title="Electoral alliance">electoral alliances</a> with other parties.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latvian_Green_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Council_of_the_Republic_of_Latvia" title="Supreme Council of the Republic of Latvia">Supreme Council of the Republic of Latvia</a> elected in 1990 contained seven Green delegates. After the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Latvia" title="Constitution of Latvia">Constitution of Latvia</a> was restored, following the collapse of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>, the election of the 5th <a href="/wiki/Saeima" title="Saeima">Saeima</a> (1993-1995) returned one Green deputy, <a href="/wiki/Anna_Seile" title="Anna Seile">Anna Seile</a>, on the list of the right-wing <a href="/wiki/Latvian_National_Independence_Movement" title="Latvian National Independence Movement">Latvian National Independence Movement</a>. In the 6th Saeima (1995-1998), there were four members: <a href="/wiki/Indulis_Emsis" title="Indulis Emsis">Indulis Emsis</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Guntis_Enins&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Guntis Enins (page does not exist)">Guntis Enins</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Janis_Kalvins&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Janis Kalvins (page does not exist)">Janis Kalvins</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Janis_Razna&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Janis Razna (page does not exist)">Janis Razna</a>.
</p><p>From <a href="/wiki/Gailis_cabinet" title="Gailis cabinet">1993</a> until <a href="/wiki/Krasts_cabinet" title="Krasts cabinet">1998</a>, the Greens were part of the governing coalition with Indulis Emsis as Minister of State for Environmental Protection. The party lost its representation in the election to the 7th Saeima (1998–2002).
</p><p>For the <a href="/wiki/Latvian_parliamentary_election,_2002" title="Latvian parliamentary election, 2002">2002 parliamentary election</a>, the party formed the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_Greens_and_Farmers" title="Union of Greens and Farmers">Union of Greens and Farmers</a> (<i>ZZS</i>) coalition with the <a href="/wiki/Latvian_Farmers%27_Union" title="Latvian Farmers&#39; Union">Latvian Farmers' Union</a>. Three members of the Green party were elected: <a href="/wiki/Indulis_Emsis" title="Indulis Emsis">Indulis Emsis</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Arvids_Ulme&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Arvids Ulme (page does not exist)">Arvids Ulme</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Leopolds_Ozolins&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Leopolds Ozolins (page does not exist)">Leopolds Ozolins</a>. The ZZS joined a four-party center-right coalition government and was represented with three ministers, one of them from the Green party, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Minister_for_the_Environment_of_Latvia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Minister for the Environment of Latvia (page does not exist)">Minister for the Environment</a> <a href="/wiki/Raimonds_Vejonis" class="mw-redirect" title="Raimonds Vejonis">Raimonds Vejonis</a>.
</p><p>In February 2004, after the breakdown of the four-party government, Indulis Emsis was appointed to form a new government and became the first <a href="/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">head of government</a> of a country anywhere in the world from a <a href="/wiki/Green_party" title="Green party">Green party</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> His minority government was forced to resign in December of the same year. A new coalition government led by the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Party_(Latvia)" title="People&#39;s Party (Latvia)">People’s Party</a> took office, in which the party was again represented as part of the ZZS.
</p><p>For the <a href="/wiki/Latvian_parliamentary_election,_2006" title="Latvian parliamentary election, 2006">2006 parliamentary election</a>, the party remained part of the ZZS, and won four seats. The party remained part of the centre-right coalition government along with the People’s Party, <a href="/wiki/Latvia%27s_First_Party/Latvian_Way" title="Latvia&#39;s First Party/Latvian Way">Latvia's First Party/Latvian Way</a>, and the Nationalist <a href="/wiki/For_Fatherland_and_Freedom" class="mw-redirect" title="For Fatherland and Freedom">For Fatherland and Freedom</a>. Party chairman and former prime minister Indulis Emsis became <a href="/wiki/Speaker_of_the_Saeima" title="Speaker of the Saeima">Speaker of the Saeima</a> from November 2006 until September 2007.
</p>
