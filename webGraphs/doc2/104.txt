<p>The <b>Green Liberal Party of Switzerland</b> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <i lang="de">Grünliberale Partei der Schweiz</i>, glp; <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i lang="fr">Parti vert'libéral</i>, pvl), abbreviated to <b>glp</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Centrism" title="Centrism">centrist</a> <a href="/wiki/Green_liberalism" title="Green liberalism">green-liberal</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Switzerland" title="List of political parties in Switzerland">political party</a> in <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>.  Founded in 2007, the party holds seven seats in the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Assembly_of_Switzerland" class="mw-redirect" title="Federal Assembly of Switzerland">Federal Assembly</a> as of 2017.
</p><p>The party was formed on 19 July 2007 by four <a href="/wiki/Cantons_of_Switzerland" title="Cantons of Switzerland">cantonal</a> branches of the <a href="/wiki/Green_Party_of_Switzerland" title="Green Party of Switzerland">Green Party</a>. Contesting the <a href="/wiki/Swiss_federal_election,_2007" title="Swiss federal election, 2007">election in October 2007</a> in <a href="/wiki/Canton_of_St._Gallen" title="Canton of St. Gallen">St. Gallen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canton_of_Zurich" class="mw-redirect" title="Canton of Zurich">Zurich</a>, the party won three seats in the <a href="/wiki/National_Council_of_Switzerland" class="mw-redirect" title="National Council of Switzerland">National Council</a>.  A month later, the party won a seat in the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_States_of_Switzerland" class="mw-redirect" title="Council of States of Switzerland">Council of States</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Verena_Diener" title="Verena Diener">Verena Diener</a> representing Zurich. The party has since expanded across Switzerland, and holds seats in thirteen <a href="/wiki/List_of_cantonal_legislatures_of_Switzerland" title="List of cantonal legislatures of Switzerland">cantonal legislatures</a> in <a href="/wiki/German-speaking_Switzerland" title="German-speaking Switzerland">German-speaking Switzerland</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Romandy" title="Romandy">Romandy</a>. The party reached 5.4% at the <a href="/wiki/Swiss_federal_election,_2011" title="Swiss federal election, 2011">2011 federal election</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> increasing the number of Members of the National Council from 3 to 12, but suffered a setback in <a href="/wiki/Swiss_federal_election,_2015" title="Swiss federal election, 2015">2015</a> retreating to 7 seats with 4,6% of the national vote.<sup id="cite_ref-NR2015_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NR2015-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Green Liberals are a party of the political <a href="/wiki/Centrism" title="Centrism">centre</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> as opposed to the left Green Party.  They seek to combine liberalism on civil liberties and moderate <a href="/wiki/Economic_liberalism" title="Economic liberalism">economic liberalism</a> with environmental <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GLPWWSF_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GLPWWSF-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The party has an autonomous parliamentary group in the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Assembly_of_Switzerland" class="mw-redirect" title="Federal Assembly of Switzerland">Federal Assembly of Switzerland</a> since the <a href="/wiki/Swiss_federal_election,_2011" title="Swiss federal election, 2011">2011 federal election</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Factions_of_the_49th_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Factions_of_the_49th-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Liberal_Party_of_Switzerland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The party was founded on 19 July 2007 by four <a href="/wiki/Cantons_of_Switzerland" title="Cantons of Switzerland">cantonal</a> parties of the same name that had seceded from the Green Party.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>  These branches were in <a href="/wiki/Basel-Landschaft" class="mw-redirect" title="Basel-Landschaft">Basel-Landschaft</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canton_of_Bern" title="Canton of Bern">Bern</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canton_of_St._Gallen" title="Canton of St. Gallen">St. Gallen</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Canton_of_Zurich" class="mw-redirect" title="Canton of Zurich">Zurich</a>.
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/Swiss_federal_election,_2007" title="Swiss federal election, 2007">2007 election</a> to the <a href="/wiki/National_Council_of_Switzerland" class="mw-redirect" title="National Council of Switzerland">National Council</a> on 22 October 2007, the party ran in Zurich and St. Gallen.<sup id="cite_ref-Paolo_Dardanelli_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Paolo_Dardanelli-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>  Despite being limited to only two cantons, the party won 1.4% of the popular vote nationwide and 3 out of 200 seats. In Zurich, they won 7% of the vote.<sup id="cite_ref-Paolo_Dardanelli_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Paolo_Dardanelli-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> One of these three had been a National Councillor for the Green Party in the previous Parliament.
</p><p>A month later, it won a seat in the <a href="/wiki/Swiss_Council_of_States" class="mw-redirect" title="Swiss Council of States">Council of States</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Verena_Diener" title="Verena Diener">Verena Diener</a> representing Zurich.  Along with the first appearance of the Green Party, this was the first time a minor party had won representation in the Council of States since 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>  When the Federal Assembly convened, the glp joined the <a href="/wiki/Christian_Democrats/EPP/glp_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Christian Democrats/EPP/glp Group">Christian Democrats/EPP/glp Group</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Paolo_Dardanelli_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Paolo_Dardanelli-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> making it the second-largest group, behind the <a href="/wiki/Swiss_People%27s_Party" title="Swiss People&#39;s Party">Swiss People's Party</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Factions_of_the_48th_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Factions_of_the_48th-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In 2010 the party got an additional seat in the Council of States with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Markus_Stadler&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Markus Stadler (page does not exist)">Markus Stadler</a> from <a href="/wiki/Canton_of_Uri" title="Canton of Uri">Uri</a>.
</p><p>There are now eighteen cantonal parties: Zurich, St. Gallen, Basel-Country, Bern, <a href="/wiki/Canton_of_Zug" title="Canton of Zug">Zug</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thurgau" class="mw-redirect" title="Thurgau">Thurgau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Basel-Stadt" class="mw-redirect" title="Basel-Stadt">Basel-Stadt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Graub%C3%BCnden" class="mw-redirect" title="Graubünden">Graubünden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canton_of_Lucerne" title="Canton of Lucerne">Lucerne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aargau" class="mw-redirect" title="Aargau">Aargau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canton_of_Solothurn" title="Canton of Solothurn">Solothurn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canton_of_Fribourg" title="Canton of Fribourg">Fribourg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vaud" class="mw-redirect" title="Vaud">Vaud</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canton_of_Geneva" title="Canton of Geneva">Geneva</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canton_of_Neuch%C3%A2tel" title="Canton of Neuchâtel">Neuchâtel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canton_of_Glarus" title="Canton of Glarus">Glarus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canton_of_Schwyz" title="Canton of Schwyz">Schwyz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canton_of_Schaffhausen" title="Canton of Schaffhausen">Schaffhausen</a>.
</p>
