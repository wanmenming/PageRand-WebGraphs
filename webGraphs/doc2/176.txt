<p>The <b>Green Party of Bolivia</b> (<b>PVB</b>, <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Partido Verde de Bolivia</i>) is a political party in <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a>, which has a <a href="/wiki/Green_politics" title="Green politics">green</a> political orientation. Founded in 2007, it participated in the <a href="/wiki/Bolivian_general_election,_2014" title="Bolivian general election, 2014">2014 general elections</a>, in opposition to the reigning President <a href="/wiki/Evo_Morales" title="Evo Morales">Evo Morales</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Movement_for_Socialism_(Bolivia)" title="Movement for Socialism (Bolivia)">Movement for Socialism</a>. The party is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Global_Greens" title="Global Greens">Global Greens</a>, an international network of <a href="/wiki/Green_party" title="Green party">green parties</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and an observer of the <a href="/wiki/Federation_of_the_Green_Parties_of_the_Americas" title="Federation of the Green Parties of the Americas">Federation of the Green Parties of the Americas</a>, a regional network of the same.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_of_Bolivia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <i>Partido Verde de Bolivia</i> was founded on 9 August 2007, after a few years of work in that direction. The newly formed party joined the Global Greens in 2008, attending the alliance's Second World Congress that year in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Between 24 and 27 November 2013 the party hosted the annual meeting of the Global Greens Coordination (GGC) in the Bolivian capital of <a href="/wiki/La_Paz" title="La Paz">La Paz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> During this meeting, the PVB announced its intention to run jointly with the <a href="/wiki/National_Council_of_Ayllus_and_Markas_of_Qullasuyu" title="National Council of Ayllus and Markas of Qullasuyu">National Council of Ayllus and Markas of Qullasuyu</a> for the October <a href="/wiki/Bolivian_general_election,_2014" title="Bolivian general election, 2014">2014 general elections</a> the following year.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 26, 2014, the PVB finalized its election candidates. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Vargas_Mosua" title="Fernando Vargas Mosua">Fernando Vargas Mosua</a>, leader of the indigenous communities of the <a href="/wiki/Isiboro_S%C3%A9cure_National_Park_and_Indigenous_Territory" title="Isiboro Sécure National Park and Indigenous Territory">Isiboro Sécure National Park and Indigenous Territory</a>, was to be the party's candidate for president, while the party leader and founder Margot Soria Saravia took the role of vice presidential candidate.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> With 24,685 out of 27,403 polling stations having reported in the Green Party had gained 126,958 votes in the presidential elections, a total of 2.79%.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
