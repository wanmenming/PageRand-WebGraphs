<p>The <b>Green Party of Lebanon</b> (<a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span lang="ar" dir="rtl"><b>حزب الخضر اللبناني</b></span>&#8206; <i>ĥizb-al-khodor-al-lubnanī</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Green_politics" title="Green politics">green</a> political party. Founded in August 2008 by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Philip_Skaf&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Philip Skaf (page does not exist)">Philip Skaf</a>, the green party advocates environmental protection, sustainable development, and human rights in Lebanon. It is the first Lebanese party to focus primarily on <a href="/wiki/Green_politics" title="Green politics">Green politics</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_of_Lebanon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Environmental concerns in <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a> have been overshadowed by the <a href="/wiki/Sect" title="Sect">sectarian</a> <a href="/wiki/Nepotism" title="Nepotism">nepotist</a> <a href="/wiki/Political_system" title="Political system">political system</a> and consigned to the bottom of the <a href="/wiki/Political_agenda" title="Political agenda">political agenda</a>. This is despite the fact that Lebanon's <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourism industry</a> - a key part of its <a href="/wiki/Economy" title="Economy">economy</a> - relies heavily on the <a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">green spaces</a> and <a href="/wiki/Woodland" title="Woodland">woodlands</a> that distinguish Lebanon from <a href="/wiki/Arab_World" class="mw-redirect" title="Arab World">neighboring countries</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-TDS_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TDS-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Green Party of Lebanon was founded on 20 August 2008 at a conference in Beirut's Monroe Hotel. The three-hour conference, which assembled 65 members of the Lebanese elite, presented the party's charter and political and economic work plan followed by the election of its 20-member executive board. The executive board elected Philip Skaf, chief executive and creative officer at <a href="/wiki/Grey_Global_Group" title="Grey Global Group">Grey Worldwide MENA</a>, an advertising agency,<sup id="cite_ref-GME_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GME-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  as party president.<sup id="cite_ref-AK_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AK-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPL_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPL-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2009, the Green Party declared a state of <a href="/wiki/Environmental_emergency" title="Environmental emergency">environmental emergency</a> and announced the launching of an "environmental pact". Skaf declared that the Party would not run candidates in the <a href="/wiki/Lebanese_general_election,_2009" title="Lebanese general election, 2009">2009 Lebanese general election</a> but stressed that the document will be presented to all Lebanese political parties in order for them to adopt its goals.<sup id="cite_ref-PA_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LIC_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LIC-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>With electing Nada Zaarour as president in 2011 the Green Party is the first party in Lebanon with a female leader.
</p>
