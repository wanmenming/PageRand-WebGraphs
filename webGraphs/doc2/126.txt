<p>The <b>Federation of Green Lists</b> (<a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>: <i lang="it">Federazione delle Liste Verdi</i>)<sup id="cite_ref-SchreursPapadakis2007_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SchreursPapadakis2007-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> or <b>Green Lists</b> (<i>Liste Verdi</i>, LV) was a <a href="/wiki/Green_politics" title="Green politics">green</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Italy" title="List of political parties in Italy">political party in Italy</a>. Its members included <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Mattioli" title="Gianni Mattioli">Gianni Mattioli</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lino_De_Benetti&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lino De Benetti (page does not exist)">Lino De Benetti</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gianfranco_Amendola&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gianfranco Amendola (page does not exist)">Gianfranco Amendola</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Langer" title="Alexander Langer">Alexander Langer</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Enrico_Falqui&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Enrico Falqui (page does not exist)">Enrico Falqui</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sauro_Turroni&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sauro Turroni (page does not exist)">Sauro Turroni</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_Pecoraro_Scanio" title="Alfonso Pecoraro Scanio">Alfonso Pecoraro Scanio</a>. The Green Lists used the <a href="/wiki/Smiling_Sun" title="Smiling Sun">Smiling Sun</a> symbol of the <a href="/wiki/Anti-nuclear_movement" title="Anti-nuclear movement">anti-nuclear movement</a>, which was inherited by its successor party, the <a href="/wiki/Federation_of_the_Greens" title="Federation of the Greens">Federation of the Greens</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Lists&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It was founded on 16 November 1986. The party was formed as a national organisation of Green Lists which had first contested regional elections in 1985, initially being joined by seventy local lists.<sup id="cite_ref-Biorcio2012_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Biorcio2012-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Italian_general_election,_1987" title="Italian general election, 1987">1987 general election</a>, the Green Lists received 2.5% for the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_(Italy)" title="Chamber of Deputies (Italy)">Chamber</a>, returning thirteen deputies as well as two senators in the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_the_Republic_(Italy)" title="Senate of the Republic (Italy)">Senate</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Biorcio2012_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Biorcio2012-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The party took part in the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament_election,_1989_(Italy)" title="European Parliament election, 1989 (Italy)">1989 European Parliamentary elections</a>, receiving 3.8% of the vote, electing 3 MEPs.<sup id="cite_ref-Ely1998_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ely1998-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> A rival ecologist list, the <a href="/wiki/Rainbow_Greens" title="Rainbow Greens">Rainbow Greens</a>, received 2.4% in the same election.<sup id="cite_ref-Moliterno2002_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Moliterno2002-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 1990 the party merged with the Rainbow Greens to form the <a href="/wiki/Federation_of_the_Greens" title="Federation of the Greens">Federation of the Greens</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Biorcio2016_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Biorcio2016-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
