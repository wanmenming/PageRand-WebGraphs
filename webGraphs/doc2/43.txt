<p>The <b>Green Party</b> (<a href="/wiki/Czech_language" title="Czech language">Czech</a>: <i lang="cs">Strana zelených</i>) is a political party in the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a>.
</p><p>The party held a single seat (<a href="/wiki/Jarom%C3%ADr_%C5%A0t%C4%9Btina" title="Jaromír Štětina">Jaromír Štětina</a>) in the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_the_Czech_Republic" title="Senate of the Czech Republic">Senate</a> (upper house of the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_Czech_Republic" title="Parliament of the Czech Republic">Parliament of the Czech Republic</a>) between 2004 and 2010. In the <a href="/wiki/Czech_legislative_election,_2006" title="Czech legislative election, 2006">2006 legislative election</a> the party received 6.3% of the vote and won six seats in the lower house – the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_of_the_Czech_Republic" title="Chamber of Deputies of the Czech Republic">Chamber of Deputies</a>. This subsequently led to taking part in the governing coalition, together with the <a href="/wiki/Civic_Democratic_Party_(Czech_Republic)" title="Civic Democratic Party (Czech Republic)">Civic Democrats</a> (ODS) and the <a href="/wiki/Christian_Democratic_Union_-_Czechoslovak_People%27s_Party" class="mw-redirect" title="Christian Democratic Union - Czechoslovak People&#39;s Party">Christian Democrats</a> (KDU–ČSL) from January 2007 to March 2009 (for more details see <a href="/wiki/Mirek_Topol%C3%A1nek%27s_Second_Cabinet" title="Mirek Topolánek&#39;s Second Cabinet">Mirek Topolánek's Second Cabinet</a>). However the Green Party was unable to repeat its success in <a href="/wiki/Czech_legislative_election,_2010" title="Czech legislative election, 2010">2010 elections</a>, losing all seats in both chambers.
</p>
