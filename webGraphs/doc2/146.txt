<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Environmental_movement" title="Environmental movement">environmental movement</a></b> in <b><a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a></b> traces its history from the early beginnings of conservation, to the rise of radicalism and activism amongst local ecologists. Before the <a href="/wiki/Chernobyl_disaster" title="Chernobyl disaster">Chernobyl disaster</a> and the fall of the <a href="/wiki/Berlin_Wall" title="Berlin Wall">Berlin Wall</a>, there were very few green activist groups in the country. <a href="/wiki/Koeberg_Alert" title="Koeberg Alert">Koeberg Alert</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Dolphin_Action_and_Protection_Group" title="Dolphin Action and Protection Group">Dolphin Action and Protection Group</a> are probably two of the oldest post-conservation groups.
</p><p>While most early conservationists, with few exceptions, implicated themselves in the <a href="/wiki/History_of_South_Africa_in_the_apartheid_era" class="mw-redirect" title="History of South Africa in the apartheid era">apartheid</a> system<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>, groups such as <a href="/wiki/Earthlife_Africa" title="Earthlife Africa">Earthlife Africa</a> were consciously aware of their role as nurturers of freedom and human rights as well as the rights of the earth and animal kingdom. The <a href="/wiki/Cape_Town_Ecology_Group" title="Cape Town Ecology Group">Cape Town Ecology Group</a> for example, openly campaigned for political freedoms with a platform that "ecologised politics and politicised ecology".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>During apartheid while political parties were banned, environmental groups served as an outlet for activism and political expression. In <a href="/wiki/Durban" title="Durban">Durban</a>, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_Justice_Network&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Environmental Justice Network (page does not exist)">Environmental Justice Network</a>, sprung up alongside ant-apartheid issues that affected the environment as well as the rights of workers. While many radical environmentalists were later absorbed into the governing <a href="/wiki/African_National_Congress" title="African National Congress">African National Congress</a>, some toyed with the formation of a political party that would represent green interests.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Timeline">Timeline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_movement_in_South_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Timeline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Apartheid_Era">Apartheid Era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_movement_in_South_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Apartheid Era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>1926: <a href="/wiki/WESSA" title="WESSA">Wildlife Society of South Africa</a> founded;</li>
<li>1973: <a href="/wiki/Endangered_Wildlife_Trust" title="Endangered Wildlife Trust">Endangered Wildlife Trust</a> founded;</li>
<li>1977: The Dolphin Action &amp; Protection Group founded with the motto and policy that 'DOLPHINS SHOULD BE FREE'.</li>
<li>1983: <a href="/wiki/Koeberg_Alert" title="Koeberg Alert">Koeberg Alert</a> founded;</li>
<li>1986: <a href="/wiki/Chernobyl" title="Chernobyl">Chernobyl</a> disaster</li>
<li>1987: <a href="/wiki/Cape_Town_Ecology_Group" title="Cape Town Ecology Group">Cape Town Ecology Group</a> founded; with motto: 'FREE THE HUMANS'</li>
<li>1988: <a href="/wiki/Earthlife_Africa" title="Earthlife Africa">Earthlife Africa</a> formed;</li>
<li>1989: <a href="/wiki/Earthlife_Africa" title="Earthlife Africa">Earthlife Africa</a> exposes mercury poisoning of workers at Thor Chemicals;<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kagenna_Magazine" title="Kagenna Magazine">Kagenna Magazine</a> is published</li>
<li>1990: A fishing industry campaign by the <a href="/wiki/Food_and_Allied_Workers_Union" title="Food and Allied Workers Union">Food and Allied Workers Union</a> links workers issues to the environment;</li>
<li>1991: <a href="/wiki/South_African_National_Conference_on_Environment_and_Development" title="South African National Conference on Environment and Development">First National Conference on Environment and Development</a>; Environmental Monitoring Group releases a document "Towards Sustainable Development in South Africa";<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> General <a href="/wiki/Magnus_Malan" title="Magnus Malan">Magnus Malan</a> takes over as new Minister of Water Affairs and Forestry; Bev Geach of the Weekly Mail publishes The Green Pages, a directory of environmental groups</li>
<li>1992: Earthlife Africa pressurizes the government for an inquiry into asbestos related deaths. Environmental Justice Networking Forum (EJNF) formed at an ELA conference.</li>
<li>1993: Group for Environmental Monitoring (GEM) founded.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-Apartheid_Era">Post-Apartheid Era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_movement_in_South_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Post-Apartheid Era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>1994: After South Africa's first democratic election, environmental rights submitted for debate to the Constitutional Assembly.</li>
<li>1995: <a href="/wiki/EThekwini_ECOPEACE" class="mw-redirect" title="EThekwini ECOPEACE">eThekwini ECOPEACE</a> founded</li>
<li>1996: South Africa's Bill of Rights proclaims: "Everyone has the right to an environment that is not harmful to their health or well-being."</li>
<li>1997: The ANC government moves to provide lead-free petrol as one of its first pro-environment policies</li>
<li>1998: The Truth Commission hears about asbestos-related deaths from mining</li>
<li>1999: Groundwork (GW), a non-profit, environmental justice service and development organization founded by 3 ex-EJNF activists</li>
<li>2000: South Durban Community Environmental Alliance (SDCEA)formed; <a href="/wiki/EThekwini_ECOPEACE" class="mw-redirect" title="EThekwini ECOPEACE">eThekwini ECOPEACE</a> wins one seat in the eThekwini Municipal Council, the first time a Green Party of any sort in South Africa has won at the polls</li>
<li>2002: Rio+10 <a href="/wiki/World_Summit_on_Sustainable_Development" class="mw-redirect" title="World Summit on Sustainable Development">World Summit on Sustainable Development</a> held in Johannesburg; Earthlife launches the People’s Environmental Centre, the Greenhouse.</li>
<li>2003: Asbestos Relief Trust (ART) set up, and the Kgalagadi Relief Trust (KRT), both of which evaluate claims and provide compensation for qualified claimants. A media statement, indicates that the ban on the use of asbestos and asbestos-related materials was "well overdue." National Energy Caucus founded.</li>
<li>2004: <a href="/wiki/Marthinus_van_Schalkwyk" title="Marthinus van Schalkwyk">Marthinus van Schalkwyk</a> appointed as <a href="/wiki/South_African_Ministry_of_Environmental_Affairs_and_Tourism" class="mw-redirect" title="South African Ministry of Environmental Affairs and Tourism">Minister of Environmental Affairs and Tourism</a></li>
<li>2006: <a href="/wiki/Eskom" title="Eskom">Eskom</a>, South Africa's national energy utility issues energy-saving lightbulbs to consumers as part of a "demand-side" energy-reduction campaign.
<ul><li>Dept of Environmental Affairs and Tourism holds hearings on nuclear power. First evidence of contamination and worker-related deaths caused b exposure to radiation.</li></ul></li>
<li>2009: South Africa participates in the Copenhagen Climate Change round.</li>
<li>2010: SA Government announces mothballing of <a href="/wiki/PBMR" class="mw-redirect" title="PBMR">PBMR</a></li>
<li>2011: South Africa hosts <a href="/wiki/COP17" class="mw-redirect" title="COP17">COP17</a> in Durban, a new framework emerges.</li>
<li>2015: South Africa hosts <a href="/w/index.php?title=South_African_International_Renewable_Energy_Conference&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="South African International Renewable Energy Conference (page does not exist)">South African International Renewable Energy Conference</a>.</li></ul>
