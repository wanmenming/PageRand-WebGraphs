<p>The <b>Green Party of Georgia</b> (<a href="/wiki/Georgian_language" title="Georgian language">Georgian</a>&#58; <span lang="ka" title="Georgian language text">საქართველოს მწვანეთა პარტია</span>, <i>Sak’art’velos mtsvanet’a partia</i>) is a political party in <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a>. It is led by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gia_Gachechiladze&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gia Gachechiladze (page does not exist)">Gia (Giorgi) Gachechiladze</a>.
</p><p>Its success has varied - having 11 out of 235 members of parliament in 1992 and having been part of the governing coalition in 1995. When it stood independently in 1999 it only received 0.55% of the vote. It stood as part of <a href="/wiki/Eduard_Shevardnadze" title="Eduard Shevardnadze">Eduard Shevardnadze</a>'s coalition in the annulled 2003 <a href="/wiki/Georgian_legislative_election,_2003" class="mw-redirect" title="Georgian legislative election, 2003">elections</a>.
It believes that the <a href="/wiki/Georgian_legislative_election,_2003" class="mw-redirect" title="Georgian legislative election, 2003">2003 elections</a> were "the most transparent process in the history of Georgia".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
