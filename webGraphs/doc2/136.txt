<p>The <b>Greens-European Free Alliance</b> (<b>Greens-EFA</b>) is the <a href="/wiki/Political_groups_of_the_European_Parliament" title="Political groups of the European Parliament">political group</a> in the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament" title="European Parliament">European Parliament</a> containing <a href="/wiki/Green_politics" title="Green politics">green</a>, <a href="/wiki/Regionalism_(politics)" title="Regionalism (politics)">regionalist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Left-wing_nationalist" class="mw-redirect" title="Left-wing nationalist">left-wing nationalist</a> political parties.<sup id="cite_ref-PetersonShackleton2012_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PetersonShackleton2012-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Staab2011_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Staab2011-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Trechsel2013_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Trechsel2013-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The group was formed following the  <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament_election,_1999" title="European Parliament election, 1999">1999 European elections</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Fifth_European_Parliament" title="Fifth European Parliament">5th European Parliament</a>.
</p><p>The Greens/EFA group consists of three distinct <a href="/wiki/European_political_party" title="European political party">European political parties</a> – the larger <a href="/wiki/European_Green_Party" title="European Green Party">European Green Party</a> (EGP) and the <a href="/wiki/European_Free_Alliance" title="European Free Alliance">European Free Alliance</a> (EFA),<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and the smaller <a href="/wiki/European_Pirate_Party" title="European Pirate Party">European Pirate Party</a>. The EFA consists of parties representing <a href="/wiki/Stateless_nations" class="mw-redirect" title="Stateless nations">stateless nations</a>, <a href="/wiki/Regionalism_(politics)" title="Regionalism (politics)">regionalist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Political_parties_of_minorities" title="Political parties of minorities">minority political</a> interests. The Alliance has generally limited its membership to <a href="/wiki/Progressivism" title="Progressivism">progressive</a> parties.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> These European parties are joined by MEPs from non-aligned national parties, which have included the Dutch <a href="/wiki/Europe_Transparent" title="Europe Transparent">Europe Transparent</a> (2004–2009) and the Swedish <a href="/wiki/Pirate_Party_(Sweden)" title="Pirate Party (Sweden)">Pirate Party</a> (2009–2014).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
