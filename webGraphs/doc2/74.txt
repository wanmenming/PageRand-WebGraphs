<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">The People &amp; Planet Green League</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>
				<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>The People &amp; Planet Green League</b> is the only comprehensive and independent ranking of United Kingdom <a href="/wiki/Universities" class="mw-redirect" title="Universities">universities</a> by environmental and ethical performance and practice.<sup id="cite_ref-greenleague_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-greenleague-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is compiled by the student campaign group <a href="/wiki/People_%26_Planet" title="People &amp; Planet">People &amp; Planet</a>. From 2007 to 2010 the Green League was published annually in the <i><a href="/wiki/Times_Higher_Education_Supplement" class="mw-redirect" title="Times Higher Education Supplement">Times Higher Education Supplement</a></i>, but since 2011 it has been published in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_People_%26_Planet_Green_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The People &amp; Planet Green League was first published in 2007, as a way of driving forward environmental performance within the university sector. The People &amp; Planet Green League publicly benchmarks the sector's green credentials by combining universities' estates performance data with information about their environmental policies and management practices.
</p><p>It initially scored UK universities on four key institutional factors needed to drive forward significant and sustained improvement in environmental performance, as highlighted by the Going Green report.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> These criteria were:
</p>
<ul><li>The active, public support of senior university management (in particular the vice-chancellor or principal) for a programme of environmental performance improvement.</li>
<li>Full-time staff dedicated to environmental management.</li>
<li>A comprehensive review to investigate all the environmental impacts of the institution.</li>
<li>A written, publicly available environmental policy.</li></ul>
<p>Published each year in affiliation with a national newspaper, the People &amp; Planet Green League aims to boost the national profile of environmental management in higher education by celebrating and sharing best practice and exposing inaction.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Methodology">Methodology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_People_%26_Planet_Green_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Methodology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The People &amp; Planet Green League adapts and modifies its methodology each year. The aim of this process is to respond to new environmental concerns, push transition further and incorporate feedback and criticism from previous years to improve the accuracy and transparency of the Green League.
</p><p>In 2012, the Green League measured universities according to 13 key indicators:
</p>
<ul><li>whether or not the institution has a publicly available environmental policy</li>
<li>the number of full-time equivalent (FTE) Environmental Management posts per 5,000 FTE students</li>
<li>environmental auditing</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ethical_investment" class="mw-redirect" title="Ethical investment">ethical investment</a></li>
<li>carbon management</li>
<li>ethical procurement and <a href="/wiki/Fairtrade" class="mw-redirect" title="Fairtrade">Fairtrade</a> status</li>
<li>sustainable food</li>
<li>staff and student engagement</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a> in the curriculum</li>
<li>energy sources</li>
<li>waste and <a href="/wiki/Recycling" title="Recycling">recycling</a></li>
<li>reduction in <a href="/wiki/Carbon_emissions" class="mw-redirect" title="Carbon emissions">carbon emissions</a></li>
<li>water-use reduction</li></ul>
<p>Universities are awarded a 'First', '2:1', '2:2' or 'Third' rating dependent upon performance.
</p>

<h3><span id="Awards_.26_response"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_&amp;_response">Awards &amp; response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_People_%26_Planet_Green_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Awards &amp; response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2012, the Green League was shortlisted for a Green Gown award.
</p><p>The Green League 2007 received critical acclaim following its publication. The <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Fund_for_Nature" title="World Wide Fund for Nature">World Wide Fund for Nature</a> (WWF) awarded The People &amp; Planet Green League with a <a href="/wiki/British_Environment_and_Media_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="British Environment and Media Award">British Environment and Media Award</a> for Best Campaign. The WWF said of The Green League 2007 that: "It succeeded in dragging environmental issues from the fringes and making them a central concern for many Vice Chancellors."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>
The Green League received praise from <a href="/wiki/Chris_Huhne" title="Chris Huhne">Chris Huhne</a> when <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Energy_and_Climate_Change" title="Secretary of State for Energy and Climate Change">Secretary of State for Energy and Climate Change</a>:</p><blockquote><p>The Green League throws a spotlight onto the work being done in universities to cut emissions, and I hope it will encourage universities and students to redouble their efforts in the fight against climate change. It is the students in universities today who will be carrying on the work to build the low-carbon Britain of tomorrow, and it is vital that universities and Government set a good example.<sup id="cite_ref-greenleague_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-greenleague-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticisms">Criticisms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_People_%26_Planet_Green_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Criticisms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The methodology has been criticised for not fully incorporating all factors that can contribute to an institution's sustainability; for instance schemes soon to be implemented.
</p><p>The ranking also did not incorporate the amount of Environmental Research and Teaching, which while not directly contributing to any reduction in the university's <a href="/wiki/Carbon_footprint" title="Carbon footprint">carbon footprint</a>, does serve to educate the next generation, and may reveal a new way to increase sustainability. However, a high level of environment research does not seem to correlate with a sustainable university (for instance, <a href="/wiki/Aberystwyth_University" title="Aberystwyth University">Aberystwyth University</a>, which runs a number of courses on Environmental Management,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> but gained only 97th position in the Green League). The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Greenwich" title="University of Greenwich">University of Greenwich</a> is another institution which believed that the criteria for marking was too limited, and following several criticms<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Green League acknowledged that it needed to expand its indicators, which it has done in the following years.
</p><p>Another criticism is that much of the information is reliant on the institutions faithfulness; People and Planet did not have the resources to mount an independent audit.
</p><p>These criticisms have largely been addressed by People and Planet, and this can be seen in the amendments which have been made in the measuring criteria which now includes many new areas of assessment which do address other areas of a universities environment.
</p>
