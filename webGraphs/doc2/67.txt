<p>The <b>Green Party of Nova Scotia</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Green_politics" title="Green politics">green</a> <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political party</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_province" class="mw-redirect" title="Canadian province">Canadian province</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>. It received official party status in the province in April 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The party has not won any seats in the <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia_House_of_Assembly" title="Nova Scotia House of Assembly">Nova Scotia House of Assembly</a>.
</p><p>As of November 2015 it emphasized that "the Green Party embraces the <i>Environmental Goals and Sustainable Prosperity Act (Nova Scotia)</i> <sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and the Nova Scotia <a href="/wiki/Genuine_Progress_Index" class="mw-redirect" title="Genuine Progress Index">Genuine Progress Index</a> (GPI)..."<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> - <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://greenparty.ns.ca/">[5]</a>.  The GPNS executive is organized regionally.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Leadership">Leadership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_of_Nova_Scotia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Leadership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Nick_Wright_(politician)" title="Nick Wright (politician)">Nick Wright</a> of Halifax, Nova Scotia won its first contested leadership nomination race over <a href="/wiki/Green_Party_of_Canada" title="Green Party of Canada">Green Party of Canada</a> candidate and organizer Sheila Richardson of Wolfville, Nova Scotia.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2007, Wright was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Ken_McGowan" title="Ken McGowan">Ken McGowan</a>, but resigned in protest with both his deputies in a constitutional struggle over  unelected rival Ellen Durkee's presence on the executive - to be replaced by Durkee as interim leader.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Ryan_Watson_(politician)" title="Ryan Watson (politician)">Ryan Watson</a> was elected Leader of the Green Party of Nova Scotia on Sunday June 29, 2008. Watson, from Halifax, was unopposed and contested the <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia_general_election,_2009" title="Nova Scotia general election, 2009">Nova Scotia general election, 2009</a>.  The party failed to file financial paperwork with Elections Nova Scotia by the April deadline, but when Watson resigned he denied this was the reason.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 31, 2009, John Percy was announced as the new leader of the Green Party of Nova Scotia following a leadership contest against Sebastian Ronin.  Dawna Toews was Percy's running mate and thus became Deputy Leader of the party.  However, Toews moved to Ontario in early 2010 and thus resigned her position. Kris MacLellan was chosen as Deputy Leader by Executive Committee vote in October 2011, prior to the AGM. Percy made a point of emphasizing politicians of other political stripes he admired including <a href="/wiki/Joe_Clark" title="Joe Clark">Joe Clark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ed_Broadbent" title="Ed Broadbent">Ed Broadbent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Lewis" title="Stephen Lewis">Stephen Lewis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Stoffer" title="Peter Stoffer">Peter Stoffer</a>.
</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia_general_election,_2013" title="Nova Scotia general election, 2013">Nova Scotia general election, 2013</a> in which it fielded only 16 candidates in 51 districts, the party was largely dormant and invisible in NS politics.  Leader John Percy was also Veterans Affairs Critic in the <a href="/wiki/Green_Party_of_Canada" title="Green Party of Canada">Green Party of Canada</a> Shadow Cabinet,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and this was an active file during the <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Harper" title="Stephen Harper">Stephen Harper</a> regime until its demise in the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_general_election,_2015" class="mw-redirect" title="Canadian general election, 2015">Canadian general election, 2015</a>.  Percy resigned in 2015 and was replaced by interim leader Brynn Nheiley.
</p><p>The party was to hold a leadership convention on February 6, 2016 however no one came forward to run for leader.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-over_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-over-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After this experience of near-collapse, Thomas Trappenberg was elected leader in November 2016. He and Deputy Leader Jessica Alexander led the party in the 2017 election, where the party ran 32 candidates and received 2.78 percent of the vote. This was the highest total ever achieved by the Party, both in terms of total votes and percentage, though the Party did not elect any Members.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conventions">Conventions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_of_Nova_Scotia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Conventions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Founding_convention">Founding convention</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_of_Nova_Scotia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Founding convention">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The party's first <a href="/wiki/Political_convention" title="Political convention">convention</a> was held on March 4–5, 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the convention the Party unanimously approved its constitution which divided powers in a way fairly conventional among <a href="/wiki/Green_party" title="Green party">Green parties</a>:  an executive controlled regional relations, regions retained control of their local policies and candidacies, and a policy committee took control of the overall platform and positions taken even during an election.  The leader's role was to act as spokesperson and organize his or her most trusted critics as a <a href="/wiki/Shadow_Cabinet" title="Shadow Cabinet">Shadow Cabinet</a> or (once elected) a Caucus.  Leaders and deputies would run together on a "ticket" for continuity and to assess leader judgement of persons before they are elected to the critical role.
</p><p>Members also voted to support the six principles of the <a href="/wiki/Global_Green_Charter" class="mw-redirect" title="Global Green Charter">Global Green Charter</a>, elected an executive and chose a Party logo.
</p>
