<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Civil_Will_Party">Civil Will Party</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Civil_Will%E2%80%93Green_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Civil Will Party">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:CWPM.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/e4/CWPM.png/200px-CWPM.png" width="200" height="114" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/e4/CWPM.png/300px-CWPM.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/e/e4/CWPM.png 2x" data-file-width="380" data-file-height="216" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:CWPM.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Logo of the Civil Will Party, one of the parties that merged to create the Civil Will–Green Party</div></div></div>
<p>Civil Will Party was established on March 9, 2000, with <a href="/wiki/Sanjaas%C3%BCrengiin_Oyuun" title="Sanjaasürengiin Oyuun">Sanjaasürengiin Oyuun</a> as chairwoman and E. Narmandakh as general secretary. The party's name is a reference to the name of S.Oyun's murdered brother <a href="/wiki/Sanjaas%C3%BCrengiin_Zorig" title="Sanjaasürengiin Zorig">Sanjaasürengiin Zorig</a>, one of the leaders of the 1990 democratic movement.
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/Mongolian_legislative_election,_2000" title="Mongolian legislative election, 2000">2000 legislative election</a>, the Civil Will Party entered into a coalition with the Green Party, and won a seat in the State Great Khural. Before the <a href="/wiki/Mongolian_legislative_election,_2008" title="Mongolian legislative election, 2008">2008 legislative election</a>, the Mongolian National Unity Party (<a href="/wiki/Mongolian_language" title="Mongolian language">Mongolian</a>: <span lang="mn">Монголын Үндэсний Эв Нэгдлийн Нам</span>) merged with the Civil Will Party. In the ensuing elections on June 29, 2008, the party won one of the 76 seats in <a href="/wiki/State_Great_Khural" title="State Great Khural">Mongolia's parliament</a>, while receiving 1.97% of the popular vote.<sup id="cite_ref-history_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Merger">Merger</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Civil_Will%E2%80%93Green_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Merger">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 7th Congress of the Civil Will Party formed the Civil Will–Green Party. With the merger, the party has two members in the State Great Khural, Party chairman <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dangaas%C3%BCrengiin_Enkhbat&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dangaasürengiin Enkhbat (page does not exist)">Dangaasürengiin Enkhbat</a> and first deputy chairwoman <a href="/wiki/Sanjaas%C3%BCrengiin_Oyuun" title="Sanjaasürengiin Oyuun">Sanjaasürengiin Oyuun</a>. E. Zorigt works as the  Adviser of Nature and the Environment affairs to the President.<sup id="cite_ref-history_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the 8th Congress of the Civil Will Party conducted on 28 January 2012, the party changed its name to the Civil Will–Green Party, and approved the decision to have up to three chairpersons, and to adopt a new flag and symbol. The changes were submitted to the Supreme Court, which were then approved on March 12, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The merger was opposed by many members, with some people blocking the application to the Supreme Court for over six months.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_for_Reform">Government for Reform</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Civil_Will%E2%80%93Green_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Government for Reform">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/Mongolian_legislative_election,_2012" title="Mongolian legislative election, 2012">2012 election</a>, the party obtained two seats in parliament and was one of the partners in “Government for Reform”. The long-time standing leader and party chairwoman <a href="/wiki/Sanjaas%C3%BCrengiin_Oyuun" title="Sanjaasürengiin Oyuun">Sanjaasürengiin Oyuun</a> was serving as Minister for Green Development and Environment. Mr. Tumenjargal, head of the youth organisation, is Deputy Minister for Culture, Sports and Tourism. In addition the party had obtained its first seat in the <a href="/wiki/Ulaanbaatar" title="Ulaanbaatar">Ulaanbaatar</a> Representative’s Council.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
