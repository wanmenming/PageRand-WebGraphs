<p>On March 29, 2006 it was announced, in accordance with the <a href="/wiki/Green_Party_of_Canada" title="Green Party of Canada">Green Party of Canada</a> constitution that there would be a <a href="/wiki/Leadership_convention" title="Leadership convention">leadership election</a> held August 24–27, 2006 in <a href="/wiki/Ottawa" title="Ottawa">Ottawa</a>.
</p><p>Leadership contests in the party were automatically held every two years (every four years under the new constitution, beginning 2006), and every member of the party is entitled to vote, whether or not they attend the convention. The party mails out ballots to all members to be returned for counting.  This voting system also applies to council members and policy changes as well.
</p><p>On April 24, 2006, incumbent party leader <a href="/wiki/Jim_Harris_(politician)" title="Jim Harris (politician)">Jim Harris</a> announced he would not be running for re-election.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_May" title="Elizabeth May">Elizabeth May</a> on August 26, 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rules">Rules</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_of_Canada_leadership_election,_2006&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Rules">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Among other rules:
</p><p>Each candidate must file a nomination form signed by 100 candidate nominators residing in 3 or more provinces or territories.  Only 50 nominators are required if the candidate lives in a rural Electoral District as defined by <a href="/wiki/Elections_Canada" title="Elections Canada">Elections Canada</a>.
</p><p>Each candidate must pay a fully refundable $1000 prospective contestant's deposit.  Their campaign must also pay a non-refundable $1,000 contestant fee to cover party administration costs.
</p><p>Each candidate may spend a maximum of $50,000 on their campaign.
</p><p>Every registered member of the party can vote for the leader either at the convention, or by mail-in ballot.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Registered_candidates">Registered candidates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_of_Canada_leadership_election,_2006&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Registered candidates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Three candidates officially entered the leadership race before the close of nominations on May 31, 2006.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="David_Chernushenko">David Chernushenko</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_of_Canada_leadership_election,_2006&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: David Chernushenko">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:122px;"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Chernushenko.jpg" class="image"><img alt="David Chernushenko.jpg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/David_Chernushenko.jpg/120px-David_Chernushenko.jpg" width="120" height="134" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/David_Chernushenko.jpg/180px-David_Chernushenko.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/David_Chernushenko.jpg/240px-David_Chernushenko.jpg 2x" data-file-width="572" data-file-height="640" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Chernushenko.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div></div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/David_Chernushenko" title="David Chernushenko">David Chernushenko</a> was the party's Deputy Leader. He owns and operates Green &amp; Gold Inc., a consulting firm specializing in suggesting ways to increase efficiency and reduce the negative environmental and social impacts of athletic events and facilities. He started Clean Air Champions, an advocacy group consisting primarily of athletes working to improve air quality in Canada.
</p><p>Chernushenko was the Green Party candidate for <a href="/wiki/Ottawa_Centre" title="Ottawa Centre">Ottawa Centre</a> in both the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_federal_election,_2004" title="Canadian federal election, 2004">2004</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canadian_federal_election,_2006" title="Canadian federal election, 2006">2006 general federal elections</a>.  In the 2006 election, he received 6,766 votes (10.2% of valid ballots cast) - the highest vote count of any Green Party candidate (though not the highest percentage of votes). By surpassing the 10% threshold, Chernushenko's campaign became eligible for a partial government reimbursement of campaign expenditures.
</p><p>Chernushenko has twice been endorsed by the <i><a href="/wiki/Ottawa_Citizen" title="Ottawa Citizen">Ottawa Citizen</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Chernushenko finished fourth in both the 2004 and 2006 races despite the endorsements.
</p><p>David Chernushenko's nomination was confirmed by the party on May 16, 2006.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Elizabeth_May">Elizabeth May</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_of_Canada_leadership_election,_2006&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Elizabeth May">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:122px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Elizabeth_May_(environmentalist).jpg" class="image"><img alt="Elizabeth May (environmentalist).jpg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Elizabeth_May_%28environmentalist%29.jpg/120px-Elizabeth_May_%28environmentalist%29.jpg" width="120" height="149" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Elizabeth_May_%28environmentalist%29.jpg/180px-Elizabeth_May_%28environmentalist%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Elizabeth_May_%28environmentalist%29.jpg/240px-Elizabeth_May_%28environmentalist%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="515" data-file-height="640" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Elizabeth_May_(environmentalist).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div></div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_May" title="Elizabeth May">Elizabeth May</a>, the former Executive Director of the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Club_of_Canada" class="mw-redirect" title="Sierra Club of Canada">Sierra Club of Canada</a>, announced her run for the leadership on May 9, 2006.  It had been widely speculated that she would enter.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  Other candidates including <a href="/wiki/Claude_Genest" title="Claude Genest">Claude Genest</a> (who had already entered the race on February 2) had dropped out citing May's likely entry.
</p><p>In her announcement, May emphasized that Canadian parties in general have failed to lead, and claimed that she would "never" come out sounding like she'd been "packaged" by anyone else.  She cited the sexism of current Canadian politics, mentioning that "four men in suits" were debating issues of no significance, and that Canadians were expected to "become stupid" and behave as if they were at a "horse race" where the issues didn't matter at all.
</p><p>Elizabeth May's campaign has had some issues raised about financing as reported on Public Eye Online.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Basically, the article says that there was an error with May's website as it listed her tar sands tour as a campaign event. If the tour were a campaign event, the article claimed that then the expenses associated with the tour should be counted towards her $50,000 campaign limit.
</p><p>May's supporters include <a href="/wiki/Jim_MacNeill" title="Jim MacNeill">Jim MacNeill</a>, Secretary General of the World Commission for Environment and Development (the Brundtland Commission); <a href="/wiki/Clifford_Lincoln" title="Clifford Lincoln">Clifford Lincoln</a>, former Minister of Environment for the Province of Quebec, and former Liberal Member of Parliament; <a href="/wiki/Adriane_Carr" title="Adriane Carr">Adriane Carr</a>, Leader of the British Columbia Green Party; <a href="/wiki/Peter_Downie" title="Peter Downie">Peter Downie</a>, former CBC broadcaster <a href="/wiki/Claude_Genest" title="Claude Genest">Claude Genest</a>; prominent Green Party leader in Quebec; <a href="/wiki/Sharon_Labchuk" title="Sharon Labchuk">Sharon Labchuk</a>, Leader of the Green Party of Prince Edward Island; <a href="/w/index.php?title=Holly_Dressel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Holly Dressel (page does not exist)">Holly Dressel</a>, co-author of several best selling books with Dr. David Suzuki; Senator <a href="/wiki/Mira_Spivak" title="Mira Spivak">Mira Spivak</a>; <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lynette_Tremblay&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lynette Tremblay (page does not exist)">Lynette Tremblay</a>,  Deputy Leader Green Party of Canada; Stephen Woollcombe, formerly an active Conservative Party member and a Progressive Conservative candidate in the federal elections of 2000;<a href="/wiki/Victor_Lau" title="Victor Lau">Victor Lau</a>, Leader of the Green Party of Saskatchewan. Honorary Campaign Chair: <a href="/wiki/Robert_Bateman_(painter)" title="Robert Bateman (painter)">Robert Bateman</a>
</p><p>Elizabeth May's nomination was confirmed by the party on May 29, 2006.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Jim_Fannon">Jim Fannon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_of_Canada_leadership_election,_2006&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Jim Fannon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:122px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jim_Fannon.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Jim Fannon.jpg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Jim_Fannon.jpg/120px-Jim_Fannon.jpg" width="120" height="156" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Jim_Fannon.jpg/180px-Jim_Fannon.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Jim_Fannon.jpg/240px-Jim_Fannon.jpg 2x" data-file-width="491" data-file-height="640" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jim_Fannon.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div></div></div></div>
<p>Jim Fannon is a real estate agent at <a href="/wiki/RE/MAX" title="RE/MAX">RE/MAX</a> Garden City Realty and founder of Nature's Hemp, a company that sells hemp seed, oil, nut, flour and protein powder.  He is a former candidate for both the Green Party of Canada (1993, 2004, 2006) and Green Party of Ontario (2003) and is currently Chief Financial Officer for the Green Party of Ontario's St. Catharines Constituency Association.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Jim Fannon's nomination was confirmed by the party on May 31, 2006.
</p><p>At the leadership convention, Fannon gave what <i><a href="/wiki/The_Globe_and_Mail" title="The Globe and Mail">The Globe and Mail</a></i> called "one of the wackiest leadership speeches ever", constantly wiping sweat away from his face, thanking his mother and his dogsitter, and referring to his leadership opponent as "Elizabeth freakin' May".<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
