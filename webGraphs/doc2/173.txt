<p>The <b>national flag of <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Urdu_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Urdu language">Urdu</a>: <span lang="ur" dir="rtl"><span class="Nastaliq" dir="rtl" title="Nastaliq" style="font-family: &#39;Jameel Noori Nastaleeq&#39;, &#39;Urdu Typesetting&#39;, &#39;Noto Nastaliq Urdu&#39;, &#39;Noto Nastaliq Urdu Draft&#39;, &#39;Hussaini Nastaleeq&#39;, &#39;AlQalam Taj Nastaleeq&#39;, IranNastaliq, &#39;Awami Nastaliq&#39;, &#39;Awami Nastaliq Beta3&#39;, &#39;Awami Nastaliq Beta2&#39;, &#39;Awami Nastaliq Beta1&#39;, &#39;Nafees Nastaleeq&#39;, &#39;Nafees Nastaleeq v1.01&#39;, &#39;Pak Nastaleeq&#39;, &#39;PDMS_Jauhar&#39;, &#39;Alvi Lahori Nastaleeq&#39;; font-size: 125%; font-style: normal;">قومی پرچم</span>&#x202c;</span>&#8206;, <i lang="ur-Latn" title="American Library Association – Library of Congress transliteration"><i>Qaumī Pārc&#x331;am</i></i>) was adopted in its present form during a meeting of the <a href="/wiki/Constituent_Assembly_of_Pakistan" title="Constituent Assembly of Pakistan">Constituent Assembly</a> on August 11, 1947, just three days before the country's <a href="/wiki/Independence_of_Pakistan" class="mw-redirect" title="Independence of Pakistan">independence</a>, when it became the official flag of the <a href="/wiki/Dominion_of_Pakistan" title="Dominion of Pakistan">Dominion of Pakistan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-flagresolution_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flagresolution-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-flagdate1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flagdate1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-flagdate2_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flagdate2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It was afterwards retained by the current-day Islamic Republic of Pakistan. The flag is a <a href="/wiki/Green" title="Green">green</a> field with a white <a href="/wiki/Star_and_crescent" title="Star and crescent">crescent moon and five-rayed star</a> at its centre, and a vertical white stripe at the hoist side. Though the green colour is mandated only as 'dark green',<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> its official and most consistent representation is <a href="/wiki/Pakistan_green" class="mw-redirect" title="Pakistan green">Pakistan green</a>, which is <a href="/wiki/Tints_and_shades" title="Tints and shades">shaded</a> distinctively darker.  The flag was designed by <a href="/wiki/Amiruddin_Kidwai" class="mw-redirect" title="Amiruddin Kidwai">Amiruddin Kidwai</a>, and is based on the <a href="/wiki/All-India_Muslim_League" title="All-India Muslim League">All-India Muslim League</a> flag.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The flag is referred to in the <a href="/wiki/Qaum%C4%AB_Tar%C4%81na" class="mw-redirect" title="Qaumī Tarāna">national anthem</a> as the Flag of the Crescent and Star. It is flown on several important days of the year including <a href="/wiki/Republic_Day_(Pakistan)" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic Day (Pakistan)">Republic Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/Independence_Day_(Pakistan)" title="Independence Day (Pakistan)">Independence Day</a> and Defence Day. It is often hoisted every morning at schools, offices and government buildings to the sound of the national anthem and lowered again before sunset. A notable flag raising and lowering ceremony is carried out each day with great pomp and enthusiasm at the <a href="/wiki/Wagah_Border" class="mw-redirect" title="Wagah Border">Wagah Border</a> attended by hundreds of spectators. A designer named Amiruddin Kidwai studied the League’s flag, as he tried to design a flag for a new, independent nation. Finally he arrived at a design, and he presented it to the leadership of the <a href="/wiki/All-India_Muslim_League" title="All-India Muslim League">Muslim League</a> who subsequently adopted his design as the flag of the Dominion of Pakistan on 11 August, 1947. The government of Pakistan has pronounced rules about the flying of the flag. It is to be displayed at full mast on 23 March of each year, marking the adoption of the <a href="/wiki/Lahore_Resolution" title="Lahore Resolution">Lahore Resolution</a> in 1940's and the Declaration of the Republic of Pakistan in 1956, and on 14 August in celebration of Independence Day, when Pakistan was carved out from <a href="/wiki/British_India" class="mw-redirect" title="British India">British India</a> as a home for Indian Muslims. The flag of Pakistan is also mentioned in Pakistan's own national anthem in the third verse reading <big><span class="nowrap"><span lang="ur" title="Urdu language text"><span class="Nastaliq" dir="rtl" title="Nastaliq" style="font-family: &#39;Jameel Noori Nastaleeq&#39;, &#39;Urdu Typesetting&#39;, &#39;Noto Nastaliq Urdu&#39;, &#39;Noto Nastaliq Urdu Draft&#39;, &#39;Hussaini Nastaleeq&#39;, &#39;AlQalam Taj Nastaleeq&#39;, IranNastaliq, &#39;Awami Nastaliq&#39;, &#39;Awami Nastaliq Beta3&#39;, &#39;Awami Nastaliq Beta2&#39;, &#39;Awami Nastaliq Beta1&#39;, &#39;Nafees Nastaleeq&#39;, &#39;Nafees Nastaleeq v1.01&#39;, &#39;Pak Nastaleeq&#39;, &#39;PDMS_Jauhar&#39;, &#39;Alvi Lahori Nastaleeq&#39;; font-size: 125%; font-style: normal;">"پرچمِ ستاره و ہلال رہبرِ ترقّی و کمال"</span>&#x202c;</span></span></big> which translates to "<i>Flag with the Star and Crescent, the leader of progress and ascent</i>".
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_symbolism">History and symbolism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Pakistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and symbolism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Before the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>, <a href="/wiki/Muslims" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslims">Muslims</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hindus" class="mw-redirect" title="Hindus">Hindus</a> lived together under the <a href="/wiki/British_Raj" title="British Raj">British Raj</a>. A number of the Muslims formed the <a href="/wiki/All_India_Muslim_League" class="mw-redirect" title="All India Muslim League">All India Muslim League</a>. After the Second World War, when  the <a href="/wiki/Pakistan_Movement" title="Pakistan Movement">independence</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> in 1947, the flag of the Muslim League served as the basis for the flag of Pakistan.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Green_in_Islam" title="Green in Islam">green</a> represents Islam and the majority Muslims in Pakistan and the white stripe represents <a href="/wiki/Status_of_minorities_in_Pakistan" class="mw-redirect" title="Status of minorities in Pakistan">religious minorities</a> and minority religions.<sup id="cite_ref-flagsig1_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flagsig1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> 
In the centre, the <a href="/wiki/Crescent_and_star" class="mw-redirect" title="Crescent and star">crescent and star</a> symbolizes progress and light respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-flagsig1_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flagsig1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The flag symbolizes Pakistan's commitment to Islam and the rights of religious minorities.<sup id="cite_ref-basicfacts_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-basicfacts-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> It is based on the original flag of the <a href="/wiki/All-India_Muslim_League" title="All-India Muslim League">Muslim League</a>, which itself drew inspiration from the flag of the <a href="/wiki/Sultanate_of_Delhi" class="mw-redirect" title="Sultanate of Delhi">Sultanate of Delhi</a>, the flag of <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_the_Mughal_Empire" title="Flag of the Mughal Empire">Flag of the Mughal Empire</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Pakistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="floatright"><a href="/wiki/File:Flag_of_Pakistan_Construction.svg" class="image" title="Diagram of the flag&#39;s design"><img alt="Diagram of the flag&#39;s design" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Flag_of_Pakistan_Construction.svg/350px-Flag_of_Pakistan_Construction.svg.png" width="350" height="247" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Flag_of_Pakistan_Construction.svg/525px-Flag_of_Pakistan_Construction.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Flag_of_Pakistan_Construction.svg/700px-Flag_of_Pakistan_Construction.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="501" data-file-height="354" /></a></div>
<p>The official design of the national flag was adopted by the Constituent Assembly together with a definition of the features and proportions.
</p><p>According to the specifications it is a dark green rectangular flag in the proportion of length [A] and width [B] as <b>3:2</b> with a white vertical bar at the mast, the green portion bearing a white crescent in the centre and a five-pointed white heraldic star. The width of the white portion [C] is one quarter the width of the flag [A], nearest the mast, so the green portion occupies the remaining three quarters [D].
Draw a diagonal L3 from the top right hand corner to the bottom left corner of the green portion. On this diagonal establish two points P1 and P2. P1 is positioned at the centre of the green portion and P2 at the intersection of the diagonal L3 and an arc C4 created from the top right hand corner equal to 13/20 the height of the flag [E]. With the centre at point P1 and a radius 3/10 the height of the flag describe the first circle C1 and with centre at point P2 and a radius 11/40 the height of the flag describe a second circle C2. The enclosures made by these two circles form the crescent. The dimensions of the five-pointed white heraldic star are determined by drawing a circle C3 with a radius 1/10 the height of the flag positioned between P2 and P3 on the diagonal L3. The circle surrounds the five points of the heraldic star and star lies with one point on the diagonal L3 at point P3 where the circle C1 intersects the diagonal L3.
The flag is colored in Pakistan green having standard RGB values (red = 0, green = 102, blue = 0) or with hex triplet #006600 or the HSV value = (h = 120, s = 100, v = 40). The left strip, the star and  the crescent are painted in white. The flag is supported from left white side.
</p>

<ul><li>No other flag or colour must fly higher.</li>
<li>When displayed or flown alongside other <a href="/wiki/National_flags" class="mw-redirect" title="National flags">national flags</a>, the National Flag must be displayed or flown at the same height as the other national flags, never lower.</li>
<li>If there are two flags or colours, the National Flag should be flown at the right. In case the number of flags is more than two and odd, the National Flag should be placed in the centre, and if the number of flags is even it should be flown the first to the right of the centre.</li>
<li>When displayed alongside provincial, military or corporate flags, the National Flag must be higher.</li>
<li>When tied to a mast, it must be tied only at the left (at the beginning of the white bar) and left to fly freely without any obstruction.</li>
<li>In a procession, the National Flag shall be carried at the centre or at the right of the procession in the line of march.</li>
<li>Must not touch the ground, shoes or feet or anything unclean.</li>
<li>Must never be flown in darkness.</li>
<li>Must be raised at dawn and lowered at dusk (except on the Parliament of Pakistan, which is the only official building on which the flag is never lowered). When flown over the Parliament of Pakistan at night, it must always remain alit with artificial light.</li>
<li>Must not be marked with anything (including words, numerals or images).</li>
<li>When raising or lowering: (i) must be saluted to by all uniformed personnel, (ii) others must stand in attention.</li>
<li>Must be raised or lowered ceremoniously.</li>
<li>When displayed horizontally, the white strip must always be on the left, with green field on the right</li>
<li>When displayed vertically, the white strip must always be at the top, with green field at the bottom.</li>
<li>Must not fly or be displayed upside down or with the crescent and star facing left.</li>
<li>Must not be displayed anywhere where it is likely to get dirty.</li>
<li>Must not be set on fire or trampled upon.</li>
<li>Must not be buried or lowered into a grave (when burying a flag-bearing casket, the National Flag must be detached from the casket and held above the grave as the casket is lowered or removed from the casket before burial).</li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Milestones">Milestones</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Pakistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Milestones">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b>2017</b>- On 14th August, People of Balochistan hosted a 2 mile long flag of Pakistan in Quetta.</li>
<li><b>2014</b> - On 15 February, 29,040 people gathered in a stadium in <a href="/wiki/Lahore" title="Lahore">Lahore</a> to form the flag of Pakistan and set a new world record for forming the world's largest national flag comprising humans, which was certified by <a href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Records" title="Guinness World Records">Guinness World Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>2012</b> - On 22 October, 24,200 people gathered in a stadium in <a href="/wiki/Lahore" title="Lahore">Lahore</a> to form the flag of Pakistan and set a new world record for forming the world's largest national flag comprising humans, which was certified by <a href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Records" title="Guinness World Records">Guinness World Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-World&#39;s_largest_flag_record_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-World&#39;s_largest_flag_record-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>2004</b> - In August, a 340×510 (173,400 square foot) foot flag of Pakistan was unfurled at the <a href="/wiki/National_Stadium,_Karachi" title="National Stadium, Karachi">National Stadium</a> Karachi, setting the world record for the largest flag.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1947</b> - On the night of August 14, 1947, a group of <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indian</a> Boy Scouts were in France when the news reached them that their motherland had become independent. Mohammad Iqbal Qureshi was one of the <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> boy scouts who with the help of his friends turned a green turban into a Pakistani flag and unfurled it.  August 15, 1947 was the first time when the Pakistani flag flew on the foreign soil.</li></ul>
