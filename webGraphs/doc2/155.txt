<p>The <b>Green Alternative</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">Hungarian</a>: <i lang="hu">Zöld Alternatíva</i>; <b>ZA</b>), later <b>Green Alternative – Alliance of Greens in Hungary</b>, was a <a href="/wiki/Green_politics" title="Green politics">green</a> political party in <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a> between 1993 and 2000.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Alternative_(Hungary)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following the radical right-wing ideological turnout within the <a href="/wiki/Green_Party_of_Hungary" title="Green Party of Hungary">Green Party of Hungary</a> (MZP), several environmentalist members decided to left the party and establish the Green Alternative in June 1993 during their first congress at the University of Horticulture (today a faculty within the <a href="/wiki/Corvinus_University_of_Budapest" title="Corvinus University of Budapest">Corvinus University of Budapest</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEVida2011466_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEVida2011466-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The congress elected <a href="/wiki/Gy%C3%B6rgy_Droppa" title="György Droppa">György Droppa</a> (a former <a href="/wiki/Duna_K%C3%B6r" title="Duna Kör">Duna Kör</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alliance_of_Free_Democrats" title="Alliance of Free Democrats">SZDSZ</a> member) and <a href="/wiki/Erzs%C3%A9bet_Schmuck" title="Erzsébet Schmuck">Erzsébet Schmuck</a> (a former <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Young_Communist_League" title="Hungarian Young Communist League">KISZ</a> member) as co-presidents. In January 1994, <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Democratic_Forum" title="Hungarian Democratic Forum">MDF</a> politician and MP <a href="/w/index.php?title=K%C3%A1roly_J%C3%A1vor&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Károly Jávor (page does not exist)">Károly Jávor</a> (father of <a href="/wiki/Member_of_the_European_Parliament" title="Member of the European Parliament">MEP</a> <a href="/wiki/Benedek_J%C3%A1vor" title="Benedek Jávor">Benedek Jávor</a>) joined the ZA, providing parliamentary representation for the party for few months until the <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_parliamentary_election,_1994" title="Hungarian parliamentary election, 1994">1994 parliamentary election</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEVida2011467_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEVida2011467-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> when ZA received only 0.02 percent of the votes.<sup id="cite_ref-NS_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In the second round, ZA entered into an electoral alliance with the <a href="/wiki/Agrarian_Alliance" title="Agrarian Alliance">Agrarian Alliance</a> (ASZ), but did not gain any individual seats.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEVida2011467_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEVida2011467-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the forthcoming years, the Green Alternative protested against the constructions of <a href="/wiki/Gab%C4%8D%C3%ADkovo%E2%80%93Nagymaros_Dams" title="Gabčíkovo–Nagymaros Dams">Gabčíkovo–Nagymaros Dams</a>, a landfill in <a href="/wiki/P%C3%A9cel" title="Pécel">Pécel</a> and the operations of the uranium mine in <a href="/wiki/P%C3%A9cs" title="Pécs">Pécs</a>, the alumina plant in <a href="/wiki/L%C3%A1batlan" title="Lábatlan">Lábatlan</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Paks_Nuclear_Power_Plant" title="Paks Nuclear Power Plant">Paks Nuclear Power Plant</a>. In September 1997, it joined the electoral alliance Union for Hungary, dominated by the <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(Hungary)" title="Republican Party (Hungary)">Republican Party</a>, however they received only 0.19 percent of the votes in the <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_parliamentary_election,_1998" title="Hungarian parliamentary election, 1998">1998 parliamentary election</a>. The coalition broke up after that. Erzsébet Schmuck left the party on 21 June 1998, she was replaced by Márta Márczis (mother of Benedek Jávor) as co-president. On 3 June 2000, the ZA merged with the Social Democracy 2000 Foundation to form the <a href="/wiki/Alliance_of_Green_Democrats" title="Alliance of Green Democrats">Alliance of Green Democrats</a> (ZDSZ).<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEVida2011467_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEVida2011467-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_relations">International relations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Alternative_(Hungary)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International relations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In January 1994, the ZA was admitted into the <a href="/wiki/European_Federation_of_Green_Parties" class="mw-redirect" title="European Federation of Green Parties">European Federation of Green Parties</a>, which established under this name few months earlier. György Droppa was also elected to the European political party's leadership. In that capacity, Droppa established a good relationship with the <a href="/wiki/GreenLeft" class="mw-redirect" title="GreenLeft">GreenLeft</a>, a prominent Dutch member of the federation.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEVida2011468_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEVida2011468-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
