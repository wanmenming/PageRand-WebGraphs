<p>The <b>Green Party</b> (<a href="/wiki/Bokm%C3%A5l_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Bokmål language">Bokmål</a>: <i lang="nb">Miljøpartiet De Grønne</i>, <a href="/wiki/Nynorsk_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Nynorsk language">Nynorsk</a>: <i lang="nn">Miljøpartiet Dei Grøne</i>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Sami_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Northern Sami language">Northern Sami</a>: <i lang="se">Birasbellodat Ruonát</i>, literally "The Environment Party The Greens") is a <a href="/wiki/Green_politics" title="Green politics">green</a><sup id="cite_ref-Nordsieck_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nordsieck-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Norway" title="List of political parties in Norway">political party</a> in <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>. The party holds one seat in the <a href="/wiki/Storting" title="Storting">Parliament of Norway</a> (gaining 2.8% in the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_parliamentary_election,_2013" title="Norwegian parliamentary election, 2013">2013 elections</a>) and also has representation in <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Norway" class="mw-redirect" title="Municipalities of Norway">municipal</a> councils and <a href="/wiki/County_council_(Norway)" title="County council (Norway)">county councils</a> (gaining 4.2% in the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_local_elections,_2015" title="Norwegian local elections, 2015">2015 elections</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The Green Party advocates <a href="/wiki/Green_politics" title="Green politics">green politics</a>, and has been described as centre-left by academics and voters.<sup id="cite_ref-uio_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-uio-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-vox_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vox-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The party itself claims distance to the two dominant right-wing and left-wing political blocs, jointly denominated as "the fossil block".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Green Party is a member of the <a href="/wiki/European_Green_Party" title="European Green Party">European Green Party</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Global_Greens" title="Global Greens">Global Greens</a>, and was founded with the <a href="/wiki/Alliance_%2790/The_Greens" class="mw-redirect" title="Alliance &#39;90/The Greens">German Greens</a> as its stated model.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> It maintains close ties to other Green parties including the German Greens and the <a href="/wiki/Green_Party_(Sweden)" title="Green Party (Sweden)">Swedish Greens</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_(Norway)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The process of forming a new national green party in Norway was initiated in December 1984, with the official launch in 1988.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Among the pioneers were the late philosopher <a href="/wiki/Arne_N%C3%A6ss" title="Arne Næss">Arne Næss</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> peace researcher <a href="/wiki/Johan_Galtung" title="Johan Galtung">Johan Galtung</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and the philosopher <a href="/wiki/Sigmund_Kval%C3%B8y_Setreng" title="Sigmund Kvaløy Setreng">Sigmund Kvaløy Setreng</a>.
</p><p>In the local elections between 1991 and 2009 the Green Party had 6 - 8 representatives elected each time. In the national elections the party never exceeded 0.5% support.
</p><p>Since 2005, the Greens have seen a significant membership rise, with the new members coming from a wide variety of other parties, including the seven established parliamentary parties.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_local_elections,_2011" title="Norwegian local elections, 2011">municipal elections of 2011</a>, the party saw its first local breakthrough, having garnered close to 22,000 votes on a national basis. Two years later, during the campaign for the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_parliamentary_election,_2013" title="Norwegian parliamentary election, 2013">2013 general election</a>, the party saw a significant rise in support in the opinion polls. The Greens were widely expected to gain parliamentary representation to some extent.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In the election, the Greens gathered over 79,000 votes, making them the 8th biggest party in the country. This vote count translates to 2.8 percent of the vote. <a href="/wiki/Rasmus_Hansson" title="Rasmus Hansson">Rasmus Hansson</a>, the party's top candidate from <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Oslo</a> was elected to parliament, becoming the first ever Green MP.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the local elections of 2015 the Green Party overtook the 4% nationally for the first time in its history and got the third place in Oslo.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ideology">Ideology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Party_(Norway)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ideology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Green Party is one of the global ecologist and environmentalist political parties and movements.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> As a member of the pan-European <a href="/wiki/European_Green_Party" title="European Green Party">European Green Party</a>, the Norwegian Greens subscribe to social progressivism and social justice.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The main focus of the party is environmental protection and ecological sustainability. The party seeks to introduce a tax on wasteful consumption, and to reorganise the food industry. The Greens have also pledged support for a reform in the agrarian industry, increasing the production of organic crops and strengthening the eco-friendly agricultural sector.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Green party seeks to reduce the Norwegian petroleum extraction in order to counteract serious <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a>. The proposal is to stop extraction by 2033.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The party does not have a leader in the traditional sense, rather it is led by the national executive committee which consists of seven persons. Among the board members, two persons (currently <a href="/wiki/Rasmus_Hansson" title="Rasmus Hansson">Rasmus Hansson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Une_Aina_Bastholm" title="Une Aina Bastholm">Une Aina Bastholm</a>), act as national spokespersons for the party. All party representatives are elected during the annual party convention.
</p>
