<p><b>Dibromine trioxide</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Chemical_compound" title="Chemical compound">chemical compound</a> composed of <a href="/wiki/Bromine" title="Bromine">bromine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygen</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Chemical_formula" title="Chemical formula">formula</a> Br<sub>2</sub>O<sub>3</sub>. It is an orange solid that is stable below &#8722;40&#160;°C. It has the structure Br&#8722;O&#8722;BrO<sub>2</sub> (bromine <a href="/wiki/Bromate" title="Bromate">bromate</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-intro_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-intro-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
The Br&#8722;O&#8722;Br bond is bent with bond angle 111.2° and the Br&#8722;O&#8722;BrO<sub>2</sub> <a href="/wiki/Bond_length" title="Bond length">bond length</a> is 1.85<a href="/wiki/Angstrom" class="mw-redirect" title="Angstrom">Å</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-inorg_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inorg-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reactions">Reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dibromine_trioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dibromine trioxide can be prepared by reacting a solution of bromine in <a href="/wiki/Dichloromethane" title="Dichloromethane">dichloromethane</a> with <a href="/wiki/Ozone" title="Ozone">ozone</a> at low temperatures.<sup id="cite_ref-intro_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-intro-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-inorg_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inorg-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> 
It <a href="/wiki/Disproportionation" title="Disproportionation">disproportionates</a> in <a href="/wiki/Alkali" title="Alkali">alkali</a> solutions to <a href="/wiki/Bromide" title="Bromide">Br<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1.2em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">&#8722;</sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sub></span></a> and <a href="/wiki/Bromate" title="Bromate">BrO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1.2em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">&#8722;</sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-inorg_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inorg-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
