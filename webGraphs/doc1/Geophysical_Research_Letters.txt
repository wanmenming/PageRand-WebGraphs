<p><i><b>Geophysical Research Letters</b></i> is a biweekly <a href="/wiki/Peer-reviewed" class="mw-redirect" title="Peer-reviewed">peer-reviewed</a> <a href="/wiki/Scientific_journal" title="Scientific journal">scientific journal</a> of <a href="/wiki/Geoscience" class="mw-redirect" title="Geoscience">geoscience</a> published by the <a href="/wiki/American_Geophysical_Union" title="American Geophysical Union">American Geophysical Union</a> that was established in 1974. The <a href="/wiki/Editor-in-chief" title="Editor-in-chief">editor-in-chief</a> is <a href="/wiki/Noah_Diffenbaugh" title="Noah Diffenbaugh">Noah Diffenbaugh</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aims_and_scope">Aims and scope</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geophysical_Research_Letters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Aims and scope">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The journal aims for rapid publication of concise research reports on one or more of the disciplines covered by the <a href="/wiki/American_Geophysical_Union" title="American Geophysical Union">American Geophysical Union</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_sciences" title="Atmospheric sciences">atmospheric sciences</a>, <a href="/wiki/Solid_Earth" class="mw-redirect" title="Solid Earth">solid Earth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Space_science" class="mw-redirect" title="Space science">space science</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oceanography" title="Oceanography">oceanography</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hydrology" title="Hydrology">hydrology</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Land_surface_processes&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Land surface processes (page does not exist)">land surface processes</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Cryosphere" title="Cryosphere">cryosphere</a>. The journal also publishes invited reviews that cover advances achieved during the past two or three years. The target readership is the <a href="/wiki/Earth_science" title="Earth science">earth science</a> community, the broader scientific community, and the general public.<sup id="cite_ref-disciplines_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-disciplines-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-change10_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-change10-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
