<p><b>Aluminium(II) oxide</b>  or <b>aluminium monoxide</b> is a compound of <a href="/wiki/Aluminium" title="Aluminium">aluminium</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygen</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Chemical_formula" title="Chemical formula">chemical formula</a> AlO. It has been detected in the gas phase after explosion of aluminized grenades in the upper atmosphere<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and in stellar absorption spectra.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
