<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Nova Science Publishers</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<p><b>Nova Science Publishers</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Academic_publishing" title="Academic publishing">academic publisher</a> of books, encyclopedias, <a href="/wiki/Handbook" title="Handbook">handbooks</a>, <a href="/wiki/E-book" title="E-book">e-books</a> and <a href="/wiki/Academic_journal" title="Academic journal">journals</a>, based in <a href="/wiki/Hauppauge,_New_York" title="Hauppauge, New York">Hauppauge, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It was founded in 1985 in New York by Frank Columbus, former <a href="/wiki/Senior_editor" class="mw-redirect" title="Senior editor">senior editor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Plenum_Publishing" class="mw-redirect" title="Plenum Publishing">Plenum Publishing</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> whose wife, Nadya Columbus, took over upon his death in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> While the firm publishes works in several fields of <a href="/wiki/Academia" class="mw-redirect" title="Academia">academia</a>, most of its publications cover the fields of science, <a href="/wiki/Social_science" title="Social science">social science</a>, and medicine. As of February 2018, it listed 100 currently published journals.<sup id="cite_ref-NovaJCat_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NovaJCat-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
In 2018 NOVA was ranked on the 13th place global main publishers of <a href="/wiki/Political_sciences" class="mw-redirect" title="Political sciences">political sciences</a> during the last 5 years<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>.
</p><p>Nova Science Publishers is included in the <a href="/wiki/Book_Citation_Index" title="Book Citation Index">Book Citation Index</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
In terms of number of books published from 2005 to 2012, Nova ranked 4th. They ranked in the top three in 8 of 14 scientific fields (engineering, clinical medicine, <a href="/wiki/Human_biology" title="Human biology">human biology</a>, animal and plant biology, <a href="/wiki/Earth_science" title="Earth science">geosciences</a>, social science medicine, health, chemistry, physics, and astronomy),<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and ranked as the 5th most prolific book publisher from 2009-2013, ranking 3rd in Engineering and Technology and 2nd in Science by numbers of books published.<sup id="cite_ref-TorresSalinas2015_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TorresSalinas2015-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> They were also ranked as the 6th most productive publisher, according to a <a href="/wiki/University_of_Granada" title="University of Granada">University of Granada</a> study.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> However, Nova had the lowest citation impact among the five most prolific publishers in both fields.<sup id="cite_ref-TorresSalinas2015_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TorresSalinas2015-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies_and_criticism">Controversies and criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nova_Science_Publishers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Controversies and criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nova has been criticized for not always evaluating authors through the <a href="/wiki/Academic_peer_review" class="mw-redirect" title="Academic peer review">academic peer review</a> process<sup id="cite_ref-Phillips_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Phillips-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and for republishing old <a href="/wiki/Public_domain" title="Public domain">public domain</a> book chapters and freely-accessible government reports at high prices.<sup id="cite_ref-Phillips_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Phillips-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Republishing_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Republishing-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> These criticisms prompted librarian <a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_Beall" title="Jeffrey Beall">Jeffrey Beall</a> to write that in his opinion Nova Science Publishers was in the "bottom-tier" of publishers.<sup id="cite_ref-Scholarly_Open_Access_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Scholarly_Open_Access-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A recent survey of national and international databases of scholarly book publishers (including the <a href="/wiki/Book_Citation_Index" title="Book Citation Index">Book Citation Index</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scopus" title="Scopus">Scopus</a>, <a href="/wiki/CRIStin" title="CRIStin">CRIStin</a>, JUFO, VIRTA and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Scholarly_Publishers_Indicators&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Scholarly Publishers Indicators (page does not exist)">SPI</a>) identified Nova as one of a "core of publishers that are indexed in all five" of the information systems surveyed. This "core" contained 46 out of the 3,765 publishers identified.<sup id="cite_ref-Giménez-Toledo_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Giménez-Toledo-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In a 2011 report of twenty-one international social-science book publishers that determined penetration on international markets and mention of books in international science index systems such as <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_Scientific_Abstracts" class="mw-redirect" title="Cambridge Scientific Abstracts">Cambridge Scientific Abstracts</a>, Nova Science Publishers ranked 17th out of 21.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In a 2017 ranking study of book publishers, Nova Science Publishers was ranked high on number of books published, but near the bottom for number of citations per book.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> A 2018 report by the same author now indicates that Nova ranks among the top half among 51 for 1.overall global standing of the company; 2.company as a factor on the market; 3.company impact on the global political and economic debate; 4.successfully distributing best-sellers; 5. impact on the scholarly community; 6.successfully distributing production to more than 50 global Worldcat libraries; 7.output during the last 5 years; 8. outstanding academic quality.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> But also as of 2018, Nova has been removed from the Norwegian list of approved scientific publishing channels (Norwegian Register for Scientific Journals, Series and Publishers).<sup id="cite_ref-NSD_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NSD-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
