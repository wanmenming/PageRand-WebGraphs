<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Planetary management</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>
				<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Planetary management</b> is intentional global-scale management of <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Biological" class="mw-redirect" title="Biological">biological</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chemical" class="mw-redirect" title="Chemical">chemical</a> and <a href="/wiki/Physical_property" title="Physical property">physical</a> processes and <a href="/wiki/Biogeochemical_cycle" title="Biogeochemical cycle">cycles</a> (<a href="/wiki/Water_cycle" title="Water cycle">water</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carbon_cycle_re-balancing" title="Carbon cycle re-balancing">carbon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nitrogen_cycle" title="Nitrogen cycle">nitrogen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sulfur_cycle" title="Sulfur cycle">sulfur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phosphorus_cycle" title="Phosphorus cycle">phosphorus</a>, and others). Planetary management also includes managing <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">humanity</a>’s influence on planetary-scale processes.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  Effective planetary management aims to prevent destabilisation of <a href="/wiki/Earth%27s_climate" class="mw-redirect" title="Earth&#39;s climate">Earth's climate</a>, protect <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">biodiversity</a> and maintain or improve human well-being. More specifically, it aims to benefit society and the <a href="/wiki/World_economy" title="World economy">global economy</a>, and safeguard the <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem_services" title="Ecosystem services">ecosystem services</a> upon which humanity depends – global <a href="/wiki/Climate" title="Climate">climate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Freshwater" class="mw-redirect" title="Freshwater">freshwater</a> supply, <a href="/wiki/Food" title="Food">food</a>, <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a>, clean air, fertile <a href="/wiki/Soil" title="Soil">soil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pollinators" class="mw-redirect" title="Pollinators">pollinators</a>, and so on.
</p><p>Because of the sheer complexity and enormous scope of the task, it remains to be seen whether planetary management is a feasible paradigm for maintaining global <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a>.  The concept currently has defenders and critics on both sides: <a href="/wiki/Environmentalist" title="Environmentalist">environmentalist</a> <a href="/wiki/David_W._Orr" title="David W. Orr">David W. Orr</a> questions whether such a task can be accomplished with human help and technology or without first examining the underlying human causes,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> while geographer <a href="/wiki/Vaclav_Smil" title="Vaclav Smil">Vaclav Smil</a> acknowledges that "the idea of planetary management may seem preposterous to many, but at this time in history there is no rational alternative".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Planetary_management&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:402px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Planetary_Boundaries_2015.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Planetary_Boundaries_2015.svg/400px-Planetary_Boundaries_2015.svg.png" width="400" height="400" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Planetary_Boundaries_2015.svg/600px-Planetary_Boundaries_2015.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Planetary_Boundaries_2015.svg/800px-Planetary_Boundaries_2015.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="800" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Planetary_Boundaries_2015.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Planetary_boundaries" title="Planetary boundaries">Planetary boundaries</a> according to Rockström <i>et al</i>. 2009<sup id="cite_ref-Rockström_2009_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rockström_2009-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and Steffen <i>et al</i>. 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The green areas represent human activities that are within safe margins, the yellow areas represent human activities that may or may not have exceeded safe margins, the red areas represent human activities that have exceeded safe margins, and the gray areas with red question marks represent human activities for which safe margins have not yet been determined.</div></div></div>
<p>The term has been around in science fiction novels since the 1970s.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In 2004, the <a href="/wiki/International_Geosphere-Biosphere_Programme" title="International Geosphere-Biosphere Programme">International Geosphere-Biosphere Programme</a> published “Global Change and the Earth System, a planet under pressure.”<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The publication’s executive summary concluded: “An overall, comprehensive, internally consistent strategy for stewardship of the Earth system is required”. It stated that a research goal is to define and maintain a stable equilibrium in the global environment. In 2009, the <a href="/wiki/Planetary_boundaries" title="Planetary boundaries">planetary boundaries</a> concept was published in the science journal <a href="/wiki/Nature_(journal)" title="Nature (journal)">Nature</a>. The paper identifies nine boundaries in the Earth system. Remaining within these nine boundaries, the authors suggest, may safeguard the current equilibrium.
</p><p>In 2007, France called for <a href="/wiki/UNEP" class="mw-redirect" title="UNEP">UNEP</a> to be replaced by a new and more powerful organization, the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Environment_Organization" title="United Nations Environment Organization">United Nations Environment Organization</a>. The rationale was that UNEP’s status as a programme, rather than an organization in the tradition of the <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Organization" title="World Health Organization">World Health Organization</a> or the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a>, weakened it to the extent that it was no longer fit for purpose given current knowledge of the state of Earth. The call was backed by 46 countries. Notably, the top five emitters of <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gases</a> failed to support the call.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Comparison_with_other_environmental_worldviews">Comparison with other environmental worldviews</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Planetary_management&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Comparison with other environmental worldviews">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Together with planetary management, <a href="/wiki/Stewardship" title="Stewardship">stewardship</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_wisdom&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Environmental wisdom (page does not exist)">environmental wisdom</a> are different ways to manage the Earth<sup id="cite_ref-cosm_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cosm-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> or "<a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_worldview&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Environmental worldview (page does not exist)">environmental worldviews</a>".
</p><p>In particular:
</p>
<ul><li>Planetary management focus its attention on humans needs and wants, while stewardship aim at the benefit of humans, organisms and ecosystems of the Earth:<sup id="cite_ref-cosm_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cosm-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> in other words, planetary management considers humans as the most important species in the Earth, while stewardship and environmental wisdom consider all the species at the same level of importance.</li>
<li>To accomplish its scope, planetary management relies on technology and innovation (as stewardship and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spaceship-earth_worldview&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Spaceship-earth worldview (page does not exist)">spaceship-earth worldview</a>), while environmental wisdom relies on the lesson learned from nature.<sup id="cite_ref-cosm_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cosm-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
