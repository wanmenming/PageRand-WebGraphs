<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Land footprint</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>
				<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Land footprint</b> is the real amount of <a href="/wiki/Land" title="Land">land</a>, wherever it is in the world, that is needed to produce a product, or used by an organisation or by a nation.<sup id="cite_ref-europa1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-europa1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Land_footprint&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Land footprint is a <a href="/wiki/Resource_consumption" title="Resource consumption">consumption</a>-based indicator, i.e. it looks at the <a href="/wiki/Natural_resources" class="mw-redirect" title="Natural resources">resources</a> needed to create a final product, or by an organisation or country, wherever they are in the world. This is in contrast to production-based indicators, which just look at resource use within a country.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> For example, cows will require land to graze on within a country, but may also be fed by feed grown on land in another country. This production vs consumption approach is extensively discussed in <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions_accounting" title="Greenhouse gas emissions accounting">greenhouse gas emissions accounting</a>, where <a href="/wiki/Carbon_footprint" title="Carbon footprint">carbon footprint</a> is the consumption-based indicator.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Related_concepts">Related concepts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Land_footprint&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Related concepts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Land footprint is closely linked to a number of other concepts, including:
</p>
<ul><li><i>Ghost acres</i> (or ghost hectares), a term that started to be in use in the 1960s, for example by Georg Borgström in his book "The Hungry Planet: The modern world at the edge of Famine" in 1965, refers to the area of land abroad that is used to grow feed for animals within a country.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><i>Embedded land</i> or "Virtual land", is the land that is effectively embedded in an imported product; i.e. the land used to produce the product (including any <a href="/wiki/Supply_chain" title="Supply chain">supply chain</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Soybean" title="Soybean">soya</a> feed for <a href="/wiki/Meat_production" class="mw-redirect" title="Meat production">beef</a>).</li>
<li><i>Global cropland</i>, as used in the <a href="/wiki/International_Resource_Panel" title="International Resource Panel">International Resource Panel</a> report on global <a href="/wiki/Land_use" title="Land use">land use</a>, is part of the total land footprint, but doesn't include e.g. <a href="/wiki/Built-up_area" title="Built-up area">built-up land</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Indirect_land_use_change_impacts_of_biofuels" title="Indirect land use change impacts of biofuels">Indirect land use change</a></i>, used in the debate on <a href="/wiki/Biofuels" class="mw-redirect" title="Biofuels">biofuels</a>, is a related concept, that looks at the land needed to grow biofuels, then considers the indirect impacts that this use of land may have on other land use in the country concerned.</li></ul>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ecological_footprint" title="Ecological footprint">Ecological footprint</a> is measured using a different approach, with two key differences to land footprint:
</p>
<ul><li>Ecological footprint adds together both real land use and an calculated (but not really existing) area of forest to absorb CO<small>2</small> emissions, to incorporate part of the impacts of climate change. In contrast, land footprint looks only at real land use, and is often used in association with <a href="/wiki/Carbon_footprint" title="Carbon footprint">carbon footprint</a> to cover all climate changing emissions.</li>
<li>Ecological footprint adjusts land areas to <a href="/wiki/Global_hectares" class="mw-redirect" title="Global hectares">global hectares</a>, while land footprint is based on real land area - or an estimate of it.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research">Research</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Land_footprint&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Research">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The International Resource Panel has produced a detailed report on land, "Assessing global land use: Balancing consumption with sustainable supply",<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> which establishes a <a href="/wiki/Planetary_boundary" class="mw-redirect" title="Planetary boundary">planetary boundary</a> for cropland, and proposes a potential safe operating space target of 0.2 ha of cropland per person per year. This is in contrast to the current EU use of cropland of around 0.31 ha per person per year.
</p><p>The most detailed study of Europe's land footprint (including forestry) and how this is traded with the rest of the work was published by Sustainable European Research Institute (SERI) in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> A briefing on this study is available from <a href="/wiki/Friends_of_the_Earth_Europe" title="Friends of the Earth Europe">Friends of the Earth Europe</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A study of Germany's land balance<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> modelled the impact of changes in diet and consumption of stimulants (e.g. coffee, wine etc.) on Germany's land balance. It found that a switch to a healthier diet, with less meat and with reduced stimulants, could lead to no net land imports, while a switch to vegetarian or vegan diets would lead to Germany being a net exporter of land.
</p><p>Sustainable Europe Research Institute have also examined potential scenarios for reducing Europe's land footprint, including dietary changes.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_policy">In policy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Land_footprint&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: In policy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The EU's 7th Environmental action plan <sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> calls for examination of land (and other) footprints:
</p>
<dl><dd>"To set a framework for action to improve resource efficiency aspects beyond GHG emissions and energy, targets for reducing the overall lifecycle environmental impact of consumption will be set.. Indicators and targets for land, water, material and carbon footprints as well as their role within the European Semester should also be considered in this regard."</dd></dl>
<p>The European Parliament's own initiative report on "Resource efficiency: moving towards a circular economy",<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> which was voted on 9 July 2015, includes the following text calling for action from the European Commission on land and other footprints:
</p>
<dl><dd>"Urges the Commission to propose, by the end of 2015, a lead indicator and a dashboard of sub-indicators on resource efficiency, including ecosystem services; points out that the use of these harmonised indicators should be legally binding as of 2018, and they should measure <a href="/wiki/Resource_consumption" title="Resource consumption">resource consumption</a>, including imports and exports, at EU, Member State and industry level and take account of the whole lifecycle of products and services and should be based on the footprint methodology, measuring at least land, water and material use and carbon;"</dd></dl>
<p>A study of the four resource footprint indicators, carried for the European Commission<sup id="cite_ref-europa1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-europa1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> examined what data was available to measure the land footprint, carbon footprint, water footprint &amp; material footprint at economy level. This study concluded that Land Footprint needed methodological improvement and harmonisation, and that a common, international, multi-regional input output database would make it easier to calculate all four footprints.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Friends_of_the_Earth_Europe" title="Friends of the Earth Europe">Friends of the Earth Europe</a>, together with a wider Land Footprint Coalition,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> has been advocating for Land Footprint to be used to measure resource consumption, as part of a wider project that argues that four footprints - land, <a href="/wiki/Water_footprint" title="Water footprint">water</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carbon_footprint" title="Carbon footprint">carbon</a> &amp; material (equivalent to either RMC or TMC, see <a href="/wiki/Material_Flow_Accounting" class="mw-redirect" title="Material Flow Accounting">Material Flow Accounting</a>) - are an effective way of measuring &amp; managing Europe's resource use.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The coalition, which includes <a href="/wiki/Slow_Food" title="Slow Food">Slow Food Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Birdlife" class="mw-redirect" title="Birdlife">Birdlife</a>, are also calling for Europe to reduce its land footprint.
</p>
