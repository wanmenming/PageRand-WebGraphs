<p><b>Manganese(IV) oxide</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Inorganic_compound" title="Inorganic compound">inorganic compound</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Chemical_formula" title="Chemical formula">formula</a> <span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span>. This blackish or brown solid occurs naturally as the mineral <a href="/wiki/Pyrolusite" title="Pyrolusite">pyrolusite</a>, which is the main ore of <a href="/wiki/Manganese" title="Manganese">manganese</a> and a component of <a href="/wiki/Manganese_nodule" title="Manganese nodule">manganese nodules</a>. The principal use for <span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> is for dry-cell <a href="/wiki/Battery_(electricity)" class="mw-redirect" title="Battery (electricity)">batteries</a>, such as the <a href="/wiki/Alkaline_battery" title="Alkaline battery">alkaline battery</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Zinc-carbon_battery" class="mw-redirect" title="Zinc-carbon battery">zinc-carbon battery</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-G&amp;E_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-G&amp;E-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> is also used as a <a href="/wiki/Pigment" title="Pigment">pigment</a> and as a precursor to other manganese compounds, such as <span class="chemf nowrap"><a href="/wiki/Potassium_permanganate" title="Potassium permanganate">KMnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">4</sub></span></a></span>.  It is used as a <a href="/wiki/Reagent" title="Reagent">reagent</a> in <a href="/wiki/Organic_synthesis" title="Organic synthesis">organic synthesis</a>, for example, for the oxidation of <a href="/wiki/Allylic" class="mw-redirect" title="Allylic">allylic</a> <a href="/wiki/Alcohol" title="Alcohol">alcohols</a>. <span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> in the α polymorph can incorporate a variety of atoms (as well as water molecules) in the "tunnels" or "channels" between the manganese oxide octahedra. There is considerable interest in <span class="chemf nowrap">α-MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> as a possible cathode for <a href="/wiki/Lithium_ion_battery" class="mw-redirect" title="Lithium ion battery">lithium ion batteries</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manganese_dioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Several <a href="/wiki/Polymorphism_(materials_science)" title="Polymorphism (materials science)">polymorphs</a> of <span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> are claimed, as well as a hydrated form. Like many other dioxides, <span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> crystallizes in the <a href="/wiki/Rutile" title="Rutile">rutile</a> <a href="/wiki/Crystal_structure" title="Crystal structure">crystal structure</a> (this polymorph is called <span class="chemf nowrap">β-MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span>), with three-coordinate oxide and octahedral metal centres.<sup id="cite_ref-G&amp;E_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-G&amp;E-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  <span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> is characteristically <a href="/wiki/Nonstoichiometric_compound" class="mw-redirect" title="Nonstoichiometric compound">nonstoichiometric</a>, being deficient in oxygen. The complicated <a href="/wiki/Solid-state_chemistry" title="Solid-state chemistry">solid-state chemistry</a> of this material is relevant to the lore of "freshly prepared" <span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> in <a href="/wiki/Organic_synthesis" title="Organic synthesis">organic synthesis</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The α-polymorph of <span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> has a very open structure with "channels" which can accommodate metal atoms such as silver or barium. <span class="chemf nowrap">α-MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> is often called <a href="/wiki/Hollandite" title="Hollandite">Hollandite</a>, after a closely related mineral.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manganese_dioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Naturally occurring manganese dioxide contains impurities and a considerable amount of <a href="/wiki/Manganese(III)_oxide" title="Manganese(III) oxide">manganese(III) oxide</a>. Only a limited number of deposits contain the γ modification in purity sufficient for the battery industry.
</p><p>Production of <a href="/wiki/Battery_(electricity)" class="mw-redirect" title="Battery (electricity)">batteries</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ferrite_(magnet)" title="Ferrite (magnet)">ferrite</a> (two of the primary uses of manganese dioxide) requires high purity manganese dioxide. Batteries require "electrolytic manganese dioxide" while ferrites require "chemical manganese dioxide".<sup id="cite_ref-ChiuZMnO2_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ChiuZMnO2-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chemical_manganese_dioxide">Chemical manganese dioxide</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manganese_dioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Chemical manganese dioxide">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>One method starts with natural manganese dioxide and converts it using <a href="/wiki/Dinitrogen_tetroxide" title="Dinitrogen tetroxide">dinitrogen tetroxide</a> and water to a <a href="/wiki/Manganese(II)_nitrate" title="Manganese(II) nitrate">manganese(II) nitrate</a> solution. Evaporation of the water, leaves the crystalline nitrate salt. At temperatures of 400&#160;°C, the salt decomposes, releasing <span class="chemf nowrap">N<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">4</sub></span></span> and leaving a residue of purified manganese dioxide.<sup id="cite_ref-ChiuZMnO2_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ChiuZMnO2-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  These two steps can be summarized as:
</p>
<dl><dd><span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span>  +  <span class="chemf nowrap">N<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">4</sub></span></span>  ⇌   <span class="chemf nowrap">Mn(NO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span>)<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span></dd></dl>
<p>In another process manganese dioxide is carbothermically reduced to <a href="/wiki/Manganese(II)_oxide" title="Manganese(II) oxide">manganese(II) oxide</a> which is dissolved in <a href="/wiki/Sulfuric_acid" title="Sulfuric acid">sulfuric acid</a>. The filtered solution is treated with <a href="/wiki/Ammonium_carbonate" title="Ammonium carbonate">ammonium carbonate</a> to precipitate <span class="chemf nowrap">MnCO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span>. The carbonate is <a href="/wiki/Calcined" class="mw-redirect" title="Calcined">calcined</a> in air to give a mixture of manganese(II) and manganese(IV) oxides. To complete the process, a suspension of this material in sulfuric acid is treated with <a href="/wiki/Sodium_chlorate" title="Sodium chlorate">sodium chlorate</a>. <a href="/wiki/Chloric_acid" title="Chloric acid">Chloric acid</a>, which forms in situ, converts any Mn(III) and Mn(II) oxides to the dioxide, releasing chlorine as a by-product.<sup id="cite_ref-ChiuZMnO2_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ChiuZMnO2-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A third process involves <a href="/wiki/Manganese_heptoxide" title="Manganese heptoxide">manganese heptoxide</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manganese_monoxide" class="mw-redirect" title="Manganese monoxide">manganese monoxide</a>. The two reagents combine with a 1:3 ratio to form manganese dioxide:
</p>
<dl><dd><span class="chemf nowrap">Mn<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">7</sub></span></span> + 3 MnO → 5 <span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span></dd></dl>

</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Electrolytic_manganese_dioxide">Electrolytic manganese dioxide</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manganese_dioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Electrolytic manganese dioxide">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Electrolytic manganese dioxide (EMD) is used in <a href="/wiki/Zinc%E2%80%93carbon_battery" title="Zinc–carbon battery">zinc–carbon batteries</a> together with <a href="/wiki/Zinc_chloride" title="Zinc chloride">zinc chloride</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ammonium_chloride" title="Ammonium chloride">ammonium chloride</a>. EMD is commonly used in zinc manganese dioxide rechargeable alkaline (Zn RAM) cells also. For these applications, purity is extremely important. EMD is produced in a similar fashion as <a href="/wiki/Copper_extraction_techniques#Electrorefining" class="mw-redirect" title="Copper extraction techniques">electrolytic tough pitch (ETP) copper</a>: The manganese dioxide is dissolved in <a href="/wiki/Sulfuric_acid" title="Sulfuric acid">sulfuric acid</a> (sometimes mixed with <a href="/wiki/Manganese_sulfate" class="mw-redirect" title="Manganese sulfate">manganese sulfate</a>) and subjected to a current between two electrodes. The MnO2 dissolves, enters solution as the sulfate, and is deposited on the anode.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reactions">Reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manganese_dioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The important reactions of <span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> are associated with its redox, both oxidation and reduction.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reduction">Reduction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manganese_dioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reduction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> is the principal  <a href="/wiki/Precursor_(chemistry)" title="Precursor (chemistry)">precursor</a> to <a href="/wiki/Ferromanganese" title="Ferromanganese">ferromanganese</a> and related alloys, which are widely used in the steel industry. </p>
<dl><dd><span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span>  +  2 C   →  Mn  +  2 CO</dd></dl>
<p>The key reactions of <span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> in batteries is the one-electron reduction:
</p>
<dl><dd><span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span>  +  e<sup>−</sup>  +  <span class="chemf nowrap">H<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">+</sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sub></span></span>   →  MnO(OH)</dd></dl>
<p><span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> <a href="/wiki/Catalysis" title="Catalysis">catalyses</a> several reactions that form <span class="chemf nowrap">O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span>. </p>
<dl><dd>2 <span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup <dl><dd>2 <span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span>  +  4 KOH  +   <span class="chemf nowrap">O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span>  →  2 <span class="chemf nowrap">K<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">4</sub></span></span>  +   2 <span class="chemf nowrap">H<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>O</span></dd></dl>

</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Applications">Applications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manganese_dioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Applications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The predominant application of <span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> is as a component of <a href="/wiki/Dry_cell" title="Dry cell">dry cell</a> batteries, so called <a href="/wiki/Leclanch%C3%A9_cell" title="Leclanché cell">Leclanché cell</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Zinc%E2%80%93carbon_battery" title="Zinc–carbon battery">zinc–carbon batteries</a>.  Approximately 500,000&#160;<a href="/wiki/Tonne" title="Tonne">tonnes</a> are consumed for this application annually.<sup id="cite_ref-Ullmann_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ullmann-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  Other industrial applications include the use of <span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> as an inorganic <a href="/wiki/Pigment" title="Pigment">pigment</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ceramic" title="Ceramic">ceramics</a> and in <a href="/wiki/Glassmaking" class="mw-redirect" title="Glassmaking">glassmaking</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Organic_synthesis">Organic synthesis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manganese_dioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Organic synthesis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A specialized use of manganese dioxide is as oxidant in <a href="/wiki/Organic_synthesis" title="Organic synthesis">organic synthesis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The effectiveness of the reagent depends on the method of preparation, a problem that is typical for other heterogeneous reagents where surface area, among other variables, is a significant factor.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The mineral <a href="/wiki/Pyrolusite" title="Pyrolusite">pyrolusite</a> makes a poor reagent. Usually, however, the reagent is generated in situ by treatment of an aqueous solution <span class="chemf nowrap">KMnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">4</sub></span></span> with a Mn(II) salt, typically the sulfate.  <span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> oxidizes <a href="/wiki/Allylic" class="mw-redirect" title="Allylic">allylic</a> alcohols to the corresponding <a href="/wiki/Aldehyde" title="Aldehyde">aldehydes</a> or <a href="/wiki/Ketone" title="Ketone">ketones</a>:<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<dl><dd><dl><dd>cis-RCH=<span class="chemf nowrap">CHCH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>OH</span>  +  <span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span>  →  cis-RCH=CHCHO  +  <a href="/wiki/MnO" class="mw-redirect" title="MnO">MnO</a>  +  <span class="chemf nowrap">H<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>O</span></dd></dl></dd></dl>
<p>The configuration of the <a href="/wiki/Double_bond" title="Double bond">double bond</a> is conserved in the reaction. The corresponding <a href="/wiki/Acetylenic" title="Acetylenic">acetylenic</a> alcohols are also suitable substrates, although the resulting <a href="/wiki/Propargylic" class="mw-redirect" title="Propargylic">propargylic</a> aldehydes can be quite reactive. <a href="/wiki/Benzylic" class="mw-redirect" title="Benzylic">Benzylic</a> and even unactivated alcohols are also good substrates. 1,2-<a href="/wiki/Diol" title="Diol">Diols</a> are cleaved by <span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> to <a href="/wiki/Dialdehyde" class="mw-redirect" title="Dialdehyde">dialdehydes</a> or <a href="/wiki/Diketone" title="Diketone">diketones</a>. Otherwise, the applications of <span class="chemf nowrap">MnO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> are numerous, being applicable to many kinds of reactions including <a href="/wiki/Amine" title="Amine">amine</a> oxidation, aromatization, <a href="/wiki/Oxidative_coupling" title="Oxidative coupling">oxidative coupling</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Thiol" title="Thiol">thiol</a> oxidation.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hazards">Hazards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manganese_dioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Hazards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Chronic exposures to contaminated air with manganese dioxide particles can cause <a href="/wiki/Manganism" title="Manganism">manganism</a>.  MnO2 is classed as a cumulative neurotoxin according to the MSDS for Manganese Dioxide.
</p>
