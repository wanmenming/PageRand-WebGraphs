<p><i><b>The Periodic Table of Videos</b></i> (usually shortened to <i><b>Periodic Videos</b></i>) is a series of videos about <a href="/wiki/Chemical_elements" class="mw-redirect" title="Chemical elements">chemical elements</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Periodic_table" title="Periodic table">periodic table</a>. They are published on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> and produced by <a href="/wiki/Brady_Haran" title="Brady Haran">Brady Haran</a>, a former <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> video journalist, featuring Sir <a href="/wiki/Martyn_Poliakoff" title="Martyn Poliakoff">Martyn Poliakoff</a> ("The Professor"), Peter Licence, <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Liddle" title="Stephen Liddle">Stephen Liddle</a>, Debbie Kays, Neil Barnes, Sam Tang and others at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Nottingham" title="University of Nottingham">University of Nottingham</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-arstechnica_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-arstechnica-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Periodic_Videos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The project began recording on 9 June 2008 and the initial videos were completed on 17 July 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-professor1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-professor1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The collection includes videos, each just a few minutes long, for all 118 known elements with a video for each element, as well as many additional supplemental chemistry videos. The 118 element videos and introduction videos were all shot unscripted in June and July 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-acs08_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acs08-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Since the initial videos were completed in 2008 the team has been refining and uploading revised versions of the videos with new video and in higher resolutions.<sup id="cite_ref-acs08_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acs08-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> A key example of this revising is with the <a href="/wiki/Xenon" title="Xenon">xenon</a> video that was redone in honour of professor <a href="/wiki/Neil_Bartlett_(chemist)" title="Neil Bartlett (chemist)">Neil Bartlett</a> who died on 5 August 2008;<sup id="cite_ref-acs08_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acs08-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Bartlett prepared one of the first xenon compounds, <a href="/wiki/Xenon_hexafluoroplatinate" title="Xenon hexafluoroplatinate">xenon hexafluoroplatinate</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-acs08_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acs08-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Content">Content</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Periodic_Videos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Content">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Poliakoff is the most visible presenter on the videos and his hair, reminiscent of <a href="/wiki/Einstein" class="mw-redirect" title="Einstein">Einstein</a> or a <a href="/wiki/Mad_scientist" title="Mad scientist">mad scientist</a>, is frequently commented upon in the videos.<sup id="cite_ref-acs08_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acs08-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The combination of the professor's hair and amusing experiments has made these videos quite popular.<sup id="cite_ref-acs08_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acs08-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Although uncertain what to think about the attention given to his hair, Professor Poliakoff is excited with the success of the videos, stating "With a few hours of work, I have lectured to more students than I have reached in my entire career."<sup id="cite_ref-acs08_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acs08-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The YouTube channel as of December 2017, has over 1 million subscribers and the videos have surpassed 170 million views.<sup id="cite_ref-professor1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-professor1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-YouTube_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-YouTube-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The YouTube channel is now one of the most popular chemistry related channels on all of YouTube.<sup id="cite_ref-EPSRCvideo_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EPSRCvideo-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The producers of the videos have received praise from Nobel Laureates, chemistry professors, and the general public, says Professor Poliakoff.<sup id="cite_ref-acs08_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acs08-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Chemistry Nobel Laureate <a href="/wiki/Roald_Hoffmann" title="Roald Hoffmann">Roald Hoffmann</a> has even offered his praise of the videos, stating they "are like the best reality show I've ever seen – the universe revealing itself, element by element."<sup id="cite_ref-acs08_4-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acs08-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The videos feature various experiments and demonstrations of the elements, some too dangerous to be performed in a classroom.<sup id="cite_ref-acs08_4-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acs08-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BBC1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Though the presenters take appropriate precautions when doing such experiments and provide adequate warnings, some scientists have criticized the dangerous experiments fearing people might try them at home and get hurt.<sup id="cite_ref-acs08_4-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acs08-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The intent of the videos is to bring chemistry to a new generation of students and to get them enthused about science and understand how chemists think and what chemists are trying to do.<sup id="cite_ref-cbs_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbs-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-telegraph1_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph1-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Many school teachers now incorporate these videos into their classes,<sup id="cite_ref-professor1_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-professor1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cbs_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbs-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and the professor has even recorded video responses to some of the students' questions.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Some of the most popular videos are those of <a href="/wiki/Sodium" title="Sodium">sodium</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-telegraph1_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph1-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Potassium" title="Potassium">potassium</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Uranium" title="Uranium">uranium</a>.
</p><p><i>The Periodic Table of Videos</i> team has had two live performances to date, the first in May 2009 at the <a href="/wiki/Broadway_Media_Centre" class="mw-redirect" title="Broadway Media Centre">Broadway Media Centre</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nottingham" title="Nottingham">Nottingham</a>, and most recently in July 2010 at the <a href="/wiki/EuroScience" title="EuroScience">EuroScience</a> Open Forum (ESOF) in <a href="/wiki/Turin" title="Turin">Turin</a>, Italy.<sup id="cite_ref-professor1_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-professor1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Financing">Financing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Periodic_Videos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Financing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A grant from the <a href="/wiki/Engineering_and_Physical_Sciences_Research_Council" title="Engineering and Physical Sciences Research Council">Engineering and Physical Sciences Research Council</a> of £25,249 was awarded on 19 January, 2010 to extend the video library to include topical videos on molecules of general interest.<sup id="cite_ref-EPSRCvideo_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EPSRCvideo-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-grant_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grant-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The first of these new videos were on <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a> and <a href="/wiki/Methane" title="Methane">methane</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-EPSRCvideo_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EPSRCvideo-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Videos">Videos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Periodic_Videos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Videos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>The Periodic Table of Videos</i> has filmed at least one video for each of the 118 elements (from <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen" title="Hydrogen">hydrogen</a> to <a href="/wiki/Oganesson" title="Oganesson">oganesson</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> They have also filmed several videos that discuss molecules such as D<sub>2</sub>O (<a href="/wiki/Heavy_water" title="Heavy water">heavy water</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Sulfuric_acid" title="Sulfuric acid">sulfuric acid</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Also filmed are "Chem definitions" that provide an explanation to words that are used in chemistry. Lastly, the team has filmed "Roadtrips" where they travel to different places in the world that have an importance in chemistry (such as the mine outside <a href="/wiki/Ytterby" title="Ytterby">Ytterby</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, which had four elements&#160;– <a href="/wiki/Yttrium" title="Yttrium">yttrium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Terbium" title="Terbium">terbium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Erbium" title="Erbium">erbium</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ytterbium" title="Ytterbium">ytterbium</a>&#160;– named after it.)
</p>
