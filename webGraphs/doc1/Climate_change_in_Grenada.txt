<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Climate change in Grenada</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>
				<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The issue of <b><a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a></b> has received significant public and political attention in <b><a href="/wiki/Grenada" title="Grenada">Grenada</a></b>. As of  2013<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Climate_change_in_Grenada&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, the mitigation of its effects has been high on the agenda of the Government of Grenada, which seeks to set an example through innovation and green technology.<sup id="cite_ref-washdiplo_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-washdiplo-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Given its small size, Grenada is not a major contributor to greenhouse gas emissions, but does use fossil fuel to generate 90% of its electricity. The Government of Grenada has set a goal of generating 50% of its energy from solar and wind power by 2030, and is taking steps to abolish Grenlec, the state-run electric utility. Because tourism is a mainstay of the economy, there is also interest in exploring the use of seawater for air-conditioning.<sup id="cite_ref-washdiplo_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-washdiplo-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of  2013<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Climate_change_in_Grenada&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, Grenada had a US$6.9 million pilot project to adapt its irrigation system to <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a> and conduct local and regional water planning, funded by the German International Climate Initiative (IKI). <a href="/wiki/Groundwater" title="Groundwater">Groundwater</a> depletion, lower water tables, disruption of water supply by hurricanes (such as <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ivan" title="Hurricane Ivan">Hurricane Ivan</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Saltwater_intrusion" title="Saltwater intrusion">saltwater intrusion</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Current_sea_level_rise" class="mw-redirect" title="Current sea level rise">rising sea levels</a> pose challenges for providing a consistent water supply for agriculture and tourism.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013, the newspaper <a href="/wiki/The_Washington_Diplomat" title="The Washington Diplomat">The Washington Diplomat</a> profiled Grenada's ambassador to the United States, Angus Friday, who has served as a "senior climate policy specialist at the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a>." In his earlier posting as Grenadian Ambassador to the United Nations, "he frequently advocated for small Caribbean and Pacific island nations threatened by rising ocean levels."<sup id="cite_ref-washdiplo_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-washdiplo-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
