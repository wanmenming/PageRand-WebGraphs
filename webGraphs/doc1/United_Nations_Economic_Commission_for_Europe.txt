<p>The <b>United Nations Economic Commission for Europe</b> (<b>UNECE</b> or <b>ECE</b>) was established in 1947<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-UN_ARES46I_page2_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UN_ARES46I_page2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> to encourage economic cooperation among its member States. It is one of five regional commissions under the administrative direction of United Nations headquarters. It has 56 member States,<sup id="cite_ref-unece_member_States_representatives_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-unece_member_States_representatives-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and reports to the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Economic_and_Social_Council" title="United Nations Economic and Social Council">United Nations Economic and Social Council</a> (ECOSOC). Besides countries in Europe, it also includes Canada, the Central Asian republics, Israel and the United States of America. The UNECE secretariat headquarters is in <a href="/wiki/Geneva" title="Geneva">Geneva</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>, and has an approximate budget of <a href="/wiki/US$" class="mw-redirect" title="US$">US$</a>50 million.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Committee_on_Economic_Cooperation_and_Integration">Committee on Economic Cooperation and Integration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Economic_Commission_for_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Committee on Economic Cooperation and Integration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This Committee promotes a policy, financial and regulatory environment conducive to economic growth, innovative development and higher competitiveness in the UNECE region, focusing mainly on countries with economies in transition. Its main areas of work are innovation and competitiveness policies, intellectual property, financing innovative development, entrepreneurship and enterprise development, and public-private partnerships.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Committee_on_Environmental_Policy">Committee on Environmental Policy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Economic_Commission_for_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Committee on Environmental Policy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>UNECE's concern with problems of the environment dates back at least to 1971, when the group of Senior Advisors to the UNECE governments on environmental issues was created which led to the establishment of the Committee on Environmental Policy, which now meets annually. The Committee provides collective policy direction in the area of environment and sustainable development, prepares ministerial meetings, develops international environmental law and supports international initiatives in the region. CEP works to support countries to enhance their environmental governance and transboundary cooperation as well as strengthen implementation of the UNECE regional environmental commitments and advance sustainable development in the region.
</p><p>Its main aim is to assess countries' efforts to reduce their overall pollution burden and manage their natural resources, to integrate environmental and socioeconomic policies, to strengthen cooperation with the international community, to harmonize environmental conditions and policies throughout the region and to stimulate greater involvement of the public and environmental discussions and decision-making.
</p><p>CEP is the overall governing body of UNECE environmental activities. The Committee's work is based on several strategic pillars:<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<ul><li>Providing the secretariat to the "Environment for Europe" process and participating in the regional promotion of Agenda 21;</li>
<li>Developing and carrying-out of <a href="/wiki/UNECE_Environmental_Performance_Reviews" title="UNECE Environmental Performance Reviews">UNECE Environmental Performance Reviews</a> in the UNECE countries non-members of OECD;<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Overseeing UNECE activities on <a href="/wiki/Environmental_monitoring" title="Environmental monitoring">environmental monitoring</a>, assessment and reporting;</li>
<li>Increasing the overall effectiveness of UNECE multilateral environmental agreements (MEAs) and facilitating the exchange of experience on MEAs' implementation. See UNECE <a href="/wiki/Espoo_Convention" class="mw-redirect" title="Espoo Convention">Espoo Convention</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aarhus_Convention" title="Aarhus Convention">Aarhus Convention</a>, <a href="/wiki/Convention_on_Long-Range_Transboundary_Air_Pollution" title="Convention on Long-Range Transboundary Air Pollution">Convention on Long-Range Transboundary Air Pollution</a>, <a href="/wiki/Convention_on_the_Protection_and_Use_of_Transboundary_Watercourses_and_International_Lakes" title="Convention on the Protection and Use of Transboundary Watercourses and International Lakes">Convention on the Protection and Use of Transboundary Watercourses and International Lakes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Convention_on_the_Transboundary_Effects_of_Industrial_Accidents" title="Convention on the Transboundary Effects of Industrial Accidents">Convention on the Transboundary Effects of Industrial Accidents</a>.</li>
<li>Participating and/or facilitating the exchange of experience in a number of cross-sectoral activities undertaken under the leadership of UNECE (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Education_for_sustainable_development" title="Education for sustainable development">education for sustainable development</a>, transport, health and environment, green building), or in partnership with other organizations (e.g. environment and security initiative, European environment and health process).</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Committee_on_Housing_and_Land_Management">Committee on Housing and Land Management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Economic_Commission_for_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Committee on Housing and Land Management">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1947, UNECE set up a Panel on Housing Problems, which later evolved into the Committee on Human Settlements and after the reform in 2005/2006 into the Committee on Housing and Land Management. The Committee is an intergovernmental body of all UNECE member States. It provides a forum for the compilation, dissemination and exchange of information and experience on housing, urban development, and land administration policies; &amp; in areas such as <a href="/wiki/Birmingham" title="Birmingham">Birmingham</a>, a more fiscal issue-<a href="/wiki/UK" class="mw-redirect" title="UK">UK</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Inland_Transport_Committee">Inland Transport Committee</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Economic_Commission_for_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Inland Transport Committee">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The UNECE Transport Division has been providing secretariat services to the <a href="/wiki/World_Forum_for_Harmonization_of_Vehicle_Regulations" title="World Forum for Harmonization of Vehicle Regulations">World Forum for Harmonization of Vehicle Regulations</a> (WP.29). In addition to acting as secretariat to the World Forum, the Vehicle Regulations and Transport Innovations section serves as the secretariat of the Administrative Committee for the coordination of work, and of the Administrative/Executives Committees of the three Agreements on vehicles administered by the World Forum.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conference_of_European_Statisticians">Conference of European Statisticians</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Economic_Commission_for_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Conference of European Statisticians">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The UNECE <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.unece.org/stats/">Statistical Division</a> provides the secretariat for the Conference and its expert groups, and implements the statistical work programme of the UNECE. The Conference brings together chief statisticians from national and international statistical organizations around the world, meaning that the word "European" in its name is no longer an accurate description of its geographical coverage. The Statistical Division helps member countries to strengthen their statistical systems, and coordinates international statistical activities in the UNECE region and beyond through the Conference and its Bureau, and the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www1.unece.org/stat/platform/display/disaarchive">Database of International Statistical Activities</a>. The Statistical Division develops guidelines and <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www1.unece.org/stat/platform/display/TRAINSTATS">training materials</a> on statistical methodology and practices, in response to demands from member countries. It works with different groups of specialists from national and international statistical organizations, and organizes meetings and on-line forums for statistical experts to exchange experiences on a wide range of topics. The UNECE Statistical Division also provides technical assistance to South-East European, East European, Caucasus and Central Asian countries.
</p><p>The division also provides:
</p><p>1) <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://w3.unece.org/pxweb/">Free on-line data</a> on the 56 UNECE member countries in Europe, Central Asia and North America in both English and Russian, on economic, gender, forestry and transport statistics.
</p><p>2) A biennial overview of key statistics for member countries: <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120424035214/http://www.unece.org/stats/profiles2011.html">UNECE Countries in Figures</a>.
</p><p>3) A <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www1.unece.org/stat/platform">set of wikis</a> to support collaboration activities and disseminate information about good practices.
</p><p>UNECE conducted the <span id="Fertility_and_Family_Survey">Fertility and Family Survey</span> in the 1990s in 23 member States, with over 150,000 participants, with hundreds of resulting scientific publications.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> This activity has hence continued in the form of the <a href="/wiki/Generations_and_Gender_Programme" class="mw-redirect" title="Generations and Gender Programme">Generations and Gender Programme</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
