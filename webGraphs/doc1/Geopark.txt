<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Geopark</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:International_Network_of_Geoparks_map.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/International_Network_of_Geoparks_map.jpg/220px-International_Network_of_Geoparks_map.jpg" width="220" height="110" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/International_Network_of_Geoparks_map.jpg/330px-International_Network_of_Geoparks_map.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/International_Network_of_Geoparks_map.jpg/440px-International_Network_of_Geoparks_map.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1000" data-file-height="500" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:International_Network_of_Geoparks_map.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>World locator map for geoparks included in the <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> <a href="/wiki/Global_Geoparks_Network" title="Global Geoparks Network">Global Geoparks Network</a> (GGN) as of 2008</div></div></div>
<p>A <b>geopark</b> is a unified area that advances the protection and use of geological heritage in a sustainable way, and promotes the economic well-being of the people who live there.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> There are global geoparks and national geoparks.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Concepts">Concepts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geopark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Concepts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A UNESCO definition of <i>global geopark</i> is a unified area with a geological heritage of international significance.<sup id="cite_ref-globalgeopark_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-globalgeopark-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Geoparks use that heritage to promote awareness of key issues facing society in the context of our dynamic planet. Many geoparks promote awareness of geological <a href="/wiki/Hazard" title="Hazard">hazards</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcanoes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Earthquake" title="Earthquake">earthquakes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tsunami" title="Tsunami">tsunamis</a> and many help prepare <a href="/wiki/Disaster" title="Disaster">disaster</a> mitigation strategies with <a href="/wiki/Local_community" title="Local community">local communities</a>. Geoparks embody records of past climate changes and are indicators of current <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate changes</a> as well as demonstrating a "best practise" approach to using renewable energy and employing the best standards of "<a href="/wiki/Green_tourism" class="mw-redirect" title="Green tourism">green tourism</a>". <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">Tourism</a> industry promotion in geoparks, as a geographically sustainable and applicable tourism model, aims to sustain, and even enhance, the geographical character of a place.
</p><p>Geoparks also inform about the sustainable use and need for <a href="/wiki/Natural_resource" title="Natural resource">natural resources</a>, whether they are mined, quarried or harnessed from the surrounding <a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">environment</a> while at the same time promoting respect for the environment and the integrity of the <a href="/wiki/Landscape" title="Landscape">landscape</a>. Geoparks are not a legislative designation though the key heritage sites within a geopark are often protected under local, regional or national legislation.<sup id="cite_ref-globalgeopark_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-globalgeopark-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The multidisciplinary nature of the concept of geopark and tourism promotion in geoparks differentiates itself from other models of <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_tourism" title="Sustainable tourism">sustainable tourism</a>. In fact, sustainable tourism promotion within geoparks encompasses many of the features of sustainable tourism including geo-tourism (geo-site tourism: as a basic factor), community-based tourism and integrated rural tourism (as a vital need), <a href="/wiki/Ecotourism" title="Ecotourism">ecotourism</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cultural_heritage" title="Cultural heritage">cultural heritage</a> tourism.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Global_Network_and_UNESCO">Global Network and UNESCO</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geopark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Global Network and UNESCO">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Global_Geoparks_Network" title="Global Geoparks Network">Global Geoparks Network</a> (GGN) is supported by <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Educational,_Scientific_and_Cultural_Organization" class="mw-redirect" title="United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization">United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization</a> (UNESCO). Many national geoparks and other local geoparks projects also exist which are not included in the Global Geoparks Network.
</p><p>The geoparks initiative was launched by UNESCO in response to the perceived need for an international initiative that recognizes sites representing an <a href="/wiki/Earth_science" title="Earth science">earth science</a> interest.<sup id="cite_ref-UNESCO_Geopark_About_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNESCO_Geopark_About-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Global Geoparks Network  aims at enhancing the value of such sites while at the same time creating <a href="/wiki/Employment" title="Employment">employment</a> and promoting regional <a href="/wiki/Economic_development" title="Economic development">economic development</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-UNESCO_Geopark_About_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNESCO_Geopark_About-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The Global Geoparks Network works in synergy with UNESCO's <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage" class="mw-redirect" title="World Heritage">World Heritage</a> Centre and <a href="/wiki/Man_and_the_Biosphere" class="mw-redirect" title="Man and the Biosphere">Man and the Biosphere</a> (MAB) World Network of Biosphere Reserves.<sup id="cite_ref-UNESCO_Geopark_About_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNESCO_Geopark_About-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualification">Qualification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geopark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Global Geoparks Network (GGN) is a UNESCO activity established in 1998. According to UNESCO, for a geopark to apply to be included in the GGN, it needs to:
</p>
<ul><li>have a management plan designed to foster socio-economic development that is <a href="/wiki/Sustainable" class="mw-redirect" title="Sustainable">sustainable</a> based on <a href="/wiki/Geotourism" title="Geotourism">geotourism</a></li>
<li>demonstrate methods for conserving and enhancing geological heritage and provide means for teaching geoscientific disciplines and broader environmental issues</li>
<li>have joint proposals submitted by public authorities, local communities and private interests acting together, which demonstrate the best practices with respect to Earth heritage conservation and its integration into <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a> strategies.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
