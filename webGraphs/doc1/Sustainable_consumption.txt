<p>As a compliment to analyses of production and its processes, <b>Sustainable Consumption</b> (SC) is the study of resource and energy use (domestic or otherwise). As the term sustainability would imply, those who study SC seek to apply the concept of “continuance”—the capacity to meet both present and future human generational needs.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> SC, then, would also include analyses of efficiency, infrastructure, and waste, as well as access to basic services, green and decent jobs and a better quality of life for all.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It shares a number of common features with and is closely linked to the terms <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_product_development" title="Sustainable product development">sustainable production</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a>. Sustainable consumption as part of <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a> is a prerequisite in the worldwide struggle against <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a> challenges such as <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a>, <a href="/wiki/Resource_depletion" title="Resource depletion">resource depletion</a>, famines or <a href="/wiki/Environmental_pollution" class="mw-redirect" title="Environmental pollution">environmental pollution</a>.
</p><p>Sustainable development as well as sustainable consumption rely on certain premises such as:
</p>
<ul><li>Effective <a href="/wiki/Resource_use" class="mw-redirect" title="Resource use">use of resources</a>, and minimisation of <a href="/wiki/Waste" title="Waste">waste</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pollution" title="Pollution">pollution</a></li>
<li>Use of <a href="/wiki/Renewable_resource" title="Renewable resource">renewable resources</a> within their capacity for renewal</li>
<li>Fuller <a href="/wiki/Product_life-cycle" class="mw-redirect" title="Product life-cycle">product life-cycles</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Intergenerational_equity" title="Intergenerational equity">Intergenerational</a> and intragenerational equity</li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Oslo_definition">The Oslo definition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainable_consumption&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The Oslo definition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The definition proposed by the 1994 Oslo Symposium on Sustainable Consumption defines it as "the use of services and related products which respond to basic needs and bring a better <a href="/wiki/Quality_of_life" title="Quality of life">quality of life</a> while minimizing the use of natural resources and toxic materials as well as emissions of waste and pollutants over the life cycle of the service or product so as not to jeopardize the needs of future generations."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Strong_and_weak_sustainable_consumption">Strong and weak sustainable consumption</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainable_consumption&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Strong and weak sustainable consumption">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-style ambox-Tone" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f2/Edit-clear.svg/40px-Edit-clear.svg.png" width="40" height="40" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f2/Edit-clear.svg/60px-Edit-clear.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f2/Edit-clear.svg/80px-Edit-clear.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="48" data-file-height="48" /></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section's <b>tone or style may not reflect the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Writing_better_articles#Tone" title="Wikipedia:Writing better articles">encyclopedic tone</a> used on Wikipedia</b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> See Wikipedia's <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Writing_better_articles#Tone" title="Wikipedia:Writing better articles">guide to writing better articles</a> for suggestions.</span>  <small><i>(October 2017)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>In order to achieve sustainable consumption, two developments have to take place: it requires both an increase in the efficiency of consumption as well as a change in consumption patterns and reductions in consumption levels in industrialized countries as well as rich social classes in developing countries which have also a large ecological footprint and give examples for increasing middle classes in developing countries<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>. The first prerequisite is not sufficient on its own and can be named weak sustainable consumption. Here, technological improvements and <a href="/wiki/Eco-efficiency" title="Eco-efficiency">eco-efficiency</a> support a necessary reduction in <a href="/wiki/Resource_consumption" title="Resource consumption">resource consumption</a>. Once this aim has been met, the second prerequisite, the change in patterns and reduction of levels of consumption is indispensable. Strong sustainable consumption approaches also pay attention to the social dimension of <a href="/wiki/Well-being" title="Well-being">well-being</a> and assess the need for changes based on a risk-averse perspective.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In order to achieve what can be termed strong sustainable consumption, changes in infrastructures as well as the choices customers have are required. In the political arena, weak sustainable consumption has been discussed whereas strong sustainable consumption is missing from all debates.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The so-called <a href="/wiki/Attitude-behavior_gap" class="mw-redirect" title="Attitude-behavior gap">attitude-behaviour</a> or <a href="/wiki/Values-action_gap" class="mw-redirect" title="Values-action gap">values-action gap</a> describes a significant obstacle to changes in individual customer behavior. Many consumers are well aware of the importance of their consumption choices and care about environmental issues, however, most of them do not translate their concerns into their consumption patterns as the purchase-decision making process is highly complicated and relies on e.g. social, political and psychological factors. Young et al. identified a lack of time for research, high prices, a lack of information and the cognitive effort needed as the main barriers when it comes to <a href="/wiki/Green_consumption" title="Green consumption">green consumption</a> choices.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_conferences_and_programs">Notable conferences and programs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainable_consumption&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Notable conferences and programs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>1992 - At the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Conference_on_Environment_and_Development" class="mw-redirect" title="United Nations Conference on Environment and Development">United Nations Conference on Environment and Development</a> (UNCED) the concept of sustainable consumption was established in chapter 4 of the Agenda 21.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>1994 - Sustainable Consumption Symposium in Oslo</li>
<li>1995 – SC was requested to be incorporated by <a href="/wiki/ECOSOC" class="mw-redirect" title="ECOSOC">UN Economic and Social Council</a> (ECOSOC) into the UN Guidelines on Consumer Protection.</li>
<li>1997 – A major report on SC was produced by the OECD.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>1998 – <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Environment_Program" class="mw-redirect" title="United Nations Environment Program">United Nations Environment Program</a> (UNEP) started a SC program and SC is discussed in the <a href="/wiki/Human_Development_Report" title="Human Development Report">Human Development Report</a> of the <a href="/wiki/UNDP" class="mw-redirect" title="UNDP">UN Development Program</a> (UNDP).<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>2002 – A ten-year program on sustainable consumption and production (SCP) was created in the Plan of Implementation at the <a href="/wiki/Earth_Summit_2002" title="Earth Summit 2002">World Summit on Sustainable Development</a> (WSSD) in Johannesburg.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>2003 - The "<a href="/w/index.php?title=Marrakesh_Process&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Marrakesh Process (page does not exist)">Marrakesh Process</a>" was developed by co-ordination of a series of meetings and other "multi-stakeholder" processes by UNEP and UNDESA following the WSSD.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
