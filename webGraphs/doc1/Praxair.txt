<p><b>Praxair, Inc.</b> is an American worldwide <a href="/wiki/Industrial_gases" class="mw-redirect" title="Industrial gases">industrial gases</a> company. It is the largest industrial gases company in North and South America, and the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_gas#Companies" title="Industrial gas">third-largest worldwide by revenue</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Praxair&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The company was founded by <a href="/wiki/Carl_von_Linde" title="Carl von Linde">Carl von Linde</a> as <b>Linde Air Products Company</b> in 1907. In 1917, it joined with 4 other chemical companies under the name <i><a href="/wiki/Union_Carbide_and_Carbon_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Union Carbide and Carbon Corporation">Union Carbide and Carbon Corporation</a></i>, while maintaining a separate identity.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Its degree of independence waxed and waned over the years, and in 1992, it was spun off as a subsidiary, <i>Union Carbide Industrial Gases Inc.</i>, and renamed to <i>Praxair</i> when it became a formally independent company three years later.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The name is derived from a combination of the Greek word "praxis", or practical application, and "air", the company's primary raw material.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Praxair was the first company in North America to commercialize <a href="/wiki/Cryogenics" title="Cryogenics">cryogenically</a> separated <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygen</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The company introduced the first distribution system for liquid gas in 1917 and developed on-site gas supply in the 1940s.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In the 1960s, Praxair introduced non-cryogenic means of air separation.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In August 2016 it was publicly announced that the American company was in official negotiations to merger with the German competitor <a href="/wiki/The_Linde_Group" title="The Linde Group">Linde Group</a>, which would form the world's largest gas supplier.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The following month in September the negotiations were halted and suspended indefinitely.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> As of mid December 2016 Praxair has resumed negotiations with Linde for a potential merger <sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In late December an official announcement was made that Praxair has reached an agreement to merger with Linde in a deal valued at $35 billion<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> with an overall merger value of $65 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In April, 2017 Linde again rejected a request for a shareholder vote at its annual general meeting in May (from German private-investor association DSW).<sup id="cite_ref-auto_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In May 2017, both companies reached a deal in principle on an all-share merger of equals valued at $75 billion,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> the deal will still need the approval of Praxair's board of directors as well as Linde's management and executive boards<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and if approved, will form a new <a href="/wiki/Holding_company" title="Holding company">holding company</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In early June, both companies agreed to the merger.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
