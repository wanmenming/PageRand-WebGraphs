<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Voluntary Emission Reduction</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">  (Redirected from <a href="/w/index.php?title=Verified_Emission_Reduction&amp;redirect=no" class="mw-redirect" title="Verified Emission Reduction">Verified Emission Reduction</a>)</span></div>
				<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Voluntary Emission Reductions</b> or <b>Verified Emission Reductions</b> (VERs) are a type of <a href="/wiki/Carbon_offset" title="Carbon offset">carbon offset</a> exchanged in the voluntary or over-the-counter market for <a href="/wiki/Carbon_credits" class="mw-redirect" title="Carbon credits">carbon credits</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Verified Emission Reductions are usually certified through a voluntary certification process.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Verified Emission Reductions are usually created by projects which have been verified outside of the <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_Protocol" title="Kyoto Protocol">Kyoto Protocol</a>. One VER is equivalent to 1 tonne of CO2e emissions.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Through these schemes, industries and individuals voluntarily compensate for their emissions or provide an additional contribution to mitigating climate change.
</p><p>VERs may be developed and calculated in compliance with one of several VER standards. These set out rules defining how emission reductions are measured. Standards provide assurance for buyers of VERs.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> At a minimum, all VERs should be verified by an independent third party.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
