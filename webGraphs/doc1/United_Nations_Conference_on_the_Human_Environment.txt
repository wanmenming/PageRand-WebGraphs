<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">United Nations Conference on the Human Environment</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>
				<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">"Stockholm Conference" redirects here. For the socialist conference that took place in 1917, see <a href="/wiki/Third_Zimmerwald_Conference" title="Third Zimmerwald Conference">Third Zimmerwald Conference</a>.</div>
<p>
The <b>United Nations Conference on the Human Environment</b> was held in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> from June 5–16 in 1972.
</p><p>When the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">United Nations General Assembly</a> decided to convene the 1972 Stockholm Conference, at the initiative of the Government of Sweden to host it, UN Secretary-General <a href="/wiki/U_Thant" title="U Thant">U Thant</a> invited <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Strong" title="Maurice Strong">Maurice Strong</a> to lead it as Secretary-General of the Conference, as the Canadian diplomat (under <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Trudeau" title="Pierre Trudeau">Pierre Trudeau</a>) had initiated and already worked for over two years on the project.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Strong_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Strong-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Conference_on_the_Human_Environment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sweden first suggested to the United Nations Economic and Social Council <a href="/wiki/ECOSOC" class="mw-redirect" title="ECOSOC">ECOSOC</a> in 1968 the idea of having a UN conference to focus on human interactions with the environment. ECOSOC passed resolution 1346 supporting the idea. General Assembly Resolution 2398 in 1969 decided to convene a conference in 1972 and mandated a set of reports from the UN secretary-general suggesting that the conference focus on "stimulating and providing guidelines for action by national government and international organizations" facing environmental issues.<sup id="cite_ref-DeSombre_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DeSombre-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Preparations for the conference were extensive, lasting 4 years, including 115 governments, and costing over $30,000,000.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Issues_at_the_Conference">Issues at the Conference</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Conference_on_the_Human_Environment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Issues at the Conference">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Warsaw_Pact" title="Warsaw Pact">Warsaw Pact</a> nations boycotted the conference due to the lack of inclusion of <a href="/wiki/East_Germany" title="East Germany">East Germany</a>, which was not allowed to participate as it was not a full member of the UN.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the conference itself, divisions between developed and developing countries began to emerge. The Chinese delegation proved hostile to the United States at the conference, issuing a 17 point memorandum condemning <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">United States policies in Indochina</a>, as well as around the world. This stance emboldened other developing countries, which made up 70 of the 122 countries attending. Multiple countries including <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> issued statements that were anti-colonial in nature, further worrying the United States delegation. So harsh was the criticism that <a href="/wiki/Rogers_Morton" title="Rogers Morton">Rogers Morton</a>, at that time secretary of the interior, remarked  "I wish the Russians were here", to divert the attention of the Chinese criticisms.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stockholm_Declaration">Stockholm Declaration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Conference_on_the_Human_Environment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Stockholm Declaration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The meeting agreed upon a <a href="/wiki/Declaration_of_the_United_Nations_Conference_on_the_Human_Environment" title="Declaration of the United Nations Conference on the Human Environment">Declaration containing 26 principles concerning the environment and development</a>; an Action Plan with 109 recommendations, and a Resolution.<sup id="cite_ref-Baylis3_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Baylis3-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Principles of the Stockholm Declaration:<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ol><li><a href="/wiki/Human_rights" title="Human rights">Human rights</a> must be asserted, <a href="/wiki/Apartheid" title="Apartheid">apartheid</a> and <a href="/wiki/Colonialism" title="Colonialism">colonialism</a> condemned</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Natural_resources" class="mw-redirect" title="Natural resources">Natural resources</a> must be safeguarded</li>
<li>The Earth's capacity to <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem_services" title="Ecosystem services">produce</a> <a href="/wiki/Renewable_resource" title="Renewable resource">renewable resources</a> must be maintained</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Wildlife" title="Wildlife">Wildlife</a> must be safeguarded</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Non-renewable_resource" title="Non-renewable resource">Non-renewable resources</a> must be shared and not exhausted</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Pollution" title="Pollution">Pollution</a> must not exceed the environment's capacity to clean itself</li>
<li>Damaging <a href="/w/index.php?title=Oceanic_pollution&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Oceanic pollution (page does not exist)">oceanic pollution</a> must be prevented</li>
<li>Development is needed to improve the environment</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Developing_countries" class="mw-redirect" title="Developing countries">Developing countries</a> therefore need assistance</li>
<li>Developing countries need reasonable prices for exports to carry out <a href="/wiki/Environmental_management" class="mw-redirect" title="Environmental management">environmental management</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Environmental_policy" title="Environmental policy">Environment policy</a> must not hamper development</li>
<li>Developing countries need money to develop environmental safeguards</li>
<li>Integrated development planning is needed</li>
<li>Rational planning should resolve conflicts between environment and development</li>
<li>Human settlements must be planned to eliminate <a href="/wiki/Environmental_problems" class="mw-redirect" title="Environmental problems">environmental problems</a></li>
<li>Governments should plan their own appropriate <a href="/wiki/Human_population_planning" title="Human population planning">population policies</a></li>
<li>National institutions must plan development of states' natural resources</li>
<li>Science and technology must be used to improve the environment</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Environmental_education" title="Environmental education">Environmental education</a> is essential</li>
<li>Environmental research must be promoted, particularly in developing countries</li>
<li>States may exploit their resources as they wish but must not endanger others</li>
<li>Compensation is due to states thus endangered</li>
<li>Each nation must establish its own standards</li>
<li>There must be cooperation on international issues</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/International_organization" title="International organization">International organizations</a> should help to improve the environment</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Weapon_of_mass_destruction" title="Weapon of mass destruction">Weapons of mass destruction</a> must be eliminated</li></ol>
<p>One of the seminal issues that emerged from the conference is the recognition for poverty alleviation for protecting the environment. The Indian Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Indira_Gandhi" title="Indira Gandhi">Indira Gandhi</a> in her seminal speech in the conference brought forward the connection between ecological management and <a href="/wiki/Poverty_reduction" title="Poverty reduction">poverty alleviation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Some argue<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> that this conference, and more importantly the scientific conferences preceding it, had a real impact on the environmental policies of the <a href="/wiki/European_Community" class="mw-redirect" title="European Community">European Community</a> (that later became the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>). For example, in 1973, the EU created the Environmental and Consumer Protection Directorate, and composed the first Environmental Action Program. Such increased interest and research collaboration arguably paved the way for further understanding of global warming, which has led to such agreements as the <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_Protocol" title="Kyoto Protocol">Kyoto Protocol</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Paris_Agreement" title="Paris Agreement">Paris Agreement</a>, and has given a foundation of modern environmentalism.
</p>
