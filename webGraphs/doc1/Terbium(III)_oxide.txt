<p><b>Terbium(III) oxide</b>, also known as <b>terbium sesquioxide</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Sesquioxide" title="Sesquioxide">sesquioxide</a> of the rare earth metal <a href="/wiki/Terbium" title="Terbium">terbium</a>, having <a href="/wiki/Chemical_formula" title="Chemical formula">chemical formula</a> <span class="chemf nowrap">Tb<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span>. It is a p-type <a href="/wiki/Semiconductor" title="Semiconductor">semiconductor</a> when doped with <a href="/wiki/Calcium" title="Calcium">calcium</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and may be prepared by the reduction of <a href="/wiki/Terbium(III,IV)_oxide" title="Terbium(III,IV) oxide"><span class="chemf nowrap">Tb<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">4</sub></span>O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">7</sub></span></span></a> in <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen" title="Hydrogen">hydrogen</a> at 1300&#160;°C for 24 hours.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is a p-type <a href="/wiki/Semiconductor" title="Semiconductor">semiconductor</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is a basic oxide and easily dissolved to dilute acids, and then almost colourless terbium salt is formed.
</p>
<dl><dd>Tb<sub>2</sub>O<sub>3</sub> + 6 H<sup>+</sup> → 2 Tb<sup>3+</sup> + 3 H<sub>2</sub>O</dd></dl>
<p>The crystal structure is <a href="/wiki/Cubic_crystal_system" title="Cubic crystal system">cubic</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Lattice_constant" title="Lattice constant">lattice constant</a> is <i>a</i> = 1057 pm.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
