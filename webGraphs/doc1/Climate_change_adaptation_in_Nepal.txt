<p><a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">Climate change</a> (CC) refers "to the change of earth’s global or regional climate over a long period of time, whether due to natural variability or as the result of human activities" <a href="/wiki/IPCC" class="mw-redirect" title="IPCC">IPCC</a>, 2007d:30. By the impact of climate change, as systems become more vulnerable to natural hazards, there is a greater need to develop responses (that is, adjustments in existing practices, processes or structures) that are able to counter potential future disasters. Such a response is known as adaptation to climate change <a href="/wiki/IPCC" class="mw-redirect" title="IPCC">IPCC</a>, 2001b; Smit et al., 1999. <a href="/wiki/Community_forestry" title="Community forestry">Community forest</a> user group <a href="/wiki/Community_forestry" title="Community forestry">(CFUG)</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is main areas to act climate change adaptation in Nepal.
</p>

<h2><span id="Nepal.27s_National_Adaptation_Programme_of_Action_.28NAPA.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Nepal's_National_Adaptation_Programme_of_Action_(NAPA)">Nepal's National Adaptation Programme of Action (NAPA)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Climate_change_adaptation_in_Nepal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Nepal&#039;s National Adaptation Programme of Action (NAPA)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2010, the Government of Nepal approved <a href="/wiki/National_Adaptation_Programme_of_Action" title="National Adaptation Programme of Action">National Adaptation Programme of Action</a> (NAPA). NAPA developed as a requirement under the UNFCCC to access funding for the most urgent and immediate adaptation needs from the Least Developed Countries Fund (LDCF).
</p><p>In Nepal, NAPA developed with three components: Preparation and dissemination of NAPA documents, development and maintenance of the Nepal Climate Change Knowledge Management Centre (NCCKMC), and development of the Multi-Stakeholder Climate Change Initiative Coordination Committee (MCCICC).
</p><p>In NAPA, nine integrated projects have been identified as the urgent and immediate national adaptation priority. They are:
</p>
<ol><li>Promoting community-based adaptation through integrated management of agriculture, water, forest and biodiversity sector</li>
<li>Building and enhancing adaptive capacity of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vulnerable_communities&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Vulnerable communities (page does not exist)">vulnerable communities</a> through improved system and access to services related to agriculture development</li>
<li>Community-based <a href="/wiki/Disaster_management" class="mw-redirect" title="Disaster management">disaster management</a> for facilitating <a href="/wiki/Climate_adaptation" class="mw-redirect" title="Climate adaptation">climate adaptation</a></li>
<li>GLOF Monitoring and disaster risk reduction and forest and <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem_management" title="Ecosystem management">ecosystem management</a> for supporting climate-led adaptation innovations</li>
<li>Adapting to climate challenges in public health and ecosystem management for climate adaptation</li>
<li>Empowering vulnerable communities through sustainable management of <a href="/wiki/Water_resource" class="mw-redirect" title="Water resource">water resource</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clean_energy" class="mw-redirect" title="Clean energy">clean energy</a> support and promoting climate smart urban settlement</li></ol>
<p>NAPA’s implementation framework envisages that the operating costs will be kept to a minimum and at least 80% of the available financial resources will reach the local level to fund activities on the ground. Stakeholders in Nepal has also started discussing National Adaptation Plans(NAPs), which are medium and long term adaptation plans for the country as decided by UNFCCC.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Effect_of_climate_change_in_Nepal">Effect of climate change in Nepal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Climate_change_adaptation_in_Nepal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Effect of climate change in Nepal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The effects of <a href="/wiki/Green_house_gases" class="mw-redirect" title="Green house gases">green house gases</a> (GHGs) on both drought and flooding events have been found, including severe winter drought<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and excessive monsoon flooding.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  Climate change has been alarming in the context of global warming. In Nepal, 95% of green house gas emissions from agriculture and forestry sectors were 77% from forestry sector only.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The consequences of <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a> have had the most impact in developing and mountainous countries like <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a>, which has high intensity rainfall during the rainy season. It has resulted in heavy floods, landslides and soil erosion. It is also common to find drought in many parts of Nepal that comes from the impacts of climate change and impacts sectors like forest, water resources, agriculture, human health and biodiversity in Nepal.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Likewise, altogether 14 glacial lake outburst floods (GLOFs)<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> have happened between 1935 &amp; 1991 in Nepal. In total, 21 GLOFs<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> have been identified as being potentially dangerous at present. In this way, CC and livelihoods integral part and have vice versa relationship. The low income &amp; subsistence users are about 38% of total population <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a> lies below the poverty line have hard time to afford for their livelihoods in Nepal. That is a great challenge to cope with climate change induced hazard &amp; extreme events. The livelihoods of more than 80% local people of hilly region are heavily depending on climate sensitive area such as agriculture, forest and livestock and on other natural resources such as water &amp; irrigation.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Potentiality_of_climate_change_adaptation">Potentiality of climate change adaptation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Climate_change_adaptation_in_Nepal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Potentiality of climate change adaptation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Response to climate change in Nepal has been growing in recent years with an effort to cope with the changing situation and build resilience capacity into adaptation to climate change. In climate induced vulnerability context, Nepal developed policy level provision regarding to adaptation policy called National Adaptation Programme of Action to climate change (NAPA).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>  The NAPA document opened the door to act adaptation activities into country. Under the provision of national level policy, Local Adaptation Plan of Action (LAPA) national framework <sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> devised out by government. It only mentioned the provision of the implementation mechanism at district or <a href="/wiki/Village_development_committee_(Nepal)" title="Village development committee (Nepal)">village development committee</a>  level to act climate change adaptation. However, this document is still silent to provision of implementation mechanism at community level. Though there are still silent to act adaptation implementation mechanism at community level, some community level adaptive strategies are being implemented as community based adaptation plan for poor &amp; vulnerable communities and who have less capacity to cope with disaster and are more dependent on natural resources for their livelihoods.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Adaptation_in_the_agricultural_sector">Adaptation in the agricultural sector</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Climate_change_adaptation_in_Nepal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Adaptation in the agricultural sector">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Adaptation to climate change in the agricultural sector and allied sectors is a major current and future challenge for Nepal. The majority of the population is still dependent on highly climate-sensitive agriculture. In recent years, long drought spells during the monsoon season and increased temperatures and unseasonal heavy rains during winter have caused serious distress to agriculture-dependent communities in many locations. If the <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_Development_Goals" title="Sustainable Development Goals">Sustainable Development Goals</a> (SDGs) of ending poverty, achieving food security and promoting sustainable agriculture are to be realised, climate change adaptation interventions need to be implemented in earnest.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Goods_and_services_from_community_forest">Goods and services from community forest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Climate_change_adaptation_in_Nepal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Goods and services from community forest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After 3&#160;decades of CF in Nepal, more than 1.652&#160;million forest lands handed over to 1.45&#160;million households of 17685 community forest user group (CFUG)<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> to conserve, manage and utilization . CFUG as a common property resource management program in Nepal have resulted in improving <a href="/wiki/Forest_cover" title="Forest cover">forest cover</a> and condition.By institutionally, Community forest user group is autonomous, independent and accountable institution for conserving, managing and utilizing of natural resources in Nepal legitimized by Forest Act 1992 and Forest Regulation 1995 of Nepal.
The additional advantages are as effective protection, wise use of resources, plantation, forest fire control, and more effective contribution to local development and economic generation. It enhanced biodiversity, water flow and soil stability. More than 90% of villagers report that their forests are in better condition than a decade ago. Furthermore, CFs are able to meet poor &amp; vulnerable household's daily subsistence needs for forest products such as firewood, fodder, &amp; timbers. Apart from this, growing forests capture and store carbon that are contributing to both mitigation and adaptation to CC. Because of, user groups have institutionally developed after CF handed over. Furthermore, the landscape of hills of Nepal drastically transformed into greenery.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Such types of changes have positive impact on carbon sequestration which has contributed in reducing effects of climate change.
</p><p>It is not only the CF contributing in climate change adaptation by providing goods and services, the CFUGs have also been used as local institutions for adaptation planning.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conclusion">Conclusion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Climate_change_adaptation_in_Nepal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Conclusion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Traditional top-down decision-making processes have become inadequate, due to their inability to create appropriate solutions for local communities. Nepal's forest cover, condition and quality are being improved. This is the success of only through <a href="/wiki/SEED-SCALE" title="SEED-SCALE">three way partnership</a> such as communities from bottom-up function, government &amp; donor from top-down function and NGOs, civil society network from outside-in. In this situation, CFUGs have to be involved in mainstreaming to implement climate change adaptation. It is due to they are playing the key role in proactive in investing their funds, climate change knowledge transfer and policy feedback to adopt to the impact of climate change. Policy shall be emphasized the establishing groups around the resources that are indispensable for the livelihoods of poor and vulnerable groups to access diversification opportunity. It is necessary to bridge this gap; bottom-up approaches may produce the best results by building on local experiences and knowledge. For this, building-up the capacity of groups and their poor and vulnerable communities on climate change mitigation and adaptation is pertinent. In addition to this, focus needs to be given on institutional development, capacity building and awarding CFUGs for their good work on forest development and bio-diversity protection which ultimately contributes to ecological and environment balance.
</p>
