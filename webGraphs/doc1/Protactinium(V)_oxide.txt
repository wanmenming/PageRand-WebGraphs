<p><b>Protactinium(V) oxide</b> is a chemical compound with the formula <a href="/wiki/Protactinium" title="Protactinium">Pa</a><sub>2</sub><a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">O</a><sub>5</sub>. When it is reduced with <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen" title="Hydrogen">hydrogen</a>, it forms <a href="/wiki/Protactinium(IV)_oxide" title="Protactinium(IV) oxide">PaO<sub>2</sub></a>. <a href="/wiki/Aristid_V._Grosse" class="mw-redirect" title="Aristid V. Grosse">Aristid V. Grosse</a> was first to prepare 2&#160;mg of Pa<sub>2</sub>O<sub>5</sub> in 1927.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Pa<sub>2</sub>O<sub>5</sub> does not dissolve in concentrated HNO<sub>3</sub>, but dissolves in HF and in a HF + H<sub>2</sub>SO<sub>4</sub> mixture and reacts at high temperatures with solid oxides of <a href="/wiki/Alkali_metal" title="Alkali metal">alkali metala</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alkaline_earth_metal" title="Alkaline earth metal">alkaline earth metals</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Myasoedov_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Myasoedov-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>195</span></sup>
</p><p>As protactinium(V) oxide, like other protactinium compounds, is <a href="/wiki/Radioactive" class="mw-redirect" title="Radioactive">radioactive</a>, toxic and very rare, it has very limited technological use. Mixed oxides of Nb, Mg, Ga and Mn, doped with 0.005–0.52% Pa<sub>2</sub>O<sub>5</sub>, have been
used as high temperature <a href="/wiki/Dielectric" title="Dielectric">dielectrics</a> (up to 1300&#160;°C) for <a href="/wiki/Ceramic_capacitor" title="Ceramic capacitor">ceramic capacitors</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Myasoedov_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Myasoedov-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>189</span></sup>
</p>
