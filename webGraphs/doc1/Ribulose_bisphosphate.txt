<p><b>Ribulose 1,5-bisphosphate</b> (<b>RuBP</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/Organic_substance" class="mw-redirect" title="Organic substance">organic substance</a> that is involved in <a href="/wiki/Photosynthesis" title="Photosynthesis">photosynthesis</a>. It is a colourless anion, a double <a href="/wiki/Phosphate_ester" class="mw-redirect" title="Phosphate ester">phosphate ester</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Ketopentose" class="mw-redirect" title="Ketopentose">ketopentose</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ketone" title="Ketone">ketone</a>-containing sugar with five <a href="/wiki/Carbon" title="Carbon">carbon</a> atoms) called <a href="/wiki/Ribulose" title="Ribulose">ribulose</a>.  Salts of RuBP can be isolated, but its crucial biological function happens in solution.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  To simplify the presentation, the image in the above table depicts the acid form of this anion.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Role_in_photosynthesis">Role in photosynthesis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ribulose_1,5-bisphosphate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Role in photosynthesis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:402px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Calvin-cycle4.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Calvin-cycle4.svg/400px-Calvin-cycle4.svg.png" width="400" height="367" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Calvin-cycle4.svg/600px-Calvin-cycle4.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Calvin-cycle4.svg/800px-Calvin-cycle4.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="836" data-file-height="766" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Calvin-cycle4.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Calvin cycle showing the role of ribulose-1,5-bisphosphate.</div></div></div>
<p>The enzyme ribulose bisphosphate carboxylase oxygenase (<a href="/wiki/RuBisCO" title="RuBisCO">RuBisCO</a>) catalyzes the reaction between RuBP and <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a>.  The product is the highly unstable six-carbon intermediate known as 3-keto-2-carboxyarabinitol 1,5-bisphosphate.  This six-carbon intermediate decays virtually instantaneously into two molecules of <a href="/wiki/3-phosphoglycerate" class="mw-redirect" title="3-phosphoglycerate">3-phosphoglycerate</a> (3-PGA) (see figure). RuBisCO also catalyzes RuBP with oxygen (<span class="chemf nowrap">O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span>) in a process called photorespiration, a process that is more prevalent at high temperatures. During photorespiration RuBP combines with <span class="chemf nowrap">O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> to become 3-PGA + phosphoglycolic acid.  In the <a href="/wiki/Calvin_cycle" class="mw-redirect" title="Calvin cycle">Calvin cycle</a>, RuBP is a product of the <a href="/wiki/Phosphorylation" title="Phosphorylation">phosphorylation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ribulose-5-phosphate" class="mw-redirect" title="Ribulose-5-phosphate">ribulose-5-phosphate</a> by <a href="/wiki/Adenosine_triphosphate" title="Adenosine triphosphate">ATP</a>.
</p>
