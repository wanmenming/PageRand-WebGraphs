<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Emissions">Emissions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_warming_in_Luxembourg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Emissions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Energy_in_Luxembourg" title="Energy in Luxembourg">Energy in Luxembourg</a></div>
<p>Emissions of <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a> in total, per capita in 2007 were 22.4 tons CO<sub>2</sub> compared to EU 27 average 7.9 tons [[CO<sub>2</sub>]].<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> 1990 emissions were 13 Mt CO<sub>2</sub><sub>eq</sub><a href="/wiki/Kyoto_protocol" class="mw-redirect" title="Kyoto protocol">Kyoto protocol</a> target is reduction of 4 Mt (28%).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Energy_use">Energy use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_warming_in_Luxembourg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Energy use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The European primary <a href="/wiki/Energy_consumption" title="Energy consumption">energy use</a> per capita was highest in 2008 in (TWh/per million): 1) <a href="/wiki/Energy_in_Iceland" class="mw-redirect" title="Energy in Iceland">Iceland</a> 191 2) <b><a href="/wiki/Energy_in_Luxembourg" title="Energy in Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a></b> 98 3) <a href="/wiki/Energy_in_Finland" title="Energy in Finland">Finland</a> 77 4) Norway 72 5) Belgium  64 6) Sweden  62 7) Netherlands  56 8) Russia 56 9) Kazakhstan 53 10)  Czech Republic 50 11) France  48, 12) Germany  47 13) Estonia 47 and 14) Austria 46.<sup id="cite_ref-IEA2010_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IEA2010-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Agreements_and_law">Agreements and law</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_warming_in_Luxembourg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Agreements and law">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Luxembourg is a member of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">EU</a> and thus the EU directives are binding.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Responsibility">Responsibility</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_warming_in_Luxembourg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Responsibility">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/Polluter_pays_principle" title="Polluter pays principle">polluter pays principle</a> the polluter has ecological and financial responsibility of the <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a> consequences. The climate change is caused cumulatively and today's emissions will affect decades forward. There are statistics available that show both total values and per individual.
</p><p>Cumulative CO<sub>2</sub> emissions, 1850–2007, per current inhabitant (tonnes CO<sub>2</sub>)&#160;: 1)  <b><a href="/wiki/Energy_in_Luxembourg" title="Energy in Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a></b> 1,429 2) <a href="/wiki/Energy_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Energy in the United Kingdom">UK</a> 1,127 3) <a href="/wiki/Energy_in_the_United_States" title="Energy in the United States">US</a> 1,126 4) <a href="/wiki/Energy_in_Belgium" title="Energy in Belgium">Belgium</a>  1,026 5) <a href="/wiki/Energy_in_the_Czech_Republic" title="Energy in the Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a> 1,006 6) <a href="/wiki/Energy_in_Germany" title="Energy in Germany">Germany</a> 987 7) <a href="/wiki/Energy_in_Estonia" title="Energy in Estonia">Estonia</a>  877  8) <a href="/wiki/Energy_in_Canada" class="mw-redirect" title="Energy in Canada">Canada</a>  779 9) <a href="/wiki/Energy_in_Kazakhstan" title="Energy in Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a> 682 10) <a href="/wiki/Energy_in_Russia" title="Energy in Russia">Russia</a> 666 11) <a href="/wiki/Energy_in_Denmark" title="Energy in Denmark">Denmark</a>  653 12) <a href="/wiki/Energy_in_Bahrain" title="Energy in Bahrain">Bahrain</a> 631 13) <a href="/wiki/Energy_in_Kuwait" title="Energy in Kuwait">Kuwait</a> 629  15) <a href="/wiki/Energy_in_Australia" title="Energy in Australia">Australia</a>  622   16) <a href="/wiki/Energy_in_Poland" title="Energy in Poland">Poland</a> 594 17) <a href="/wiki/Energy_in_Qatar" title="Energy in Qatar">Qatar</a> 584 18) <a href="/wiki/The_Energy_Sector_and_the_Trinidad_and_Tobago_Economy" class="mw-redirect" title="The Energy Sector and the Trinidad and Tobago Economy">Trinidad &amp; Tobago</a> 582 19) S<a href="/wiki/Energy_in_Slovakia" title="Energy in Slovakia">Slovakia</a> 579 and 20) <a href="/wiki/Energy_in_the_Netherlands" title="Energy in the Netherlands">Netherlands</a> 576 <sup id="cite_ref-G122011_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-G122011-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
