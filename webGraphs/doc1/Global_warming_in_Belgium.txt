<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Emissions">Emissions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_warming_in_Belgium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Emissions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1990, the <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gas</a> (GHG) emissions were 146.9 million tons of <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide_equivalent" title="Carbon dioxide equivalent">CO<sub>2</sub> equivalent</a> (Mt CO<sub>2</sub> eq), whose 88 million tons came from the <a href="/wiki/Flanders" title="Flanders">Flemish Region</a>,  54.8 from the <a href="/wiki/Wallonia" title="Wallonia">Walloon Region</a> and 4 Mt from the Brussels-capital Region.<sup id="cite_ref-nap_belgium_final_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nap_belgium_final-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Agreements">Agreements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_warming_in_Belgium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Agreements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Being a member of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>, applied the <a href="/wiki/European_Union_Emission_Trading_Scheme" title="European Union Emission Trading Scheme">European Union Emission Trading Scheme</a> set up by the <a href="/wiki/Directive_2003/87/EC" class="mw-redirect" title="Directive 2003/87/EC">Directive 2003/87/EC</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_protocol" class="mw-redirect" title="Kyoto protocol">Kyoto protocol</a> sets a 7.5% reduction of <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gas</a> emission target compared to 1990. Belgium set up a <a href="/wiki/National_Allocation_Plan" class="mw-redirect" title="National Allocation Plan">National Allocation Plan</a> at the federal level with target for each of the three regions.
</p><p>On 14 November 2002, Belgium signed the <i>Cooperation Agreement for the implementation of a National Climate Plan and reporting in the context of the UNFCCC and the Kyoto protocol</i>. The first <a href="/wiki/National_Allocation_Plan" class="mw-redirect" title="National Allocation Plan">National Allocation Plan</a> was for the period from 2005 to 2007. The <a href="/wiki/European_commission" class="mw-redirect" title="European commission">European commission</a> approved it on 20 October 2004. The second allocation plan was for the period 2008-2012 and aims a reduction of 7.5% of greenhouse gas emissions compared to 1990.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Flemish_Region">Flemish Region</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_warming_in_Belgium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Flemish Region">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The target of the Flemish Region is a reduction of 5.2% of GHG in the period 2008-2012 compared to 1990. That means an average emissions of 83,436 million tons CO<sub>2</sub> equivalent in the 2008-2012 period. The 
2008-2012, Flemish allocation plan deals with installation consuming more than 0.5 <a href="/wiki/Peta-" title="Peta-">PJ</a> (139 <a href="/wiki/GWh" class="mw-redirect" title="GWh">GWh</a>) annually.<sup id="cite_ref-nap_belgium_final_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nap_belgium_final-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> 17% of GHG emissions comes from <a href="/wiki/Transportation" class="mw-redirect" title="Transportation">transportation</a> and 21 from electricity production and heat production (excluded heat for buildings).<sup id="cite_ref-nap_belgium_final_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nap_belgium_final-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> There are 178 installations listed.
</p><p>The largest emitters are with the value in MTon CO<sub>2</sub> equivalent per year:<sup id="cite_ref-nap_belgium_final_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nap_belgium_final-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Sidmar" class="mw-redirect" title="Sidmar">Sidmar</a> owned by <a href="/wiki/ArcelorMittal" title="ArcelorMittal">ArcelorMittal</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ghent" title="Ghent">Ghent</a>: 8,918,495</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Total_S.A." title="Total S.A.">Total</a> <a href="/wiki/Oil_refinery" title="Oil refinery">refinery</a> in <a href="/wiki/Antwerp" title="Antwerp">Antwerp</a>: 4,323,405</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/BASF" title="BASF">BASF</a> in Antwerp: 2,088,422</li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zandvliet_Power&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Zandvliet Power (page does not exist)">Zandvliet Power</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Joint_venture" title="Joint venture">joint venture</a> of <a href="/wiki/BASF" title="BASF">BASF</a> and <a href="/wiki/GDF_Suez" class="mw-redirect" title="GDF Suez">GDF Suez</a>,  in <a href="/wiki/Zandvliet" title="Zandvliet">Zandvliet</a>: 1,119,158</li>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Brussels-Capital_Region">Brussels-Capital Region</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_warming_in_Belgium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Brussels-Capital Region">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Being a federal state, Brussels-Capital Region also made a second allocation plan for 2008–2012 based on the decree of June 3, 2004 that implements the <a href="/wiki/European_directive" class="mw-redirect" title="European directive">European directive</a> 2003/87/CE. In that plan, Brussels objective is to have an increase of maximum 3,475% of greenhouse gas emissions compared to 1990.
</p><p>In 2004, the Brussels-Capital Region emitted 4.4 million tons CO<sub>2</sub> equivalent, an increase of 9% compared to 1990 when emissions were 4,083 MTons CO<sub>2</sub> eq. The emissions come from domestic use (45%), tertiary sector (25%) and transportation (19%), and energy/industry (2%).<sup id="cite_ref-nap_belgium_final_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nap_belgium_final-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The 4.4 MTons CO<sub>2</sub> eq do not take into account GHG emission due to electricity production outside the region.
</p><p>The 2008–2012 allocation plans include only eight facilities:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Audi" title="Audi">Audi</a> (former <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen" title="Volkswagen">Volkswagen</a> plant) auto production plant in <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">Forest</a></li>
<li>a <a href="/wiki/BNP_Paribas" title="BNP Paribas">BNP Paribas</a> facility (former <a href="/wiki/Fortis_(finance)" title="Fortis (finance)">Fortis</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bruda&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bruda (page does not exist)">Bruda</a> plant producing <a href="/wiki/Asphalt" title="Asphalt">Asphalt</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Electrabel" title="Electrabel">Electrabel</a> <a href="/wiki/Turbo-jet" class="mw-redirect" title="Turbo-jet">turbo-jet</a> <a href="/wiki/Power_plant" class="mw-redirect" title="Power plant">power plant</a> in <a href="/wiki/Schaarbeek" class="mw-redirect" title="Schaarbeek">Schaarbeek</a></li>
<li>Electrabel turbo-jet power plant at <i>Buda</i></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Electrabel" title="Electrabel">Electrabel</a> turbo-jet power plant owned at <i>Volta</i></li>
<li>an <a href="/wiki/RTBF" title="RTBF">RTBF</a> television facility</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/World_Trade_Center_(Brussels)" title="World Trade Center (Brussels)">World Trade Center</a> building</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Walloon_Region">Walloon Region</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_warming_in_Belgium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Walloon Region">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the second allocation plan (for the period 2008-2012), the Walloon Region is planning a reduction of 7.5% of GHG emissions compared to 1990 when 54.84 million tons CO<sub>2</sub> equivalent was emitted.
<sup id="cite_ref-nap_belgium_final_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nap_belgium_final-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The plan for 2008-2012 includes 172 premises. In 2005, the largest emitters were (number in tons CO<sub>2</sub> equivalent):<sup id="cite_ref-nap_belgium_final_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nap_belgium_final-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/w/index.php?title=CCB_(cement_company)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="CCB (cement company) (page does not exist)">CCB</a> <a href="/wiki/Cement" title="Cement">cement</a> <a href="/wiki/Plant" title="Plant">plant</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gaurain-Ramecroix" title="Gaurain-Ramecroix">Gaurain-Ramecroix</a>: 1,515,543</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Holcim" title="Holcim">Holcim</a> cement plant in <a href="/wiki/Obourg" title="Obourg">Obourg</a>: 1,508,060</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Electrabel" title="Electrabel">Electrabel</a> <a href="/wiki/Power_plant" class="mw-redirect" title="Power plant">power plant</a> in <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monceau&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Monceau (page does not exist)">Monceau</a>: 1,260,520</li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=CBR_(cement_company)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="CBR (cement company) (page does not exist)">CBR</a> cement plant in <a href="/wiki/Lixhe" class="mw-redirect" title="Lixhe">Lixhe</a>: 1,059,929</li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dumont_Wautier&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dumont Wautier (page does not exist)">Dumont Wautier</a> <a href="/wiki/Lime_(mineral)" class="mw-redirect" title="Lime (mineral)">Lime</a> plant in <a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Georges,_Belgium&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Saint Georges, Belgium (page does not exist)">Saint Georges</a>: 1,294,087</li></ul>
<p>Other large emitter are cast iron and steel producer in <a href="/wiki/Charleroi" title="Charleroi">Charleroi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Li%C3%A8ge" title="Liège">Liège</a>.
</p><p>On October 22, 2009, <a href="/wiki/BASF" title="BASF">BASF</a> announced that they will close the plant located at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Feluy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Feluy (page does not exist)">Feluy</a> at the end of 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> That plant had a yearly allocation of 36,688 tons of CO<sub>2</sub> equivalent.
</p>
