<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">United Nations Millennium Declaration</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<p>On 8 September 2000, following a three-day <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Summit" title="Millennium Summit">Millennium Summit</a> of world leaders at the headquarters of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">General Assembly</a>
adopted the <b>Millennium Declaration (Resolution 55/2)</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEUN2000_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEUN2000-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> A follow-up outcome of the resolution was passed by the General Assembly on 14 December 2000 to guide its implementation. Progress on implementation of the Declaration was reviewed at the <a href="/wiki/2005_World_Summit" title="2005 World Summit">2005 World Summit</a> of leaders.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The Declaration includes 8 chapters and 32 paragraphs.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chapters">Chapters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Millennium_Declaration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Chapters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Millennium Declaration has eight chapters and key objectives, adopted by 189 world leaders during the summit: The Declaration, after the <a href="/wiki/Vienna_Declaration_and_Programme_of_Action" title="Vienna Declaration and Programme of Action">Vienna Declaration and Programme of Action</a>, stresses the observance of <a href="/wiki/International_human_rights_law" title="International human rights law">international human rights law</a> and <a href="/wiki/International_humanitarian_law" title="International humanitarian law">international humanitarian law</a> under the Principles of <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Charter" title="United Nations Charter">United Nations Charter</a> as well as the treaties on <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a>. The Declaration also urges observance of the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_truce" class="mw-redirect" title="Olympic truce">Olympic truce</a> individually and collectively.
</p>
<ol><li>Values and Principles</li></ol>
<dl><dd><ul><li>Freedom</li>
<li>Equality</li>
<li>Solidarity</li>
<li>Tolerance</li>
<li>Respect for nature - "Shown in the management of all living species and natural resources, in accordance with the precepts of sustainable development."</li>
<li>Shared responsibility</li></ul></dd></dl>
<ol><li class="mw-empty-elt"></li><li value="2"><a href="/wiki/Peace" title="Peace">Peace</a>, Security and <a href="/wiki/Disarmament" title="Disarmament">Disarmament</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Right_to_development" title="Right to development">Development</a> and <a href="/wiki/Poverty_reduction" title="Poverty reduction">Poverty Eradication</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Environmental_protection" title="Environmental protection">Protecting our Common Environment</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Human_Rights" class="mw-redirect" title="Human Rights">Human Rights</a>, <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">Democracy</a> and Good <a href="/wiki/Governance" title="Governance">Governance</a></li>
<li>Protecting the <a href="/wiki/Social_vulnerability" title="Social vulnerability">Vulnerable</a></li>
<li>Meeting the Special Needs of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a></li>
<li>Strengthening the United Nations</li></ol>
