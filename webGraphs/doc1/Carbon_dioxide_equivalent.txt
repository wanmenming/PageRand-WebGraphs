				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Carbon dioxide equivalent</b> (<b>CDE</b>) and <b>equivalent carbon dioxide</b> (<b>CO<sub>2</sub>e</b> and <b>CO<sub>2</sub>eq</b>) are two related but distinct measures for describing how much <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a> a given type and amount of <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gas</a> may cause, using the functionally equivalent amount or concentration of <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a> (CO<sub>2</sub>) as the reference.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Global_warming_potential">Global warming potential</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_dioxide_equivalent&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Global warming potential">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Global_warming_potential" title="Global warming potential">Global warming potential</a></div>
<p>Carbon dioxide equivalency is a <a href="/wiki/Quantity" title="Quantity">quantity</a> that describes, for a given mixture and amount of greenhouse gas, the amount of <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">CO<sub>2</sub></a> that would have the same <a href="/wiki/Global_warming_potential" title="Global warming potential">global warming potential</a> (GWP), when measured over a specified timescale (generally, 100 years). Carbon dioxide equivalency thus reflects the time-integrated radiative forcing of a quantity of <i>emissions</i> or rate of greenhouse gas emission—a <i>flow</i> into the atmosphere—rather than the instantaneous value of the radiative forcing of the <i>stock</i> (concentration) of greenhouse gases <i>in the atmosphere</i> described by CO<sub>2</sub>e.
</p><p>The carbon dioxide equivalency for a gas is obtained by multiplying the mass and the GWP of the gas. The following <a href="/wiki/Units_of_measurement" class="mw-redirect" title="Units of measurement">units</a> are commonly used:
</p>
<ul><li>By the <a href="/wiki/UN" class="mw-redirect" title="UN">UN</a> climate change panel <a href="/wiki/Intergovernmental_Panel_on_Climate_Change" title="Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change">IPCC</a>: n×10<sup>9</sup> <a href="/wiki/Tonne" title="Tonne">tonnes</a> of CO<sub>2</sub> equivalent (GtCO<sub>2</sub>eq).</li>
<li>In industry: million metric tonnes of carbon dioxide equivalents (MMTCDE).</li>
<li>For vehicles: g of carbon dioxide equivalents / km (gCDE/km).</li></ul>
<p>For example, the GWP for methane over 100 years is 34<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and for nitrous oxide 298. This means that emissions of 1 million metric tonnes of methane and nitrous oxide respectively is equivalent to emissions of 34 and 298 million metric tonnes of carbon dioxide.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Equivalent_carbon_dioxide">Equivalent carbon dioxide</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_dioxide_equivalent&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Equivalent carbon dioxide">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Equivalent CO<sub>2</sub> (CO<sub>2</sub>e) is the concentration of CO<sub>2</sub> that would cause the same level of <a href="/wiki/Radiative_forcing" title="Radiative forcing">radiative forcing</a> as a given type and concentration of greenhouse gas. Examples of such greenhouse gases are <a href="/wiki/Methane" title="Methane">methane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Perfluorocarbons" class="mw-redirect" title="Perfluorocarbons">perfluorocarbons</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nitrous_oxide" title="Nitrous oxide">nitrous oxide</a>. CO<sub>2</sub>e is expressed as parts per million by <a href="/wiki/Volume" title="Volume">volume</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ppmv" class="mw-redirect" title="Ppmv">ppmv</a>.
