<p><b>Rhenium(VII) oxide</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Inorganic_compound" title="Inorganic compound">inorganic compound</a> with the formula <a href="/wiki/Rhenium" title="Rhenium">Re<sub>2</sub></a><a href="/wiki/Oxide" title="Oxide">O<sub>7</sub></a>.  This yellowish solid is the <a href="/wiki/Anhydride" class="mw-redirect" title="Anhydride">anhydride</a> of  HOReO<sub>3</sub>. <a href="/wiki/Perrhenic_acid" title="Perrhenic acid">Perrhenic acid</a>, Re<sub>2</sub>O<sub>7</sub>&#183;2H<sub>2</sub>O, is closely related to Re<sub>2</sub>O<sub>7</sub>.  Re<sub>2</sub>O<sub>7</sub> is the raw material for all rhenium compounds, being the volatile fraction obtained upon roasting the host ore.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rhenium(VII)_oxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Crystalline Re<sub>2</sub>O<sub>7</sub> is an <a href="/wiki/Inorganic_polymer" title="Inorganic polymer">inorganic polymer</a>, which consists of alternating octahedral and tetrahedral Re centres.  Upon heating, the polymer <a href="/wiki/Cracking_(chemistry)" title="Cracking (chemistry)">cracks</a> to give molecular (nonpolymeric) Re<sub>2</sub>O<sub>7</sub>.  This molecular species closely resembles <a href="/wiki/Manganese_heptoxide" title="Manganese heptoxide">manganese heptoxide</a>, consisting of a pair of ReO<sub>4</sub> tetrahedra that share a vertex, i.e., O<sub>3</sub>Re-O-ReO<sub>3</sub>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synthesis_and_reactions">Synthesis and reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rhenium(VII)_oxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Synthesis and reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rhenium(VII) oxide is formed when metallic rhenium or its oxides or sulfides are oxidized at 500-700 °C in air.:<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Re<sub>2</sub>O<sub>7</sub> is very reactive toward water.  It dissolves in water to give perrhenic acid.  It is a precursor to <a href="/wiki/Methylrhenium_trioxide" title="Methylrhenium trioxide">methylrhenium trioxide</a> ("MTO"), a catalyst for oxidations.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Uses">Uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rhenium(VII)_oxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hydrogenation_catalyst">Hydrogenation catalyst</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rhenium(VII)_oxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Hydrogenation catalyst">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rhenium(VII) oxide finds some use in <a href="/wiki/Organic_synthesis" title="Organic synthesis">organic synthesis</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Catalyst" class="mw-redirect" title="Catalyst">catalyst</a> for <a href="/wiki/Carbonyl_reduction" title="Carbonyl reduction">carbonyl reduction</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amide_reduction" title="Amide reduction">amide reduction</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
