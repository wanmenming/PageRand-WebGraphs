<p>The <b>Mauna Loa Observatory</b> (<b>MLO</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/Earth%27s_atmosphere" class="mw-redirect" title="Earth&#39;s atmosphere">atmospheric</a> baseline station on <a href="/wiki/Mauna_Loa" title="Mauna Loa">Mauna Loa</a>, on the island of <a href="/wiki/Hawaii_(island)" title="Hawaii (island)">Hawaii</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_observatory">The observatory</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mauna_Loa_Observatory&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The observatory">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mauna_Loa_Solar_Observatory.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Mauna_Loa_Solar_Observatory.jpg/220px-Mauna_Loa_Solar_Observatory.jpg" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Mauna_Loa_Solar_Observatory.jpg/330px-Mauna_Loa_Solar_Observatory.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Mauna_Loa_Solar_Observatory.jpg/440px-Mauna_Loa_Solar_Observatory.jpg 2x" data-file-width="900" data-file-height="600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mauna_Loa_Solar_Observatory.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Two domes house solar sensors.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mauna_Loa_CO2_monthly_mean_concentration.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Mauna_Loa_CO2_monthly_mean_concentration.svg/220px-Mauna_Loa_CO2_monthly_mean_concentration.svg.png" width="220" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Mauna_Loa_CO2_monthly_mean_concentration.svg/330px-Mauna_Loa_CO2_monthly_mean_concentration.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Mauna_Loa_CO2_monthly_mean_concentration.svg/440px-Mauna_Loa_CO2_monthly_mean_concentration.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="708" data-file-height="708" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mauna_Loa_CO2_monthly_mean_concentration.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Keeling Curve: Atmospheric CO<sub>2</sub> concentrations as measured at Mauna Loa Observatory</div></div></div>
<p>Since 1958, initially under the direction of <a href="/wiki/Charles_David_Keeling" title="Charles David Keeling">Charles Keeling</a>, followed by his son <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Keeling" title="Ralph Keeling">Ralph</a> and later <a href="/wiki/Elmer_Robinson_(meteorologist)" title="Elmer Robinson (meteorologist)">Elmer Robinson</a>, Mauna Loa Observatory (MLO) has been monitoring and collecting data relating to atmospheric change, and is known especially for the <a href="/wiki/Continuous_emissions_monitoring_system" title="Continuous emissions monitoring system">continuous monitoring</a> of atmospheric <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a> (CO<sub>2</sub>), which is sometimes referred to as the <a href="/wiki/Keeling_Curve" title="Keeling Curve">Keeling Curve</a>. The observatory is under the <a href="/wiki/Earth_System_Research_Laboratory" title="Earth System Research Laboratory">Earth System Research Laboratory</a> which is part of the <a href="/wiki/National_Oceanic_and_Atmospheric_Administration" title="National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration">National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration</a> (NOAA). According to the NOAA, Mauna Loa is the world's oldest continuous <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_monitoring" title="Greenhouse gas monitoring">CO<sub>2</sub> monitoring</a> station, and the world's primary benchmark site for measurement of the gas. <sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The latest observation of CO<sub>2</sub> concentrations from MLO can be found at web sites along with data from other sites<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and trends at Mauna Loa.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The MLO levels can be compared with other sites in the global monitoring network.
</p><p>MLO has activities at five locations on the Big Island. The primary observing site is located at the 3397 m (11,141&#160;ft) level on Mauna Loa's north slope (<span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Mauna_Loa_Observatory&amp;params=19_32_10_N_155_34_34_W_type:landmark_region:US-HI"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">19°32′10″N</span> <span class="longitude">155°34′34″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">&#xfeff; / &#xfeff;</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">19.53611°N 155.57611°W</span><span style="display:none">&#xfeff; / <span class="geo">19.53611; -155.57611</span></span></span></a></span><span style="font-size: small;"><span id="coordinates"><a href="/wiki/Geographic_coordinate_system" title="Geographic coordinate system">Coordinates</a>: <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Mauna_Loa_Observatory&amp;params=19_32_10_N_155_34_34_W_type:landmark_region:US-HI"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">19°32′10″N</span> <span class="longitude">155°34′34″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">&#xfeff; / &#xfeff;</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">19.53611°N 155.57611°W</span><span style="display:none">&#xfeff; / <span class="geo">19.53611; -155.57611</span></span></span></a></span></span></span>) about 5&#160;km north of the summit <a href="/wiki/Mokuaweoweo" class="mw-redirect" title="Mokuaweoweo">Mokuaweoweo</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Mauna_Loa_Solar_Observatory" title="Mauna Loa Solar Observatory">Mauna Loa Solar Observatory</a> shares this site. The administration and some data processing are done in the <a href="/wiki/Hilo,_Hawaii" title="Hilo, Hawaii">Hilo, Hawaii</a> office. Kulani Mauka is a rain collection site. Cape Kumukahi is a flask sample site located on the easternmost point of Hawaii. At the <a href="/wiki/Hilo_International_Airport" title="Hilo International Airport">Hilo airport</a>, weekly balloon-borne instruments are prepared and launched to measure <a href="/wiki/Ozone" title="Ozone">ozone</a> from the surface to usually over 30&#160;km.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
The observatory site is also a temporary home to a <a href="/wiki/Cosmic_microwave_background" title="Cosmic microwave background">cosmic microwave background</a> observatory called <a href="/wiki/AMiBA" title="AMiBA">AMiBA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mauna Loa was originally chosen as a monitoring site because, located far from any continent, the air was sampled and is a good average for the central Pacific. Being high, it is above the <a href="/wiki/Inversion_(meteorology)" title="Inversion (meteorology)">inversion layer</a> where most of the local effects are present and there was already a rough road to the summit built by the military. The contamination from local volcanic sources is sometimes detected at the observatory, and is then removed from the background data.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
