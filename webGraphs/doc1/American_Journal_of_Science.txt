<p>The <i><b>American Journal of Science</b></i> (<i><b>AJS</b></i>) is the United States of America's longest-running <a href="/wiki/Scientific_journal" title="Scientific journal">scientific journal</a>, having been published continuously since its conception in 1818 by Professor <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Silliman" title="Benjamin Silliman">Benjamin Silliman</a>, who edited and financed it himself. Until 1880, it was also known as the <i>American Journal of Science and Arts</i>, but its focus was always on natural sciences and especially on <a href="/wiki/Geology" title="Geology">geology</a> and related subjects.
</p><p>In early years, the journal was often referred to as "Silliman's Journal", and the publication became associated with <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a> due to his long tenure there (1804–1853). The editorship long remained in the family of Professor Silliman, as he was assisted by his son, <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Silliman,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Benjamin Silliman, Jr.">Benjamin Silliman, Jr.</a>, from 1838. On the death of the elder Silliman in 1864, he was succeeded as chief editor by his son-in-law, <a href="/wiki/James_Dwight_Dana" title="James Dwight Dana">James Dwight Dana</a>, and then from 1895 till 1926 by Dana's son <a href="/wiki/Edward_Salisbury_Dana" title="Edward Salisbury Dana">Edward Salisbury Dana</a>. Associate editors included the botanist <a href="/wiki/Asa_Gray" title="Asa Gray">Asa Gray</a> and the zoologist <a href="/wiki/Louis_Agassiz" title="Louis Agassiz">Louis Agassiz</a>.
</p><p>The current editors are C. Page Chamberlain, a professor at <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a>, and Danny M. Rye and Mark T. Brandon, professors of geology at <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a>.
</p>
