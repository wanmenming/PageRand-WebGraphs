<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Effects of climate change on plant biodiversity</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>
				<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:382px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Alpine_flora_logan_pass.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/Alpine_flora_logan_pass.jpg/380px-Alpine_flora_logan_pass.jpg" width="380" height="285" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/Alpine_flora_logan_pass.jpg/570px-Alpine_flora_logan_pass.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/Alpine_flora_logan_pass.jpg/760px-Alpine_flora_logan_pass.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1152" data-file-height="864" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Alpine_flora_logan_pass.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Alpine flora at <a href="/wiki/Logan_Pass" title="Logan Pass">Logan Pass</a>, <a href="/wiki/Glacier_National_Park_(U.S.)" title="Glacier National Park (U.S.)">Glacier National Park</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Montana" title="Montana">Montana</a>, United States: <a href="/wiki/Alpine_plants" class="mw-redirect" title="Alpine plants">Alpine plants</a> are one group expected to be highly susceptible to the <a href="/wiki/Effects_of_global_warming" title="Effects of global warming">impacts of climate change</a></div></div></div>
<p>Environmental conditions play a key role in defining the function and <a href="/wiki/Range_(biology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Range (biology)">distribution</a> of <a href="/wiki/Plants" class="mw-redirect" title="Plants">plants</a>, in combination with other factors. Changes in long term environmental conditions that can be collectively coined <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a> are known to have had enormous impacts on current plant diversity patterns; further impacts are expected in the future.<sup id="cite_ref-SahneyBentonFalconLang_2010RainforestCollapse_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SahneyBentonFalconLang_2010RainforestCollapse-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is predicted that climate change will remain one of the major drivers of <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">biodiversity</a> patterns in the future.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Palaeo_context">Palaeo context</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Effects_of_climate_change_on_plant_biodiversity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Palaeo context">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Daintree_Rainforest.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Daintree_Rainforest.JPG/260px-Daintree_Rainforest.JPG" width="260" height="304" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Daintree_Rainforest.JPG/390px-Daintree_Rainforest.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Daintree_Rainforest.JPG/520px-Daintree_Rainforest.JPG 2x" data-file-width="657" data-file-height="768" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Daintree_Rainforest.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Australian <a href="/wiki/Rainforest" title="Rainforest">Rainforest</a>: An ecosystem known to have significantly contracted in area over recent geological time as a result of climatic changes.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Last_glacial_vegetation_map.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Last_glacial_vegetation_map.png/260px-Last_glacial_vegetation_map.png" width="260" height="159" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Last_glacial_vegetation_map.png/390px-Last_glacial_vegetation_map.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Last_glacial_vegetation_map.png/520px-Last_glacial_vegetation_map.png 2x" data-file-width="782" data-file-height="477" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Last_glacial_vegetation_map.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Map of global vegetation distributions during the last <a href="/wiki/Glacial_maximum" class="mw-redirect" title="Glacial maximum">glacial maximum</a></div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a> has experienced a constantly changing <a href="/wiki/Climate" title="Climate">climate</a> in the time since plants first evolved. In comparison to the present day, this history has seen Earth as cooler, warmer, drier and wetter, and CO<sub>2</sub> (<a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a>) concentrations have been both higher and lower.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> These changes have been reflected by constantly shifting <a href="/wiki/Vegetation" title="Vegetation">vegetation</a>, for example <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">forest</a> communities dominating most areas in <a href="/wiki/Interglacial" title="Interglacial">interglacial</a> periods, and <a href="/wiki/Herbaceous_plant" title="Herbaceous plant">herbaceous</a> communities dominating during <a href="/wiki/Glacial_periods" class="mw-redirect" title="Glacial periods">glacial periods</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> It has been shown that past climatic change has been a major driver of the processes of <a href="/wiki/Speciation" title="Speciation">speciation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Extinction" title="Extinction">extinction</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SahneyBentonFalconLang_2010RainforestCollapse_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SahneyBentonFalconLang_2010RainforestCollapse-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The best known example of this is the <a href="/wiki/Carboniferous_Rainforest_Collapse" class="mw-redirect" title="Carboniferous Rainforest Collapse">Carboniferous Rainforest Collapse</a> which occurred 350 million years ago. This event decimated amphibian populations and spurred on the evolution of reptiles.<sup id="cite_ref-SahneyBentonFalconLang_2010RainforestCollapse_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SahneyBentonFalconLang_2010RainforestCollapse-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_Context">Modern Context</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Effects_of_climate_change_on_plant_biodiversity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Modern Context">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There is significant current interest and research focus on the phenomenon of recent <a href="/wiki/Human_impact_on_the_environment" title="Human impact on the environment">anthropogenic</a> climate changes, or <a href="/wiki/Global_Warming" class="mw-redirect" title="Global Warming">global warming</a>. Focus is on identifying the current impacts of climate change on biodiversity, and predicting these effects into the future.
</p><p>Changing climatic variables relevant to the function and distribution of plants include increasing <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">CO<sub>2</sub></a> concentrations, increasing global temperatures, altered <a href="/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Precipitation (meteorology)">precipitation</a> patterns, and changes in the pattern of ‘extreme’ weather events such as cyclones, fires or storms. Highly variable species distribution has resulted from different models with variable bioclimatic changes.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Because individual plants and therefore species can only function <a href="/wiki/Plant_physiology" title="Plant physiology">physiologically</a>, and successfully complete their <a href="/wiki/Biological_life_cycle" title="Biological life cycle">life cycles</a> under specific environmental conditions (ideally within a subset of these), changes to climate are likely to have significant impacts on plants from the level of the individual right through to the level of the ecosystem or <a href="/wiki/Biome" title="Biome">biome</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Effects_of_CO2">Effects of CO<sub>2</sub></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Effects_of_climate_change_on_plant_biodiversity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Effects of CO2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:282px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mauna_Loa_CO2_monthly_mean_concentration.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Mauna_Loa_CO2_monthly_mean_concentration.svg/280px-Mauna_Loa_CO2_monthly_mean_concentration.svg.png" width="280" height="280" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Mauna_Loa_CO2_monthly_mean_concentration.svg/420px-Mauna_Loa_CO2_monthly_mean_concentration.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Mauna_Loa_CO2_monthly_mean_concentration.svg/560px-Mauna_Loa_CO2_monthly_mean_concentration.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="708" data-file-height="708" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mauna_Loa_CO2_monthly_mean_concentration.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Recent increases in atmospheric CO<sub>2</sub>.</div></div></div>
<p>CO<sub>2</sub> concentrations have been steadily rising for more than two centuries.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Increases in atmospheric CO<sub>2</sub> concentration affect how plants <a href="/wiki/Photosynthesis" title="Photosynthesis">photosynthesise</a>, resulting in increases in plant water use efficiency, enhanced photosynthetic capacity and increased growth.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Increased CO<sub>2</sub> has been implicated in ‘vegetation thickening’ which affects plant <a href="/wiki/Community_(ecology)" title="Community (ecology)">community</a> structure and function.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Depending on environment, there are differential responses to elevated atmospheric CO<sub>2</sub> between major ‘functional types’ of plant, such as <a href="/wiki/C3_plant" class="mw-redirect" title="C3 plant">C<sub>3</sub></a> and <a href="/wiki/C4_carbon_fixation" title="C4 carbon fixation">C<sub>4</sub></a> plants, or more or less woody species; which has the potential among other things to alter competition between these groups.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Increased CO<sub>2</sub> can also lead to increased <a href="/wiki/Carbon_to_nitrogen_ratio" class="mw-redirect" title="Carbon to nitrogen ratio">Carbon : Nitrogen ratios</a> in the leaves of plants or in other aspects of leaf chemistry, possibly changing <a href="/wiki/Herbivore" title="Herbivore">herbivore</a> nutrition.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Effects_of_temperature">Effects of temperature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Effects_of_climate_change_on_plant_biodiversity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Effects of temperature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2005_surface_temperature_anomaly.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/2005_surface_temperature_anomaly.jpg/200px-2005_surface_temperature_anomaly.jpg" width="200" height="137" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/2005_surface_temperature_anomaly.jpg/300px-2005_surface_temperature_anomaly.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/2005_surface_temperature_anomaly.jpg/400px-2005_surface_temperature_anomaly.jpg 2x" data-file-width="463" data-file-height="316" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2005_surface_temperature_anomaly.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Global annual surface temperature anomaly in 2005, relative to 1951-1980 mean</div></div></div>
<p>Increases in temperature raise the rate of many physiological processes such as photosynthesis in plants, to an upper limit.  These increases in photosynthesis and other physiological processes are driven by increased rates of chemical reactions and roughly a doubling of enzymatic product conversion rates for every 10&#160;°C increase in temperature.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>  Extreme temperatures can be harmful when beyond the physiological limits of a plant.
</p><p>One common hypothesis among scientists is that the warmer an area is, the higher the plant diversity. This hypothesis can be observed in nature, where higher plant biodiversity is often located at certain latitudes (which often correlates with a specific climate/temperature).<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Effects_of_water">Effects of water</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Effects_of_climate_change_on_plant_biodiversity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Effects of water">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:US_precipitation_trends.gif" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/US_precipitation_trends.gif/200px-US_precipitation_trends.gif" width="200" height="238" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/US_precipitation_trends.gif/300px-US_precipitation_trends.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/US_precipitation_trends.gif/400px-US_precipitation_trends.gif 2x" data-file-width="600" data-file-height="714" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:US_precipitation_trends.gif" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Precipitation trends in the United States, from the period 1901-2005. In some areas rainfall has increased in the last century, while some areas have dried.</div></div></div>
<p>As water supply is critical for plant growth, it plays a key role in determining the distribution of plants. Changes in <a href="/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Precipitation (meteorology)">precipitation</a> are predicted to be less consistent than for temperature and more variable between regions, with predictions for some areas to become much wetter, and some much drier.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> This can cause a major change in some ecosystems which are dependent on water supply.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="General_effects">General effects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Effects_of_climate_change_on_plant_biodiversity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: General effects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Environmental variables act not in isolation, but in combination with other pressures such as <a href="/wiki/Habitat" title="Habitat">habitat</a> degradation, habitat loss, and the introduction of <a href="/wiki/Exotic_species" class="mw-redirect" title="Exotic species">exotic species</a> that can potentially be <a href="/wiki/Invasive_species" title="Invasive species">invasive</a>. It is suggested that these other drivers of biodiversity change will act in synergy with climate change to increase the pressure on species to survive.<sup id="cite_ref-Mackey07_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mackey07-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Direct_impacts_of_climate_change">Direct impacts of climate change</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Effects_of_climate_change_on_plant_biodiversity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Direct impacts of climate change">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Changes_in_distributions">Changes in distributions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Effects_of_climate_change_on_plant_biodiversity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Changes in distributions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Alpineplantssweden.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Alpineplantssweden.jpg/200px-Alpineplantssweden.jpg" width="200" height="163" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Alpineplantssweden.jpg/300px-Alpineplantssweden.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Alpineplantssweden.jpg/400px-Alpineplantssweden.jpg 2x" data-file-width="482" data-file-height="394" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Alpineplantssweden.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Pine tree representing an elevational tree-limit rise of 105 m over the period 1915–1974. Nipfjället, Sweden</div></div></div>
<p>If climatic factors such as <a href="/wiki/Temperature" title="Temperature">temperature</a> and <a href="/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Precipitation (meteorology)">precipitation</a> change in a region beyond the tolerance of a species <a href="/wiki/Phenotypic_plasticity" title="Phenotypic plasticity">phenotypic plasticity</a>, then distribution changes of the species may be inevitable.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> There is already evidence that plant species are shifting their ranges in altitude and latitude as a response to changing regional climates.<sup id="cite_ref-Parmesan03_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Parmesan03-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Walther02_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walther02-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Yet it is difficult to predict how species ranges will change in response to climate and separate these  changes from all the other man-made environmental changes such as <a href="/wiki/Eutrophication" title="Eutrophication">eutrophication</a>, <a href="/wiki/Acid_rain" title="Acid rain">acid rain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Habitat_destruction" title="Habitat destruction">habitat destruction</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When compared to the reported past migration rates of plant species, the rapid pace of current change has the potential to not only alter species distributions, but also render many species as unable to follow the climate to which they are adapted.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The environmental conditions required by some species, such as those in alpine regions may disappear altogether. The result of these changes is likely to be a rapid increase in extinction risk.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Adaptation" title="Adaptation">Adaptation</a> to new conditions may also be of great importance in the response of plants.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Predicting the extinction risk of plant species is not easy however. Estimations from particular periods of rapid climatic change in the past have shown relatively little species extinction in some regions, for example.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Knowledge of how species may adapt or persist in the face of rapid change is still relatively limited.
</p><p>Changes in the suitability of a habitat for a species drive distributional changes by not only changing the area that a species can physiologically tolerate, but how effectively it can compete with other plants within this area. Changes in community composition are therefore also an expected product of climate change.
</p>
<h4><span id="Changes_in_life-cycles_.28phenology.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Changes_in_life-cycles_(phenology)">Changes in life-cycles (phenology)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Effects_of_climate_change_on_plant_biodiversity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Changes in life-cycles (phenology)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The timing of <a href="/wiki/Phenology" title="Phenology">phenological</a> events such as <a href="/wiki/Flowering" class="mw-redirect" title="Flowering">flowering</a> are often related to environmental variables such as temperature. Changing environments are therefore expected to lead to changes in life cycle events, and these have been recorded for many species of plants.<sup id="cite_ref-Parmesan03_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Parmesan03-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> These changes have the potential to lead to the asynchrony between species, or to change competition between plants. Flowering times in British plants for example have changed, leading to <a href="/wiki/Annual_plants" class="mw-redirect" title="Annual plants">annual plants</a> flowering earlier than <a href="/wiki/Perennials" class="mw-redirect" title="Perennials">perennials</a>, and insect pollinated plants flowering earlier than wind pollinated plants; with potential ecological consequences.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> A recently published study has used data recorded by the writer and naturalist <a href="/wiki/Henry_David_Thoreau" title="Henry David Thoreau">Henry David Thoreau</a> to confirm effects of climate change on the phenology of some species in the area of Concord, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Indirect_impacts_of_climate_change">Indirect impacts of climate change</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Effects_of_climate_change_on_plant_biodiversity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Indirect impacts of climate change">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>All species are likely to be directly impacted by the changes in environmental conditions discussed above, and also indirectly through their interactions with other species. While direct impacts may be easier to predict and conceptualise, it is likely that indirect impacts are equally important in determining the response of plants to climate change.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
A species whose distribution changes as a direct result of climate change may ‘invade’ the range of another species or 'be invaded' for example, introducing a new competitive relationship or altering other processes such as carbon sequestration.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In Europe, the temperature and precipitation effects due to climate change can indirectly affect certain populations of people.  The rise of temperatures and lack of precipitation results in different river floodplains, which reduce the populations of people sensitive to flood risk.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The range of a symbiotic fungi associated with plant roots may directly change as a result of altered climate, resulting in a change in the plant's distribution.
</p><p>A new grass may spread into a region, altering the fire regime and greatly changing the species composition.
</p><p>A pathogen or parasite may change its interactions with a plant, such as a pathogenic fungus becoming more common in an area where rainfall increases.
</p><p>Increased temperatures may allow herbivores to expand further into alpine regions, significant impacting the composition of alpine <a href="/wiki/Herbfield" title="Herbfield">herbfields</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Higher_level_changes">Higher level changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Effects_of_climate_change_on_plant_biodiversity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Higher level changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Species respond in very different ways to climate change. Variation in the distribution, phenology and abundance of species will lead to inevitable changes in the relative abundance of species and their interactions. These changes will flow on to affect the structure and function of ecosystems.<sup id="cite_ref-Walther02_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walther02-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Challenges_of_modeling_future_impacts">Challenges of modeling future impacts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Effects_of_climate_change_on_plant_biodiversity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Challenges of modeling future impacts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Accurate predictions of the future impacts of climate change on plant diversity are critical to the development of conservation strategies. These predictions have come largely from bioinformatic strategies, involving modeling individual species, groups of species such as ‘functional types’, communities, ecosystems or biomes. They can also involve modeling species observed environmental <a href="/wiki/Ecological_niche" title="Ecological niche">niches</a>, or observed physiological processes.
</p><p>Although useful, modeling has many limitations. Firstly, there is uncertainty about the future levels of greenhouse gas emissions driving climate change <sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> and considerable uncertainty in <a href="/wiki/Global_climate_model" class="mw-redirect" title="Global climate model">modeling</a> how this will affect other aspects of climate such as local rainfall or temperatures. For most species the importance of specific climatic variables in defining distribution (e.g. minimum rainfall or maximum temperature) is unknown. It is also difficult to know which aspects of a particular climatic variable are most biologically relevant, such as average vs. maximum or minimum temperatures. Ecological processes such as interactions between species and dispersal rates and distances are also inherently complex, further complicating predictions.
</p><p>Improvement of models is an active area of research, with new models attempting to take factors such as life-history traits of species or processes such as migration into account when predicting distribution changes; though possible trade-offs between regional accuracy and generality are recognised.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Climate change is also predicted to interact with other drivers of biodiversity change such as habitat destruction and fragmentation, or the introduction of foreign species. These threats may possibly act in <a href="/wiki/Synergy" title="Synergy">synergy</a> to increase extinction risk from that seen in periods of rapid climate change in the past.<sup id="cite_ref-Mackey07_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mackey07-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
