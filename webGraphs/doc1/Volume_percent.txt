<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Volume fraction</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">  (Redirected from <a href="/w/index.php?title=Volume_percent&amp;redirect=no" class="mw-redirect" title="Volume percent">Volume percent</a>)</span></div>
				<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>

				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>In <a href="/wiki/Chemistry" title="Chemistry">chemistry</a>, the <b>volume fraction</b> &#966;<sub><i>i</i></sub> is defined as the volume of a constituent <i>V</i><sub><i>i</i></sub> divided by the volume of all constituents of the mixture <i>V</i> prior to mixing:<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Being <a href="/wiki/Dimensionless_quantity" title="Dimensionless quantity">dimensionless</a>, its unit is 1; it is expressed as a number, e.g., 0.18. It is the same concept as volume percent (vol%) except that the latter is expressed with a <a href="/wiki/Denominator" class="mw-redirect" title="Denominator">denominator</a> of 100, e.g., 18%.
</p><p>The volume fraction coincides with the <b>volume concentration</b> in <a href="/wiki/Ideal_solution" title="Ideal solution">ideal solutions</a> where the volumes of the constituents are additive (the volume of the solution is equal to the sum of the volumes of its ingredients).
</p><p>The sum of all volume fractions of a mixture is equal to 1:

<p>The volume fraction (percentage by volume, vol%) is one way of expressing the composition of a mixture with a <a href="/wiki/Dimensionless_quantity" title="Dimensionless quantity">dimensionless quantity</a>; <a href="/wiki/Mass_fraction_(chemistry)" title="Mass fraction (chemistry)">mass fraction</a> (percentage by weight, wt%) and <a href="/wiki/Mole_fraction" title="Mole fraction">mole fraction</a> (percentage by <a href="/wiki/Mole_(unit)" title="Mole (unit)">moles</a>, mol%) are others.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Volume_percent">Volume percent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Volume_fraction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Volume percent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Volume percent is a common expression of a <a href="/wiki/Solution" title="Solution">solution</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Concentration" title="Concentration">concentration</a>. It is the volume fraction expressed with a <a href="/wiki/Denominator" class="mw-redirect" title="Denominator">denominator</a> of 100, and thus is defined as:
</p>
<dl><dd><span class="mwe-math-element"><span class="mwe-math-mathml-inline mwe-math-mathml-a11y" style="display: none;"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"  alttext="{\displaystyle {\text{volume percent}}={\frac {\text{volume of solute}}{\text{volume of solution}}}\times 100}">
  <semantics>
    <mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">

        </mrow>
        <mo>=</mo>
        <mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">
          <mfrac>
            <mtext>volume of solute</mtext>
            <mtext>volume of solution</mtext>
          </mfrac>
        </mrow>
        <mo>&#x00D7;<!-- × --></mo>
        <mn>100</mn>
      </mstyle>
    </mrow>
    <annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{\displaystyle {\text{volume percent}}={\frac {\text{volume of solute}}{\text{volume of solution}}}\times 100}</annotation>
  </semantics>
</math></span><img src="https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/9b5c1def05e851a2f7e53f870b963bdbb52201b5" class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline" aria-hidden="true" style="vertical-align: -2.005ex; width:43.997ex; height:5.509ex;" alt="{\displaystyle {\text{volume percent}}={\frac {\text{volume of solute}}{\text{volume of solution}}}\times 100}"/></span></dd></dl>
<p><a href="/wiki/Volume" title="Volume">Volume</a> percent is usually used when the solution is made by mixing two <a href="/wiki/Fluid" title="Fluid">fluids</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Liquids" class="mw-redirect" title="Liquids">liquids</a> or <a href="/wiki/Gases" class="mw-redirect" title="Gases">gases</a>. However, <a href="/wiki/Percentages" class="mw-redirect" title="Percentages">percentages</a> are only additive for <a href="/wiki/Ideal_gas" title="Ideal gas">ideal gases</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <b>percentage by volume</b> (<b>vol%</b>) is one way of expressing the composition of a mixture with a <a href="/wiki/Dimensionless_quantity" title="Dimensionless quantity">dimensionless quantity</a>; <a href="/wiki/Mass_fraction_(chemistry)" title="Mass fraction (chemistry)">mass fraction</a> (percentage by weight, wt%) and <a href="/wiki/Mole_fraction" title="Mole fraction">mole fraction</a> (percentage by <a href="/wiki/Mole_(unit)" title="Mole (unit)">moles</a>, mol%) are others.
</p><p>In the case of a mixture of ethanol and water, which are <a href="/wiki/Miscible" class="mw-redirect" title="Miscible">miscible</a> in all proportions, the designation of solvent and solute is arbitrary. The volume of such a mixture is slightly less than the sum of the volumes of the components. Thus, by the above definition, the term "40% alcohol by volume" refers to a mixture of 40 volume units of ethanol with enough water to make a final volume of 100 units, rather than a mixture of 40 units of ethanol with 60 units of water.
</p>
