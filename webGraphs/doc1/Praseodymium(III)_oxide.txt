<p><b>Praseodymium(III) oxide</b>, <b>praseodymium oxide</b> or <b>praseodymia</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Chemical_compound" title="Chemical compound">chemical compound</a> composed of <a href="/wiki/Praseodymium" title="Praseodymium">praseodymium</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygen</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Chemical_formula" title="Chemical formula">formula</a> Pr<sub>2</sub>O<sub>3</sub>. It forms white <a href="/wiki/Hexagonal_crystal_system" class="mw-redirect" title="Hexagonal crystal system">hexagonal</a> crystals.<sup id="cite_ref-hand_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hand-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Praseodymium(III) oxide crystallizes in the <a href="/wiki/Manganese(III)_oxide" title="Manganese(III) oxide">manganese(III) oxide</a> or <a href="/wiki/Bixbyite" title="Bixbyite">bixbyite</a> structure.<sup id="cite_ref-semi_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-semi-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Uses">Uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Praseodymium(III)_oxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Praseodymium(III) oxide can be used as a <a href="/wiki/Dielectric" title="Dielectric">dielectric</a> in combination with <a href="/wiki/Silicon" title="Silicon">silicon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-semi_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-semi-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Praseodymium-doped glass, called <a href="/wiki/Didymium" title="Didymium">didymium</a> <a href="/wiki/Glass" title="Glass">glass</a>, turns yellow and is used in <a href="/wiki/Welding_goggles" title="Welding goggles">welding goggles</a> because it blocks <a href="/wiki/Infrared" title="Infrared">infrared</a> radiation. Praseodymium(III) oxide is also used to color glass and ceramics yellow.<sup id="cite_ref-hist_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hist-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
For coloring ceramics, also the very dark brown mixed-valence compound praseodymium(III,IV) oxide, Pr<sub>6</sub>O<sub>11</sub>, is used.
</p>
