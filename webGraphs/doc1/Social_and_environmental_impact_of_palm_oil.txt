<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Social and environmental impact of palm oil</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>
				<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Riau_palm_oil_2007.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Riau_palm_oil_2007.jpg/300px-Riau_palm_oil_2007.jpg" width="300" height="200" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Riau_palm_oil_2007.jpg/450px-Riau_palm_oil_2007.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Riau_palm_oil_2007.jpg/600px-Riau_palm_oil_2007.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="1365" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Riau_palm_oil_2007.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Deforestation_in_Indonesia" title="Deforestation in Indonesia">Deforestation</a> in <a href="/wiki/Riau" title="Riau">Riau province</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sumatra" title="Sumatra">Sumatra</a>, to make way for an oil palm plantation (2007)</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Palm_oil" title="Palm oil">Palm oil</a>, produced from the <a href="/wiki/Oil_palm" class="mw-redirect" title="Oil palm">oil palm</a>, is a basic source of income for many farmers in South East Asia, Central and West Africa, and Central America. It is locally used as a cooking oil, exported for use in many commercial food and personal care products and is converted into biofuel. It produces up to 10 times more oil per unit area than <a href="/wiki/Soyabean" class="mw-redirect" title="Soyabean">soyabeans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rapeseed" title="Rapeseed">rapeseed</a> or <a href="/wiki/Sunflower" class="mw-redirect" title="Sunflower">sunflowers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-econ2010_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-econ2010-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Oil palms produce 38% of the world's vegetable-oil output on 5% of the world’s vegetable-oil farmland.<sup id="cite_ref-econ2010_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-econ2010-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Palm oil plantations are under increasing scrutiny for their effects on the <a href="/wiki/Environment_(biophysical)" class="mw-redirect" title="Environment (biophysical)">environment</a>, including loss of carbon-sequestering forest land.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> There is also concern over displacement and disruption of human and animal populations due to palm oil cultivation.<sup id="cite_ref-Wong1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wong1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-displace_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-displace-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Statistics">Statistics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Social_and_environmental_impact_of_palm_oil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Statistics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Elaeis_guineensis_MS_3467.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Elaeis_guineensis_MS_3467.jpg/250px-Elaeis_guineensis_MS_3467.jpg" width="250" height="167" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Elaeis_guineensis_MS_3467.jpg/375px-Elaeis_guineensis_MS_3467.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Elaeis_guineensis_MS_3467.jpg/500px-Elaeis_guineensis_MS_3467.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3456" data-file-height="2304" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Elaeis_guineensis_MS_3467.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Oil_palm" class="mw-redirect" title="Oil palm">Oil palms</a> (<i>Elaeis guineensis</i>)</div></div></div>
<p>An estimated 1.5 million small farmers grow the crop in Indonesia, along with about 500,000 people directly employed in the sector in Malaysia, plus those connected with related industries.<sup id="cite_ref-mpoa_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mpoa-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-forbes_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of 2006, the cumulative land area of palm oil plantations is approximately 11,000,000 hectares (42,000&#160;sq&#160;mi).<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 2005 the Malaysian Palm Oil Association, responsible for about half of the world's crop, estimated that they manage about half a billion perennial <a href="/wiki/CO2_sequestration" class="mw-redirect" title="CO2 sequestration">carbon-sequestering</a> palm trees.<sup id="cite_ref-mpoa_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mpoa-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Demand for palm oil has been rising and is expected to climb further.
</p><p>Between 1967 and 2000 the area under cultivation in Indonesia expanded from less than 2,000 square kilometres (770&#160;sq&#160;mi) to more than 30,000 square kilometres (12,000&#160;sq&#160;mi). <a href="/wiki/Deforestation_in_Indonesia" title="Deforestation in Indonesia">Deforestation in Indonesia</a> for palm oil (and <a href="/wiki/Illegal_logging" title="Illegal logging">illegal logging</a>) is so rapid that a 2007 United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP) report said that most of the country's forest might be destroyed by 2022. The rate of forest loss has declined in the past decade.<sup id="cite_ref-econ2010_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-econ2010-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Global production is forecast at a record 46.9m tonnes in 2010, up from 45.3m in 2009, with Indonesia providing most of the increase.<sup id="cite_ref-econ2010_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-econ2010-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Social_issues">Social issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Social_and_environmental_impact_of_palm_oil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Social issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Oil palm is a valuable economic crop and provides a source of employment.<sup id="cite_ref-Budidarsono_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Budidarsono-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Norwana_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Norwana-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> It allows small landholders to participate in the cash economy<sup id="cite_ref-OBG_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OBG-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and often results in improvements to local infrastructure and greater access to services such as schools and health facilities. In some areas, the cultivation of oil palm has replaced traditional practices, often due to the higher income potential of palm oil.<sup id="cite_ref-Budidarsono_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Budidarsono-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Norwana_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Norwana-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>However, in some cases, land has been developed by oil palm plantations without consultation or compensation of the indigenous people occupying the land. This has occurred in <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea" title="Papua New Guinea">Papua New Guinea</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-displace_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-displace-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Colombia_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Colombia-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FPP_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FPP-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Sarawak" title="Sarawak">Sarawak</a> state of Malaysian <a href="/wiki/Borneo" title="Borneo">Borneo</a>, there has been debate over whether there was an appropriate level of consultation with the Long Teran Kanan community prior to the development of local land for palm oil plantations.<sup id="cite_ref-Colchester_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Colchester-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Appropriation of native lands has led to conflict between the plantations and local residents in each of these countries.<sup id="cite_ref-displace_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-displace-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Colombia_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Colombia-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FPP_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FPP-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Colchester_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Colchester-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to a 2008 report by NGOs including <a href="/wiki/Friends_of_the_Earth" title="Friends of the Earth">Friends of the Earth</a>, palm oil companies have also reportedly used force to acquire land from indigenous communities in Indonesia.<sup id="cite_ref-FPP_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FPP-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Additionally, some Indonesian oil palm plantations are dependent on imported labor or undocumented immigrants, which has raised concerns about the working conditions and social impacts of these practices.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>In Indonesia, rising demand for palm oil and timber has led to the clearing of <a href="/wiki/Tropical_forest" title="Tropical forest">tropical forest</a> land in <a href="/wiki/National_parks_of_Indonesia" class="mw-redirect" title="National parks of Indonesia">Indonesian national parks</a>. According to a 2007 report published by <a href="/wiki/UNEP" class="mw-redirect" title="UNEP">UNEP</a>, at the rate of deforestation at that time, an estimated 98 percent of Indonesian forest would be destroyed by 2022 due to legal and illegal logging, <a href="/wiki/Forest_fire" class="mw-redirect" title="Forest fire">forest fires</a> and the development of palm oil plantations.<sup id="cite_ref-UNEP_Report_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNEP_Report-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Malaysia, the second largest producer of palm oil has pledged to conserve a minimum of 50 percent of its total land area as forests. As of 2010, 58 percent of Malaysia was forested.<sup id="cite_ref-Morales_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Morales-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Palm oil cultivation has been criticized for:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">Greenhouse gas</a> emissions. Deforestation in tropical areas accounts for an estimated 10 percent of manmade <a href="/wiki/CO2" class="mw-redirect" title="CO2"><span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span></a> emissions, and is a driver toward <a href="/wiki/Avoiding_dangerous_climate_change" class="mw-redirect" title="Avoiding dangerous climate change">dangerous climate change</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Doom_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Doom-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Habitat destruction, leading to the demise of critically <a href="/wiki/Endangered_species" title="Endangered species">endangered species</a> (e.g. the <a href="/wiki/Sumatran_elephant" title="Sumatran elephant">Sumatran elephant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sumatran_tiger" title="Sumatran tiger">Sumatran tiger</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Sumatran_rhinoceros" title="Sumatran rhinoceros">Sumatran rhinoceros</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-rhinos_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rhinos-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Sumatran_orangutan" title="Sumatran orangutan">Sumatran orangutan</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-UNEP_Report_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNEP_Report-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-foe_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-foe-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_Pongo_pygmaeus_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN_Pongo_pygmaeus-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_Pongo_abelii_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN_Pongo_abelii-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cspi_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cspi-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Mail_Online_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mail_Online-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Reduced <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">biodiversity</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-hdr2008_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hdr2008-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> including damage to <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity_hotspot" title="Biodiversity hotspot">biodiversity hotspots</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-cspi_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cspi-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Cultivating crops on land that belongs to indigenous people in the <a href="/wiki/Sarawak" title="Sarawak">Sarawak</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kalimantan" title="Kalimantan">Kalimantan</a> states on the island of <a href="/wiki/Borneo" title="Borneo">Borneo</a> and the Malaysian state of <a href="/wiki/Sabah" title="Sabah">Sabah</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Mongabay2011_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mongabay2011-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Water_pollution">Water pollution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Social_and_environmental_impact_of_palm_oil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Water pollution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Water_pollution" title="Water pollution">water pollution</a></div>
<p>In some states where oil palm is established, lax enforcement of environmental legislation leads to encroachment of plantations into riparian strips,<sup id="cite_ref-NST_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NST-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> and release of pollutants such as palm oil mill effluent (POME) in the environment.<sup id="cite_ref-NST_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NST-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>  
</p><p>More environment-friendly practices have been developed.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Among those approaches is anaerobic treatment of POME, which might allow for biogas (methane) production and electricity generation, but it is very difficult to maintain optimum growth conditions for the anaerobic organisms that break down acetate to methane (primarily <i><a href="/wiki/Methanosaeta" title="Methanosaeta">Methanosaeta</a> concilii</i>, a species of <a href="/wiki/Archaea" title="Archaea">Archaea</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-ejbiotechnology.info_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ejbiotechnology.info-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Greenhouse_gas_emissions">Greenhouse gas emissions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Social_and_environmental_impact_of_palm_oil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Greenhouse gas emissions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Damage to <a href="/wiki/Peatland" class="mw-redirect" title="Peatland">peatland</a>, partly due to palm oil production, is claimed to contribute to environmental degradation, including four percent of global <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions" class="mw-redirect" title="Greenhouse gas emissions">greenhouse gas emissions</a><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> and eight percent of all global emissions caused annually by burning fossil fuels,<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> due to the clearing of large areas of rainforest for palm oil plantations. Many Indonesian and Malaysian rainforests lie atop <a href="/wiki/Peat_bogs" class="mw-redirect" title="Peat bogs">peat bogs</a> that store great quantities of <a href="/wiki/Carbon" title="Carbon">carbon</a>. Forest removal and bog drainage to make way for plantations releases this carbon.
</p><p>Researchers are looking for possible solutions and ways to help the situation and have suggested that if enough land is conserved and there remain large enough areas of primary forest reserves, the effects of the palm oil industry may not have as much of an impact on wildlife and biodiversity. Environmental groups like Greenpeace, the <a href="/wiki/Roundtable_on_Sustainable_Palm_Oil" title="Roundtable on Sustainable Palm Oil">Roundtable on Sustainable Palm Oil</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Amnesty_International" title="Amnesty International">Amnesty International</a> are also taking part in advocating bans on unsustainable palm oil crops and the companies that purchase these exports.
</p><p>Environmental groups such as <a href="/wiki/Greenpeace" title="Greenpeace">Greenpeace</a> claim that this <a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">deforestation</a> produces far more emissions than biofuels remove.<sup id="cite_ref-pachter_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pachter-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Greenpeace_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Greenpeace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Greenpeace identified Indonesian peatlands&#8212;unique tropical forests whose dense soil can be burned to release carbon emissions&#8212;which are being destroyed to make way for palm oil plantations. Greenpeace argues the peatlands represent massive carbon sinks, and they claim the destruction already accounts for four percent of annual global CO₂ emissions. However, according to the Tropical Peat Research Laboratory, at least one measurement has shown that oil palm plantations are carbon sinks because oil palms convert carbon dioxide into oxygen just as other trees do,<sup id="cite_ref-New_Straits_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-New_Straits-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> and, as reported in Malaysia's Second National Communication to the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Framework_Convention_on_Climate_Change" title="United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change">United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change</a>, oil palm plantations contribute to Malaysia's net carbon sink.<sup id="cite_ref-NC2_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NC2-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="where does it say that? A page number would be helpful (February 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Greenpeace recorded peatland destruction in the Indonesian province of Riau on the island of <a href="/wiki/Sumatra" title="Sumatra">Sumatra</a>, home to 25 percent of Indonesia's palm oil plantations. Greenpeace claims this would have devastating consequences for Riau's peatlands, which have already been degraded by industrial development and store a massive 14.6 billion tonnes of carbon, roughly one year's greenhouse gas emissions.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Environmentalists and conservationists have been called upon to team up with palm oil companies to purchase small tracts of existing palm plantation, so they can use the profits to create privately owned nature reserves.<sup id="cite_ref-Koh_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Koh-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> It has been suggested that this is a more productive strategy than the current confrontational approach that threatens the livelihoods of millions of smallholders.<sup id="cite_ref-Koh_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Koh-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_differences">National differences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Social_and_environmental_impact_of_palm_oil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: National differences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Perkebunan_kelapa_sawit_milik_rakyat_(12).JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/Perkebunan_kelapa_sawit_milik_rakyat_%2812%29.JPG/300px-Perkebunan_kelapa_sawit_milik_rakyat_%2812%29.JPG" width="300" height="204" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/Perkebunan_kelapa_sawit_milik_rakyat_%2812%29.JPG/450px-Perkebunan_kelapa_sawit_milik_rakyat_%2812%29.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/Perkebunan_kelapa_sawit_milik_rakyat_%2812%29.JPG/600px-Perkebunan_kelapa_sawit_milik_rakyat_%2812%29.JPG 2x" data-file-width="768" data-file-height="521" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Perkebunan_kelapa_sawit_milik_rakyat_(12).JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A palm oil plantation in Indonesia</div></div></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Indonesia_and_Malaysia">Indonesia and Malaysia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Social_and_environmental_impact_of_palm_oil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Indonesia and Malaysia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Palm_oil_production_in_Indonesia" title="Palm oil production in Indonesia">Palm oil production in Indonesia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Palm_oil_production_in_Malaysia" title="Palm oil production in Malaysia">Palm oil production in Malaysia</a></div>
<p>In the two countries responsible for over 80% of world oil palm production, Indonesia and Malaysia, smallholders account for 35–40% of the total area of planted oil palm and as much as 33% of the output. Elsewhere, as in West African countries that produce mainly for domestic and regional markets, smallholders produce up to 90% of the annual harvest.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a result of Malaysia's commitment to retain natural forest cover on at least 50 percent of the nation's land, the growth of new palm oil plantations has slowed in recent years. According to Malaysia's Plantation Industries and Commodities Minister Bernard Dompok, significant expansion of palm oil is no longer possible, therefore Malaysian farmers are now focusing on increasing production without expansion.<sup id="cite_ref-Morales_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Morales-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2008, the CEO of the Malaysian Palm Oil Council wrote a letter to the Wall Street Journal stating that Malaysia was aware of the need to pursue a <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainable</a> palm oil industry.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Since then the Malaysian government, along with palm oil companies, have increased production of certified sustainable palm oil (CSPO).<sup id="cite_ref-Sarif_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sarif-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Malaysia has been recognized by the <a href="/wiki/Roundtable_on_Sustainable_Palm_Oil" title="Roundtable on Sustainable Palm Oil">Roundtable on Sustainable Palm Oil</a> as the largest producer of CSPO, producing 50 percent of the world's supply,<sup id="cite_ref-McDougall_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McDougall-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> and accounting for 40% of CSPO growers worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-WWF2012_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WWF2012-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Indonesia produces 35 percent of the world's CSPO.<sup id="cite_ref-Sarif_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sarif-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In Indonesia, the Indigenous Peoples' Alliance of the Archipelago (AMAN) under the direction of <a href="/wiki/Mina_Susana_Setra" title="Mina Susana Setra">Mina Susana Setra</a> has fought for policies that find balance between economic need and indigenous people's rights. 99% of the palm oil concessions in the country concern land that is occupied by indigenous people.<sup id="cite_ref-Eshelman_(2014)_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Eshelman_(2014)-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> In 2012, AMAN led an advocacy team which won a Constitutional Court case recognizing customary land rights;<sup id="cite_ref-Nasional_(2014)_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nasional_(2014)-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> however, implementation of programs that protect indigenous rights, the environment and developers have failed to come to fruition except in limited cases.<sup id="cite_ref-Eshelman_(2014)_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Eshelman_(2014)-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Africa">Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Social_and_environmental_impact_of_palm_oil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Africa, the situation is very different compared to Indonesia or Malaysia. In its <a href="/wiki/Human_Development_Report" title="Human Development Report">Human Development Report</a> 2007-2008, the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Development_Program" class="mw-redirect" title="United Nations Development Program">United Nations Development Program</a> says production of palm oil in West Africa is largely sustainable, mainly because it is undertaken on a smallholder level without resorting to diversity-damaging <a href="/wiki/Monoculture" title="Monoculture">monoculture</a>. The United Nations Food and Agriculture program is encouraging small farmers across Africa to grow palm oil, because the crop offers opportunities to improve livelihoods and incomes for the poor.<sup id="cite_ref-hdr2008_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hdr2008-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Increasing_demand">Increasing demand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Social_and_environmental_impact_of_palm_oil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Increasing demand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Food and cosmetics companies, including <a href="/wiki/Archer_Daniels_Midland" title="Archer Daniels Midland">ADM</a>, <a href="/wiki/Unilever" title="Unilever">Unilever</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cargill" title="Cargill">Cargill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Procter_%26_Gamble" title="Procter &amp; Gamble">Procter &amp; Gamble</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nestle" class="mw-redirect" title="Nestle">Nestle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kraft_Foods" title="Kraft Foods">Kraft</a> and <a href="/wiki/Burger_King" title="Burger King">Burger King</a>, are driving the demand for new palm oil supplies,<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> demand was partly driven by a need for a replacement for high <a href="/wiki/Trans_fat" title="Trans fat">trans fat</a> content oils.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although palm oil is used in the production of <a href="/wiki/Biofuel" title="Biofuel">biofuels</a> and proposals have been made to use it in large installations,<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> a 2012 report by the <a href="/wiki/International_Food_Policy_Research_Institute" title="International Food Policy Research Institute">International Food Policy Research Institute</a> concluded that the increase in palm oil production is related to food demands, not biofuel demands.<sup id="cite_ref-Saunders_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Saunders-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Biodiesel">Biodiesel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Social_and_environmental_impact_of_palm_oil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Biodiesel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Biodiesel" title="Biodiesel">Biodiesel</a> made from palm oil grown on sustainable non-forest land and from established plantations reduces greenhouse gas emissions.<sup id="cite_ref-CSIRO_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSIRO-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> According to Greenpeace, clearing peatland to plant oil palms releases large amounts of <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gasses" class="mw-redirect" title="Greenhouse gasses">greenhouse gasses</a>, and that biodiesel produced from oil palms grown on this land may not result in a net reduction of greenhouse gas emissions.<sup id="cite_ref-Greenpeace_FAQ_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Greenpeace_FAQ-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> However, research by Malaysia's Tropical Peat Research Unit has found that oil palm plantations developed on peatland produce lower carbon dioxide emissions than forest peat swamp. However, it has been suggested that this research unit was commissioned by politicians who have interests in the palm oil industry.<sup id="cite_ref-Wong2_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wong2-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011, eight of Malaysia's <a href="/wiki/Federal_Land_Development_Authority" title="Federal Land Development Authority">Federal Land Development Authority</a> (FELDA) plantations were certified under the International Sustainability and Carbon Certification System (ISCC), becoming part of Asia's first ISCC certified supply and production chain for palm biodiesel. This certification system complies with the European Union's Renewable Energy Directive (RED).<sup id="cite_ref-Vogele_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vogele-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> In 2012, the <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a> approved the RSPO's biofuel certification scheme allowing certified sustainable palm oil biofuel to be sold in Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-RSPO_press_57-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RSPO_press-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sustainability">Sustainability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Social_and_environmental_impact_of_palm_oil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Sustainability">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Palm_forest.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Palm_forest.jpg/250px-Palm_forest.jpg" width="250" height="556" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Palm_forest.jpg/375px-Palm_forest.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Palm_forest.jpg/500px-Palm_forest.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1438" data-file-height="3196" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Palm_forest.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>In Borneo, the forest (F), is being replaced by oil palm plantations (G). These changes are irreversible for all practical purposes (H).</div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Roundtable_on_Sustainable_Palm_Oil" title="Roundtable on Sustainable Palm Oil">Roundtable on Sustainable Palm Oil</a> (RSPO), founded in 2004, works to promote the production of sustainably sourced palm oil through involvement with growers, processors, food companies, investors and NGOs.<sup id="cite_ref-econ2010_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-econ2010-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Beginning in 2008, palm oil that meets RSPO introduced standards has been designated "certified sustainable palm oil" (CSPO). Within two years of implementation, CSPO-designated palm oil comprised 7 percent of the global palm oil market.<sup id="cite_ref-Adnan_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Adnan-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> As of October 2012, 12 percent of palm oil has been certified by the RSPO.<sup id="cite_ref-Watson_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Watson-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> However, in the first year of CSPO certification only 30 percent of sustainable oil was marketed as CSPO.<sup id="cite_ref-econ2010_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-econ2010-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <i><a href="/wiki/The_Economist" title="The Economist">The Economist</a></i> in 2010, the RSPO was criticized for not setting standards for greenhouse-gas emissions for plantations and because its members account for only 40 percent of palm oil production.<sup id="cite_ref-econ2010_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-econ2010-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In a 2007 report, <a href="/wiki/Greenpeace" title="Greenpeace">Greenpeace</a> was critical of RSPO-member food companies saying that they are "dependent on suppliers that are actively engaged in deforestation and the conversion of peatlands".<sup id="cite_ref-Greenpeace2007_60-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Greenpeace2007-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following a contribution of $1 billion from Norway, in May 2010, Indonesia announced a two-year suspension on new agreements to clear natural forests and peatlands. Additionally, Indonesia announced plans to create its own organization similar to the RSPO, which, as a government certification system, will introduce mandatory regulation for all Indonesian palm oil producers.<sup id="cite_ref-econ2010_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-econ2010-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Gillespie_61-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gillespie-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011, Malaysia began developing a national certification, the "Malaysia sustainable palm oil" (MSPO) certification, to improve involvement in sustainable palm oil production nationwide.<sup id="cite_ref-Daily_Express_62-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Daily_Express-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> The certification program, aimed at small and medium-sized producers, is expected to be launched in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> Malaysia has initiated its own environmental assessment on oil palm industry based on <a href="/wiki/Life_Cycle_Assessment" class="mw-redirect" title="Life Cycle Assessment">Life Cycle Assessment</a> (LCA) approaches. LCA has been applied to assess the environmental impact of production of oil palm seedlings,<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> oil palm fresh fruit bunches,<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> crude palm oil,<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> crude palm kernel oil<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> and refined palm oil.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> The assessment on downstream industries such as oil palm plywood<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup> and bio-diesel,<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup> was also conducted.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Carbon_credit_programs">Carbon credit programs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Social_and_environmental_impact_of_palm_oil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Carbon credit programs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Oil palm producers are eligible to take part in <a href="/wiki/Clean_Development_Mechanism" title="Clean Development Mechanism">Clean Development Mechanism</a> (CDM) programs in which developed nations invest in clean energy projects in developing nations to earn carbon credits to offset their own greenhouse gas emissions and to reduce greenhouse gas emissions worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-Chiew_71-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chiew-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Investors have been cautious about investing in palm oil biofuel projects because of the impact the expansion of oil palm plantations has had on tropical rain forests,<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup> but according to the South East Asian CDM development company <a href="/wiki/YTL_Corporation#YTL-SV_Carbon" title="YTL Corporation">YTL-SV Carbon</a>, many CDM projects in the palm oil sector focus on improving use of waste products to reduce gas emissions and do not contribute to the establishment of new oil palm plantations.<sup id="cite_ref-Varming_73-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Varming-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Use_of_sustainable_oil_by_corporations">Use of sustainable oil by corporations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Social_and_environmental_impact_of_palm_oil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Use of sustainable oil by corporations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/World_Wildlife_Foundation" class="mw-redirect" title="World Wildlife Foundation">World Wildlife Foundation</a> (WWF) publishes an annual report on the use of sustainable palm oil by major corporations. In the 2011 report, 31 of the 132 companies surveyed received a top score for their use of sustainable palm oil. This represents an increase from 2009, the first year the report was issued, where no companies received top scores.<sup id="cite_ref-WWF2011_74-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WWF2011-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The WWF reports that 87 companies have committed to using only sustainable palm oil by 2015, including <a href="/wiki/Unilever" title="Unilever">Unilever</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nestl%C3%A9" title="Nestlé">Nestlé</a>, both of which committed to exclusively using sustainable palm oil following demonstrations and urgings from environmental organizations in the late 2000s.<sup id="cite_ref-econ2010_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-econ2010-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-foodnav_75-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-foodnav-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup> However, according to the WWF, the overall growth in the use of sustainable palm oil is too slow.<sup id="cite_ref-WWF2011_74-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WWF2011-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Retailers who have made commitments to offering products containing sustainable oil, including <a href="/wiki/Walmart" title="Walmart">Walmart</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carrefour" title="Carrefour">Carrefour</a>, have attributed the slow rate of growth in the availability of sustainable palm oil to a lack of consumer interest and awareness in products made with sustainable palm oil. These companies have expressed concern about the potential impact of low consumer demand on the cost and future availability of sustainable palm oil.<sup id="cite_ref-Watson2_76-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Watson2-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Lingga_77-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lingga-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Persuading_governments">Persuading governments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Social_and_environmental_impact_of_palm_oil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Persuading governments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It may be possible to persuade governments of nations that produce competing products to enact <a href="/wiki/Protectionism" title="Protectionism">protectionist</a> legislation against the products of deforestation, an approach that was presented in a report by the <a href="/wiki/National_Farmers_Union_(United_States)" title="National Farmers Union (United States)">National Farmers Union (United States)</a> and <a href="/wiki/Avoided_Deforestation_Partners" title="Avoided Deforestation Partners">Avoided Deforestation Partners</a>. The 2010 report estimates that protecting the 13,000,000 hectares (50,000&#160;sq&#160;mi) of mostly tropical forest that are lost annually worldwide would boost American agricultural revenue by $190–270 billion between 2012 and 2030. However, several conservation groups, including <a href="/wiki/Conservation_International" title="Conservation International">Conservation International</a>, <a href="/wiki/Environmental_Defense_Fund" title="Environmental Defense Fund">Environmental Defense Fund</a>, <a href="/wiki/National_Wildlife_Federation" title="National Wildlife Federation">National Wildlife Federation</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Nature_Conservancy" title="The Nature Conservancy">The Nature Conservancy</a>, presented a rebuttal to the report, stating that it was "based on the assumption, totally unfounded, that deforestation in tropical countries can be easily interrupted, and its conclusions are therefore also unrealistic."<sup id="cite_ref-econ2010_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-econ2010-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gardner2010_78-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gardner2010-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
