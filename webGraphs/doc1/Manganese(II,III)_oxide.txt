<p><b>Manganese(II,III) oxide</b> is the chemical compound with formula Mn<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>. <a href="/wiki/Manganese" title="Manganese">Manganese</a> is present in two <a href="/wiki/Oxidation_state" title="Oxidation state">oxidation states</a> +2 and +3 and the formula is sometimes written as MnO.Mn<sub>2</sub>O<sub>3</sub>. Mn<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> is found in nature as the mineral <a href="/wiki/Hausmannite" title="Hausmannite">hausmannite</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparation">Preparation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manganese(II,III)_oxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Preparation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mn<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> formed when any manganese oxide is heated in air above 1000&#160;°C.<sup id="cite_ref-Greenwood_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Greenwood-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Considerable research has centred on producing <a href="/wiki/Nanocrystal" title="Nanocrystal">nanocrystalline</a> Mn<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> and various syntheses that involve oxidation of Mn<sup>II</sup> or reduction of Mn<sup>VI</sup>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reactions">Reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manganese(II,III)_oxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mn<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> has been found to act as a catalyst for a range of reactions e.g. the oxidation of methane and carbon monoxide;<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> the decomposition of NO,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> the reduction of nitrobenzene<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Catalytic_combustion" title="Catalytic combustion">catalytic combustion</a> of organic compounds.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manganese(II,III)_oxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mn<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> has the <a href="/wiki/Spinel" title="Spinel">spinel</a> structure, where the oxide ions are cubic close packed and the Mn<sup>II</sup> occupy tetrahedral sites and the Mn<sup>III</sup> octahedral sites.<sup id="cite_ref-Greenwood_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Greenwood-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The structure is distorted due to the <a href="/wiki/Jahn%E2%80%93Teller_effect" title="Jahn–Teller effect">Jahn–Teller effect</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Greenwood_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Greenwood-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> At room temperature Mn<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> is <a href="/wiki/Paramagnetic" class="mw-redirect" title="Paramagnetic">paramagnetic</a>, below 41-43 K, it is <a href="/wiki/Ferrimagnetic" class="mw-redirect" title="Ferrimagnetic">ferrimagnetic</a><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> although this has been reported as reducing in nanocrystalline samples to around 39 K.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>it is paramegnetic in nature
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Uses">Uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manganese(II,III)_oxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mn<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> is sometimes used as a starting material in the production of soft <a href="/wiki/Ferrite_(magnet)" title="Ferrite (magnet)">ferrites</a> e.g. manganese zinc  ferrite,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and lithium manganese oxide, used in lithium batteries.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Manganese tetraoxide can also be used as a weighting agent while drilling reservoir sections in oil and gas wells.
</p>
