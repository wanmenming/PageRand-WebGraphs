<p><b>Gallium(I) oxide</b> or <b>gallium suboxide</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Inorganic_compound" title="Inorganic compound">inorganic compound</a> with the formula <a href="/wiki/Gallium" title="Gallium">Ga<sub>2</sub></a><a href="/wiki/Oxide" title="Oxide">O</a>.  
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Properties">Properties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gallium(I)_oxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Properties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gallium(I) oxide is a brown-black <a href="/wiki/Diamagnetic" class="mw-redirect" title="Diamagnetic">diamagnetic</a> solid which is resistant to further oxidation in dry air. It starts decomposing upon heating at temperatures above 500 °C, and the decomposition rate depends on the atmosphere (vacuum, inter gas, air).<sup id="cite_ref-brauer_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brauer-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
