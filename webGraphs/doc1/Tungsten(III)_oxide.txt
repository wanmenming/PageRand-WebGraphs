<p><b>Tungsten(III) oxide</b> (W<sub>2</sub>O<sub>3</sub>) is a compound of <a href="/wiki/Tungsten" title="Tungsten">tungsten</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygen</a>. It has  been reported (2006) as being  grown as a thin film by atomic layer deposition  at temperatures between 140 and 240&#160;°C using W<sub>2</sub>(N(CH<sub>3</sub>)<sub>2</sub>)<sub>6</sub> as a precursor.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is not referred to in major textbooks.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
Some older literature refers to  the compound W<sub>2</sub>O<sub>3</sub> but as the atomic weight of tungsten was believed at the time to be 92, i.e., approximately half the modern accepted value of 183.84, the compound actually being referred to was <a href="/wiki/Tungsten(VI)_oxide" class="mw-redirect" title="Tungsten(VI) oxide">WO<sub>3</sub></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage">Usage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tungsten(III)_oxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Usage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tungsten(III) oxide is used in various types of infrared absorbing coatings and foils.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
