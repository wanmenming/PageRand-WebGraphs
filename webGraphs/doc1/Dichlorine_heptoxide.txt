<p><b>Dichlorine heptaoxide</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Chemical_compound" title="Chemical compound">chemical compound</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Chemical_formula" title="Chemical formula">formula</a> Cl<sub>2</sub>O<sub>7</sub>. This <a href="/wiki/Chlorine_oxide" title="Chlorine oxide">chlorine oxide</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Acid_anhydride" title="Acid anhydride">anhydride</a> of <a href="/wiki/Perchloric_acid" title="Perchloric acid">perchloric acid</a>. It is produced by the careful distillation of perchloric acid in the presence of the dehydrating agent <a href="/wiki/Phosphorus_pentoxide" title="Phosphorus pentoxide">phosphorus pentoxide</a>:<sup id="cite_ref-Holl_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Holl-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<dl><dd>2 HClO<sub>4</sub> + P<sub>4</sub>O<sub>10</sub> → Cl<sub>2</sub>O<sub>7</sub> + H<sub>2</sub>P<sub>4</sub>O<sub>11</sub></dd></dl>
<p>The chlorine(VII) oxide can be distilled off from the mixture.
</p><p>It may also be formed by illumination on mixtures of chlorine and <a href="/wiki/Ozone" title="Ozone">ozone</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It slowly <a href="/wiki/Hydrolyzes" class="mw-redirect" title="Hydrolyzes">hydrolyzes</a> back to <a href="/wiki/Perchloric_acid" title="Perchloric acid">perchloric acid</a>, which is also hazardous when <a href="/wiki/Anhydrous" title="Anhydrous">anhydrous</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dichlorine_heptoxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cl<sub>2</sub>O<sub>7</sub> is an endergonic molecule, meaning it is intrinsically unstable, decomposing according to.
</p>
<dl><dd>2 Cl<sub>2</sub>O<sub>7</sub> → 2 Cl<sub>2</sub> + 7 O<sub>2</sub> (ΔH = 135 kJ/mol)</dd></dl>
<p>Cl<sub>2</sub>O<sub>7</sub> is bent with Cl&#8722;O&#8722;Cl angle of 118.6° giving the molecule C<sub>2</sub> <a href="/wiki/Symmetry_group" title="Symmetry group">symmetry</a>. The terminal Cl&#8722;O distances are 1.709&#160;Å and the Cl=O distances are 1.405&#160;Å.<sup id="cite_ref-Holl_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Holl-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In this compound, chlorine exists in its highest formal <a href="/wiki/Oxidation_state" title="Oxidation state">oxidation state</a> of +7, although the bonding in this molecule is significantly covalent.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chemistry">Chemistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dichlorine_heptoxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Chemistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dichlorine heptaoxide reacts with primary and secondary <a href="/wiki/Amine" title="Amine">amines</a> in <a href="/wiki/Carbon_tetrachloride" title="Carbon tetrachloride">carbon tetrachloride</a> solution to yield perchloric amides:<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<dl><dd>2 <span class="chemf nowrap">RNH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> + <span class="chemf nowrap">Cl<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">7</sub></span></span> → 2 <span class="chemf nowrap">RNHClO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span> + <span class="chemf nowrap">H<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>O</span></dd>
<dd>2 <span class="chemf nowrap">R<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>NH</span> + <span class="chemf nowrap">Cl<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">7</sub></span></span> → 2 <span class="chemf nowrap">R<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>NClO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span> + <span class="chemf nowrap">H<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br /><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>O</span></dd></dl>
<p>It also reacts with <a href="/wiki/Alkene" title="Alkene">alkenes</a> to give alkyl perchlorates. For example, it reacts with <a href="/wiki/Propene" title="Propene">propene</a> in carbon tetrachloride solution to yield isopropyl perchlorate and 1-chloro-2-propyl perchlorate.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Dichlorine heptoxide is a strongly acidic oxide, and in solution it forms an equilibrium with perchloric acid.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Safety">Safety</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dichlorine_heptoxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Safety">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although it is the most stable chlorine oxide, Cl<sub>2</sub>O<sub>7</sub> is a strong oxidizer as well as an explosive that can be set off with flame or mechanical shock, or by contact with <a href="/wiki/Iodine" title="Iodine">iodine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Nevertheless, it is less strongly oxidising than the other chlorine oxides, and does not attack <a href="/wiki/Sulfur" title="Sulfur">sulfur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phosphorus" title="Phosphorus">phosphorus</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Paper" title="Paper">paper</a> when cold.<sup id="cite_ref-Holl_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Holl-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It has the same effects on the human body as elemental <a href="/wiki/Chlorine" title="Chlorine">chlorine</a>, and requires the same precautions.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
