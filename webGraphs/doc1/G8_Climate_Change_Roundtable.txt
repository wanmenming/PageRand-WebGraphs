<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">G8 Climate Change Roundtable</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>
				<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>G8 Climate Change Roundtable</b> was formed in January 2005 at the <a href="/wiki/World_Economic_Forum" title="World Economic Forum">World Economic Forum</a> in <a href="/wiki/Davos" title="Davos">Davos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  Its purpose was to address the global climate change issue facing governments, business and civil society. The first meeting was held in <a href="/wiki/Gleneagles,_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="Gleneagles, Scotland">Gleneagles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, from 6&#8211;8 July 2005, to coincide with the <a href="/wiki/31st_G8_summit" title="31st G8 summit">31st G8 summit</a>.
</p><p>The roundtable was established by twenty-three leading <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">international businesses</a> with the assistance of <a href="/wiki/G8" class="mw-redirect" title="G8">G8</a> President and <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Prime Minister of the United Kingdom">Prime Minister of the United Kingdom</a> <a href="/wiki/Tony_Blair" title="Tony Blair">Tony Blair</a>, to co-operatively engage in a global plan of action.<sup id="cite_ref-bhg8_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bhg8-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The aim of the group was to ensure that a long-term <a href="/wiki/Policy_framework" title="Policy framework">policy framework</a> was set up to enable consistent and transparent market-based solutions in <a href="/wiki/Mitigation_of_global_warming" class="mw-redirect" title="Mitigation of global warming">mitigating climate change</a>, while also addressing issues linked to <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a>; such as <a href="/wiki/Economic_growth" title="Economic growth">economic growth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poverty</a>, and adequate <a href="/wiki/Energy_supply" title="Energy supply">energy supplies</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-economic_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-economic-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  <a href="/wiki/Carbon_trading" class="mw-redirect" title="Carbon trading">Carbon trading</a> is one of the most popular pricing mechanisms used to the reduce <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_emissions" class="mw-redirect" title="Greenhouse emissions">greenhouse emissions</a> worldwide.
</p><p>The group devised a Framework for Action which called for technology incentive programs, the establishment of common metrics,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> for example in <a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">energy efficiency</a>, and the expansion of <a href="/wiki/Emissions_trading" title="Emissions trading">emissions trading</a> schemes.
</p><p>Some of the companies participating include <a href="/wiki/Ford" class="mw-redirect" title="Ford">Ford</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Airways" title="British Airways">British Airways</a>, <a href="/wiki/HSBC" title="HSBC">HSBC</a>, <a href="/wiki/EdF" class="mw-redirect" title="EdF">EdF</a> and <a href="/wiki/BP" title="BP">BP</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pa_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pa-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Now, the Climate Change Roundtable has a membership of 150 businesses spread across the globe.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Environmentalists raised concerns that the body's statement does not call for targets or include timetables.<sup id="cite_ref-pa_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pa-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Friends_of_the_Earth" title="Friends of the Earth">Friends of the Earth</a> noted that the roundtable represented a major shift by the <a href="/wiki/Business_action_on_climate_change" title="Business action on climate change">business community towards</a> efforts to <a href="/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation" title="Climate change mitigation">mitigate climate change</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pa_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pa-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Key_Principles">Key Principles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=G8_Climate_Change_Roundtable&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Key Principles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The G8 Climate Change Roundtable stated 5 key points to be addressed:
</p>
<ul><li>Companies must recognize their responsibility in addressing <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a>.</li>
<li>The issue of climate change requires further international attention and support.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Market_forces" class="mw-redirect" title="Market forces">Market forces</a> must be considered in the crafting of a solution, consumer support is needed to solve the problem.</li>
<li>Specific policies for the <a href="/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation" title="Climate change mitigation">mitigation</a> of climate change.</li>
<li>Specific policies which should be adopted by <a href="/wiki/G8" class="mw-redirect" title="G8">G8</a> members.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Key_Principles_for_Business">Key Principles for Business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=G8_Climate_Change_Roundtable&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Key Principles for Business">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The G8 Climate Change Roundtable developed 5 key principles for businesses to consider when crafting a climate change mitigation strategy. These principles are:
</p>
<ul><li>Strategies should be based on <a href="/wiki/Scientific" class="mw-redirect" title="Scientific">scientific</a> and <a href="/wiki/Economic" class="mw-redirect" title="Economic">economic</a> facts.</li>
<li>Businesses should adopt market based policy frameworks which are <a href="/wiki/Transparency_(behavior)" title="Transparency (behavior)">transparent</a> and offer consistent <a href="/wiki/Price_signals" class="mw-redirect" title="Price signals">price signals</a> in the long-term.</li>
<li>Solutions should be adopted globally in order to achieve long term success.</li>
<li>Climate change solutions must not be viewed in isolation from other global challenges, such as ensuring access to <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a>, expanding availability of <a href="/wiki/Clean_water" class="mw-redirect" title="Clean water">clean water</a>, alleviating <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poverty</a>, and achieving <a href="/wiki/Economic_growth" title="Economic growth">economic growth</a> in <a href="/wiki/Emerging_markets" class="mw-redirect" title="Emerging markets">emerging markets</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Siemens_Power_Generation_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Siemens_Power_Generation-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Businesses should seek a <a href="/wiki/System" title="System">system</a> wide solution, identifying opportunities for <a href="/wiki/CO2" class="mw-redirect" title="CO2">CO2</a> mitigation throughout the <a href="/wiki/Supply_chain" title="Supply chain">supply chain</a>.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Key_Principles_for_Governments">Key Principles for Governments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=G8_Climate_Change_Roundtable&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Key Principles for Governments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The G8 Climate Change Roundtable developed 3 key principles for governments to consider when addressing climate change. Governments should:
</p>
<ul><li>Focus their <a href="/wiki/Resources" class="mw-redirect" title="Resources">resources</a> on measuring the <a href="/wiki/Effects_of_climate_change_on_human_health" class="mw-redirect" title="Effects of climate change on human health">effects of climate change on human</a>, <a href="/wiki/Economic" class="mw-redirect" title="Economic">economic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Environmental_health" title="Environmental health">environmental health</a>.</li>
<li>Increase their understanding of current and future changes taking place within the <a href="/wiki/Global_environment" class="mw-redirect" title="Global environment">global environment</a>. (e.g. sea level rise, sea/ice cover).<sup id="cite_ref-economic_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-economic-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Quickly and effectively adopt climate stabilization policies with the goal of mitigating future environmental harm.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Policy_Framework">Policy Framework</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=G8_Climate_Change_Roundtable&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Policy Framework">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The G8 Climate Change Roundtable aimed to create a long-term <a href="/wiki/Policy_framework" title="Policy framework">policy framework</a> which was both <a href="/wiki/Transparency_(behavior)" title="Transparency (behavior)">transparent</a> and <a href="/wiki/Consistent" class="mw-redirect" title="Consistent">consistent</a>. Through market based solutions such as <a href="/wiki/Carbon_trading" class="mw-redirect" title="Carbon trading">carbon trading</a>, the Roundtable established a <a href="/wiki/Policy_framework" title="Policy framework">policy framework</a> which now encompasses 150 businesses across the globe.
</p><p>By creating long term value, these members established a market based framework extending to 2030 and indicative signals extending to 2050.<sup id="cite_ref-economic_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-economic-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> This policy framework they hoped would take on a global scale, with consistent <a href="/wiki/Policies" class="mw-redirect" title="Policies">policies</a> throughout the various states. The framework was meant to not only mitigate climate change but also promote <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a> by addressing issues of <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poverty</a>, <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Economic_growth" title="Economic growth">economic growth</a> in <a href="/wiki/Emerging_markets" class="mw-redirect" title="Emerging markets">emerging markets</a>.
</p><p>The Roundtable recommended encouraging <a href="/wiki/Technological_innovation" title="Technological innovation">technological innovation</a> through performance based incentives.<sup id="cite_ref-economic_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-economic-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> By quickly commercializing <a href="/wiki/Low-carbon_emission" title="Low-carbon emission">low carbon technologies</a> they aimed to mitigate climate change quickly. They noted that by ensuring that climate change mitigation goals aligned with societal goals, governments would be able to optimize <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gas</a> reductions.
By ensuring that emerging markets, such as <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_African" class="mw-redirect" title="South African">South African</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, invest in low carbon economic growth, new technologies can be applied globally while streamlining emerging markets. New investments should align with societal goals, creating partnerships between G8 nations as well as emerging nations. Through such a partnership, members can effectively collaborate on specific projects within countries or rapid dissemination of specific technologies.
</p><p>Members of the new policy framework should establish common metrics. By streamlining greenhouse emissions reporting processes and systems, countries can achieve this goal.
Business and G8 governments should use supply chain drivers and the power of <a href="/wiki/Procurement" title="Procurement">procurement</a> to integrate climate change solutions into their global supply chain requirements.<sup id="cite_ref-economic_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-economic-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> By committing to the use of the Roundtable’s policy framework throughout the supply chain, optimal greenhouse gas mitigation can be achieved.
</p>
